— Poverty is not a sin, my friend, well, what can you do! It is known, gunpowder, couldn't bear an insult. You, perhaps, took offense against him and couldn't restrain yourself—continued Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov— but you're wasting your time: he is, I'll tell you, the most noble of all, but gunpowder, gunpowder! He flared up, boiled over, burned out—and there he was! And everything passed! And in the end, only a heart of gold! And in the regiment they called him: "Ensign Gunpowder"...

— What a splendid regiment! — exclaimed Ilyá Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so nicely teased, but still buzzing with irritation. Raskolnikov suddenly felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — Please, captain, — he began, very casually addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly — take a closer look at my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I've done anything wrong on my part.
