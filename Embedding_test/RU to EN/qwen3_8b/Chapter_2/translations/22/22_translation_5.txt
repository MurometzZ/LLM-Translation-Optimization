— Louise Ivanovna, please sit, — said he to the woman dressed in bright red, hastily, who stood still as if not daring to sit herself, although a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said, and quietly, with a silk-like sound, sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim, like a balloon, spread around the chair and occupied almost half the room. It carried the scent. But the lady, obviously, was shy about taking up almost half the room and the scent from her, although she smiled nervously and boldly, but with evident anxiety. The mourning lady finally finished and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, very manly and somehow particularly twisting her shoulders with every step, entered the officer, threw his hat with a cockade on the table, and sat in the chair. The puffy lady jumped up from her place upon seeing him, and with some particular enthusiasm began to sit down; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared to sit in his presence.
