— Don't shout! — I am not shouting, but speaking very calmly, and it is you who are shouting at me; I am a student, and I will not allow myself to be shouted at. The clerk had flown into a rage, so in the first moment he could not even utter a word, and only some spittle flew from his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. — Please be quiet! You are in the presence. Don't be a rude person, sir! — And you are also in the presence, — screamed Raskolnikov, — and besides, you are shouting and smoking a cigarette, so you are being rude to all of us. — Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
