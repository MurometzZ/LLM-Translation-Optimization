— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, he said to the hastily dressed red and pink lady who stood there as if she was afraid to sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said and quietly, with a soft rustling sound, sat down on the chair. Her light blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like an air balloon and occupied almost half of the room. It was carried by perfumes. But the lady, it was clear, was nervous about taking up half of the room and that she was carrying such strong perfumes, although she smiled timidly and shamelessly at the same time, but with obvious anxiety. The mourning lady finally finished and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, very bravely and somehow especially turning her shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table and sat in the armchair. The lavish lady jumped out of her seat at the sight of him and took on a special excitement, began to squat; but the officer didn't pay any attention to her, and she was no longer able to sit down next to him.
