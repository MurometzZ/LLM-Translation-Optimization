His head was spinning and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me with a trick and then knock me down," he continued to himself as he stepped onto the stairs. "The worst thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the stairs, he remembered that he always left all his belongings in the wall hole—"and here, perhaps deliberately, there was a search without him," he thought, and stopped. But such despair and such, if one could say so, cynicism about his death suddenly took hold of him, and he waved his hand and went on. "Just hurry up..."  

Again, unbearable heat stood on the street; not a drop of rain for all these days. Again, dust, bricks, and plaster, again the stench from the benches and taverns, again the frequent drunken beggars and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun gleamed brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely—usual sensation of a feverish person stepping suddenly out into bright sunlight. Reaching the corner of the street from yesterday, he looked with a torturous anxiety into it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, I might say," he thought, approaching the office.
