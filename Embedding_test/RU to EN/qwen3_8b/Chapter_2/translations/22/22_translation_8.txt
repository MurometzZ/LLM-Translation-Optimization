— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, — he said briefly to the lady, dressed in a blood-red gown, who stood still as if unable to sit herself, although a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said, and quietly, with a silk-like sound, sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like a balloon, taking up almost half the room. It carried a scent. But the lady, obviously feeling shy about occupying almost half the room and the scent emanating from her, though she smiled timidly and boldly at the same time, but with evident anxiety. The widow finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very confidently and somehow particularly turning his shoulders with each step, entered the officer, tossed his hat with a cockade on the table, and sat in the chair. The plump lady jumped up from her seat upon seeing him and began to sit down with some special enthusiasm; but the officer paid her no attention at all, and she no longer dared to sit in his presence.
