And suddenly, to his greatest astonishment, he caught a glimpse of his landlady's voice. She was wailing, screaming and lamenting, rushing, hurrying, uttering words so indistinct that they couldn't be made out, begging for something - of course, for them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the beater had become so terrifying from malice and fury that it could hardly be heard anymore, but the beater himself was still saying something, too, quickly and indistinctly, in a hurry and choking. Suddenly Raskolnikov shuddered like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice! And Ilya Petrovich is here beating his landlady! He's kicking her with his feet, pounding her head against the step - that's clear from the sounds, the screams and the blows! What's going on? Has the light turned upside down? It could be heard how a crowd was gathering in all the apartments, along the entire staircase; voices, exclamations, footsteps were rising, knocking, banging on doors, rushing out. "But why, why is this possible?" he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he had gone completely mad. But no, he's hearing it all too clearly!
