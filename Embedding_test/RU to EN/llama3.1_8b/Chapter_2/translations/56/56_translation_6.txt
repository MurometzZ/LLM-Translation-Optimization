His face expressed surprise. "What's this?" he shouted, examining his friend from head to foot; then fell silent and whistled. "Is it really that bad?" he added, looking at Raskolnikov's tattered clothes. "You've outdone our brother, I must say," he said, glancing at him. "Sit down, you must be exhausted!" And when Raskolnikov collapsed onto the worn Turkish divan, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly realized that his guest was ill. "Are you seriously ill?" he asked, knowing it to be true. He began to feel Raskolnikov's pulse; Raskolnikov pulled his hand away. "No need," he said, "I came... well, I wanted to... but I don't need any lessons anyway..." "Do you know what?"
