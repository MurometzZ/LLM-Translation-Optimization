Meanwhile, Razumikhin sat down on the sofa next to him awkwardly, like a bear, grabbed his head with his left hand, despite the fact that he could have lifted himself up, and brought a spoon of soup to his mouth with his right hand, blowing on it several times beforehand to prevent him from burning. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov greedily swallowed one spoon, then another, the third. But after bringing several spoons, Razumikhin suddenly stopped and announced that they needed to consult with Zosimov about the further course of action. Nastasya entered, carrying two bottles of beer. — Do you want some tea? — I want. — Hurry up and make tea, Nastasya, since, I suppose, we can manage without the faculty for tea.
