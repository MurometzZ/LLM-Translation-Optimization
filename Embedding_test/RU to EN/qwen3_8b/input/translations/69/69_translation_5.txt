But no, he hears it too clearly!... But then, if that's the case, they will come to him now too, because... surely, all of this is from the same... from yesterday's... Lord! He wanted to lock himself in, but his hand wouldn't raise... and it was useless! Fear, like ice, had clamped his soul, tormented him, paralyzed him... But at last, after this commotion, which had lasted for sure ten minutes, began to gradually subside. The mistress was groaning and crying, Ilia Petrovich was still threatening and shouting... But at last, it seemed, he had also fallen silent; now he was no longer heard; "Has he gone? Lord!" Yes, the mistress is leaving now, still groaning and weeping... and now her door has closed... And now the crowd is dispersing down the stairs to the apartments, shouting, arguing, calling out, raising their voices to a shout, then lowering them to a whisper. There must have been many of them; perhaps the whole building had gathered. "But God, is all this really possible! And why, why did he come here!" 

Raskolnikov fell helplessly onto the sofa, but could no longer close his eyes; he lay for half an hour in such suffering, in such an unbearable feeling of boundless terror, which he had never experienced before. Suddenly, a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a plate of soup. Looking at him attentively and noticing that he was not asleep, she placed the candle on the table and began to arrange the things she had brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
