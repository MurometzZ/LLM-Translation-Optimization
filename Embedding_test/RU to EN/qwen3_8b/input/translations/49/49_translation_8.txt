He became extremely scattered and forgetful, and he knew it. He had to act decisively! He went to the Neva along the B Prospect; but on the way, a sudden thought came to him: "Why to the Neva? Why into the water? Isn't it better to go somewhere very far, once again to the Islands, and there, somewhere in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush—bury everything and the tree, perhaps notice it?" Although he felt he was unable to clearly and soundly consider everything at that moment, the thought seemed infallible to him. But even reaching the Islands was not destined for him, and something else happened: exiting from the B Prospect onto the square, he suddenly saw a gate to the courtyard, completely enclosed by soundproof walls. To the right, immediately after entering the gates, a soundproof, unwhitewashed wall of the neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. To the left, parallel to the soundproof wall and also right next to the gates, ran a wooden fence twenty steps deep into the courtyard, and then made a turn to the left. This was a secluded, walled-off place where some materials lay. Further, in the depth of the courtyard, a corner of a low, soot-stained, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
