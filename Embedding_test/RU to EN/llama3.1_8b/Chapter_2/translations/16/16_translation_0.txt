His head was spinning and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with cunning and then knock me out completely," he continued to himself as he stepped onto the staircase. "It's terrible that I'm almost delirious... I can tell some silly lie..." On the stairs, he remembered leaving all his belongings scattered about - "and here, perhaps, they're deliberately searching for them without me," he thought and stopped. But such despair and cynicism overcame him suddenly that he waved his hand in dismissal and continued on. "Just as quickly as possible..."

On the street, the heat was unbearable again; just a single drop of rain would have been welcome these past few days. Again there was dust, brick, and plaster, again the stench from the shops and bars, again drunkards, Chuvash peddlers, and half-ruined cab drivers. The sun flashed brightly in his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely around - a common feeling of someone with a fever suddenly stepping out into bright sunlight. Having reached the turn onto yesterday's street, he looked anxiously down it, at that house... and immediately turned away his eyes. "If they ask, I might just say so," he thought as he approached the office.
