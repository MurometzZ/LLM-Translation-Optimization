— Let me, let me, I completely agree with you, but let me explain too, — Raskolnikov picked up again, addressing not the clerk, but still Nikodim Fomich, but trying with all his might to address Ilya Petrovich as well, although he stubbornly pretended to be rummaging through papers and contemptuously ignored him, — let me explain on my part that I have been living with her for about three years, ever since I arrived from the province and before... before... however, why shouldn't I admit it in turn, from the very beginning I promised that I would marry her daughter, a verbal promise, completely free... She was a girl... however, she even liked me... although I wasn't in love... in a word, youth, that is, I mean to say that my landlady gave me a lot of credit then and I led a somewhat such life... I was very frivolous...
— You are not required to disclose such intimacies at all, sir, and there's no time for it, — Ilya Petrovich interrupted rudely and triumphantly, but Raskolnikov stopped him with passion, although he suddenly found it extremely difficult to speak. — But let me, let me tell you a little... how things were and... in turn... although it's superfluous, I agree with you, to tell... a year ago this girl died of typhus, but I remained a tenant as I was, and the landlady, when she moved to her current apartment, told me... and said friendly... that she completely trusted me and all... but wouldn't I like to give her this loan letter for one hundred and fifteen rubles, everything she considered my debt. Please: she specifically said that as soon as I gave this paper, she would lend me money again as much as she liked and that never, never, in turn, — these were her own words, — she wouldn't use this paper until I paid up myself... And now, when I lost my lessons and have nothing to eat, she sues for recovery... What will I say now? — All these sensitive details, sir, do not concern us, — Ilya Petrovich rudely cut in, — you must give feedback and a commitment, and what you happened to be in love with and all these tragic parts, we don't care about that at all.
