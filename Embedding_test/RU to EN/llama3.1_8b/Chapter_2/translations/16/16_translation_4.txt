His head was spinning and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with cunning and then knock me out completely," he continued to himself as he stepped onto the staircase. "It's terrible that I'm almost delirious... I can tell some stupid lie..." On the stairs, he remembered leaving all his belongings scattered around, "and here they'll probably search for them on purpose without me," he thought and stopped. But such desperation and cynicism about death suddenly took hold of him that he waved his hand in dismissal and continued further. "Just get it over with quickly..."

On the street, the heat was unbearable again; not a single drop of rain had fallen in these days. Again there was dust, bricks, and plaster, again the stench from the shops and bars, again drunkards, Chuvash peddlers, and half-ruined cab drivers. The sun blazed brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely around - a common feeling of someone with a fever suddenly stepping out into a bright sunny day. Having reached the turn onto yesterday's street, he looked at it with tormenting anxiety, at that house... and immediately turned away his eyes. "If they ask, I might say so," he thought as he approached the office.
