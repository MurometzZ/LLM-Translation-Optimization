Ilya Petrovich! — the clerk began to say carefully, but stopped to wait for a moment, because the boiling-over lieutenant could not be restrained otherwise than by holding him by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the lush lady, at first she was so startled by the thunder and lightning; but it's a strange thing: the more numerous and fierce her curses became, the more charming her appearance became, the more enchanting her smile turned out to be, directed at the stern lieutenant. She was hopping about in place and constantly squatting, impatiently waiting for them finally to allow her to speak up, and she waited. — There won't be any noise or a fight here with me, Mr. Captain, she suddenly babbled on like a burst of peas, with a strong German accent, although speaking quickly in Russian, — and no scandal at all, none whatsoever; but they came drunk, and I'll tell you everything, Mr. Captain, and I'm not to blame... I have a noble house, Mr. Captain, and noble manners, Mr. Captain, and I always, always didn't want any scandal. But they came completely drunk and then asked three times for the bill, and then one of them picked up his legs and started playing the piano with his feet, which is not nice at all in a noble house, and he's breaking the piano, and there's absolutely no manners here, and I said so. And he took the pipe and started pushing everyone behind him with it. And then as soon as I quickly called the janitor to come and Karl came too, he grabbed Karl and poked his eye, and Henriette got her eye poked too, but they hit me five times on the cheek. And that's not nice at all in a noble house, Mr. Captain, and I shouted. And he opened the window onto the canal and started sticking out of it like a little pig, squealing; and that's shameful.
