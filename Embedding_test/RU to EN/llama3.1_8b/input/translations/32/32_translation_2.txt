Yes, Captain, and what a dishonorable guest is the captain when in a noble house...

— Ah-ah-ah! Enough! I've already told you, told you, I've been telling you...

— Mr. Illya Petrovich! — The clerk spoke again with significance. The lieutenant quickly glanced at him; the clerk slightly nodded his head.

...So here it is, esteemed Lavisia Ivanovna, my last word, and this will be the last time, — continued the lieutenant. — If there's even one more scandal in your noble house, I'll make you a cudgeon, as they say in high style. Did you hear? So a writer, a composer, took five rubles for a fiddle in a "noble house"? Look at them, writers!
