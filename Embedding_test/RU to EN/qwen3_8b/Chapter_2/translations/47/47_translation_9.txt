Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely ajar. He walked quickly and firmly, and though he felt entirely broken, his mind was with him. He feared the pursuit, feared that within half an hour, within a quarter of an hour, someone would already come out, perhaps with orders to keep an eye on him; therefore, no matter what, he had to get rid of everything before the time came. He had to take care of it while there was still some strength left and some reasoning left… Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the ditch, and the ends into the water, and that's that." He had decided this during the night, in a delirium, in those moments when, as he remembered, he several times tried to get up and go: "As soon as possible, as soon, and throw everything away." But throwing it away proved to be very difficult. He had been wandering along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, maybe even more, and several times looked at the wharves in the ditch where they had met. But it was impossible to carry out the plan: either the rafts were tied up at the wharves and the laundresses were washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and people were everywhere, and from all sides, from the embankments, from all around, one could see, notice: suspiciously, that a person deliberately went off, stopped, and threw something into the water. What if the cases don't sink but float?
