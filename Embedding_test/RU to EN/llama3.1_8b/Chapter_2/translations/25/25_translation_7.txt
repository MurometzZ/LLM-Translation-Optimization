— Don't shout! I'm not shouting, I'm speaking very calmly, and it's you who are shouting at me; and I am a student and won't allow anyone to shout at me. The clerk was so flustered that in the first minute he couldn't even get out a word, and only some sort of spluttering came from his lips. He jumped up from his seat. — Go ahead and m-m-m-make a noise! You're in my presence. Don't g-g-gabble, sir! — Yes, and you too are in my presence, — shouted Raskolnikov, — and besides, what's more, you're shouting, smoking a cigar, so we're all equally guilty. — Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable sense of pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
