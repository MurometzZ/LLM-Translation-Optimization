And suddenly, to his greatest amazement, he caught a glimpse of the voice of his landlady. She was wailing, screaming and crying out in a rush, uttering words so indistinct that they could hardly be made out, begging for something - of course, for them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the beater had become so terrifying from malice and fury that it was already nothing but a croak, but still the beater too was saying something, and just as quickly, incoherently, rushing and choking. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was the voice of Ilya Petrovich. Ilya Petrovich is here and beating his landlady! He's kicking her with his feet, pounding her head against the step - that's clear, it can be heard from the sounds, screams, blows! What's going on, has the light turned upside down? It was audible how a crowd gathered in all the apartments, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations were heard, people were rushing up and down, knocking, banging doors, running out. "But why, why is this possible?" he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he had gone mad. But no, he's hearing it too clearly...
