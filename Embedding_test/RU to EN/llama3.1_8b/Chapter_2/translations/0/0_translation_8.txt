He lay there for a very long time. Sometimes he seemed to wake up and in those moments noticed that it was already late at night, but the thought of getting up didn't come to him. Finally, he realized that it was already daylight. He was lying on the couch upside down, still dazed from recent forgetfulness. The terrible, desperate screams from the street reached him sharply, which, however, he listened to every night under his window at three o'clock. They were what woke him up now. "Ah! And here they're coming out of the drinking dens drunk, — thought he, — third hour, — and suddenly jumped up as if someone had pulled him off the couch. — What! Already the third hour!" He sat on the couch, — and then remembered everything!
