"Put on the sock!" — it suddenly occurred to him, "it will get even more dusty, and the traces will disappear." But just as he put it on, he immediately pulled it off with disgust and horror. He pulled it off, but realizing there was no other option, he took it again and put it on — and laughed again. "Everything is conditional, everything is relative, all of this is just forms," — he thought briefly, with only a fleeting thought, while trembling all over, "after all, he did put it on! After all, he ended up putting it on!" Laughter, however, immediately turned into despair. "No, it's beyond my strength..." — he thought. His legs were trembling. "From fear," — he muttered to himself. His head was spinning and aching from the heat.
