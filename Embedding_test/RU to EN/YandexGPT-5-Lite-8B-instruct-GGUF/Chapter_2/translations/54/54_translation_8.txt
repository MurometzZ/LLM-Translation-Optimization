 "Not that at all!.."

Suddenly he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question knocked him off balance and surprised him bitterly:

"If indeed all this was done consciously and not stupidly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how is it that you haven't even looked into the wallet yet and don't know what you got, why you went through all the муки and deliberately set out to do such a vile, гадкое, low deed? You were going to throw the wallet away in the water right now, along with all the things you also haven't seen yet... How is that possible?"

Yes, it's like that; it's all like that. He knew this before, and this isn't a new question for him at all; and when it was decided to throw it into the water at night, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if that's how it should be, as if it couldn't be any other way... Yes, he knew all this and remembered everything; yes, perhaps it was already decided yesterday, at the very moment when he was sitting over the chest and dragging cases out of it... And yet!.. "This is because I am very ill," he finally decided gloomily, "I have tormented and tortured myself, and I don't know what I'm doing... Both yesterday and the day before yesterday, and all this time I've been tormenting myself... I will recover and... won't torture myself anymore... But what if I don't recover at all? Lord! How sick I am of all this!.." He walked without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow dissipate himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to undertake. A new, irresistible sensation took hold of him more and more with almost every minute: it was some kind of endless, almost physical disgust for everything that met and surrounded him, stubborn, spiteful, hateful. He felt disgusted by everyone who met him — their faces, gait, movements were disgusting to him. He would have spat on someone, bitten them, it seems, if anyone had spoken to him...

He suddenly stopped when he came out onto the embankment of the Malaya Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge.
