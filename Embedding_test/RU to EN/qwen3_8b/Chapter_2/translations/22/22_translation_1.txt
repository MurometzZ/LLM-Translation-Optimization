— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, — he said to the brightly dressed crimson lady, who stood still as if unable to sit herself, although a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said, and quietly, with a silk-like sound, sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim, like an air balloon, spread around the chair and occupied almost half the room. It carried a scent of perfume. But the lady, obviously, felt shy about taking up almost half the room and the strong perfume emanating from her, although she smiled timidly and boldly at the same time, but with evident unease. The lady in black finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very manly and somehow particularly twisting her shoulders with every step, entered the officer, threw his hat with a cockade on the table, and sat in the chair. The plump lady jumped up from her place upon seeing him, and with some special enthusiasm began to sit down; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared to sit in his presence.
