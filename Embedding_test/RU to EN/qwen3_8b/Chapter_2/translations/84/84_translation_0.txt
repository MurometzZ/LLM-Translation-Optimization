—I'll bring it. Raskolnikov looked at everything with deep astonishment and a dull, senseless fear. He decided to remain silent and wait: what would happen next? "It seems I'm not dreaming," he thought, "it seems this is really happening..."

Two minutes later, Nastasya returned with soup and announced that tea would come soon. With the soup came two spoons, two plates, and the entire set: salt, pepper, horseradish for beef, and other items that had long since not been used in such order. The tablecloth was clean. — It would be nice, Nastasya, if Praskovya Pavlovna sent two beer bottles. We'll have a drink. — Well, you, tall fellow! — Nastasya muttered and went to carry out the command.
