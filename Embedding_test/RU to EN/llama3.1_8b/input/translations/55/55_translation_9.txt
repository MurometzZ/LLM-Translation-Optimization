He would have just spat on someone and bitten them if anyone had spoken to him...

He stopped suddenly when he came out onto the embankment of the Little Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near a bridge. "That's where he lives, in that house," he thought. "What is it? But I myself came here! The same old story as before... And yet, very interesting: did I come here or was I just passing by and happened to stop here? Doesn't matter; I said... three days ago... that I would go see him the next day, well, I'll go! As if I couldn't go in now..."

He went up to Razumikhin's place on the fifth floor. He was at home, in his own little room, and was writing at this moment, and he himself let him in. They hadn't seen each other for four months. Razumikhin sat in his tattered dressing gown, in slippers with one foot bare, disheveled, unshaven, and unwashed. A look of surprise appeared on his face.
