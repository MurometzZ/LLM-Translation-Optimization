And suddenly, to his greatest amazement, he caught a glimpse of the voice of his landlady. She was wailing, screaming and lamenting, rushing, hurrying, uttering words so indistinct that they couldn't be made out, begging for something - of course, for them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the beater had become so terrifying from malice and fury that it could hardly be heard as anything more than a croak, but the beater himself was saying something too, and just as quickly, incoherently, rushing and choking. Suddenly Razumikhin shuddered like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice! And Ilya Petrovich is here beating his landlady! He's kicking her with his feet, pounding her head against the step - that's clear, that can be heard from the sounds, the screams, the blows! What is this, has the light turned upside down? It was audible how a crowd gathered in all the apartments, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, rising, knocking, banging on doors, rushing out. "But why, why, and how can it be?" he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he had gone mad. But no, he is hearing everything too clearly!
