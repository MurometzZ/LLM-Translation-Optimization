— All these sensitive details, sir, do not concern us at all, — Ilya Petrovich snapped rudely, — you must give a review and an undertaking, and as for what you were in love with and all those tragic places, we don't care about any of that. — Well, you... are cruel... — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He felt somehow ashamed. — Write on, — said the clerk to Raskolnikov. — What am I supposed to write? — he asked somewhat especially roughly. — And I'll dictate it to you, — said the clerk. Raskolnikov thought that the clerk had become careless and contemptuous towards him after his confession, but strangely enough, — he suddenly felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change occurred somehow in one moment, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little bit, he would have certainly been surprised at how he could speak with them like that just a minute ago, and even impose his feelings on them? And where did these feelings come from?
