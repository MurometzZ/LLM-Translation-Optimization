His face showed surprise. "What's the matter?" he shouted, examining his incoming companion from head to foot; then he fell silent and whistled. "Is it really that bad? You've surpassed our brother, brother," he added, looking at Raskolnikov's tattered clothes. "Sit down, you must be tired!" And when the latter collapsed onto the Turkish crepe paper divan, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly noticed that his guest was ill. "You're seriously ill, you know that?" He started feeling his pulse; Raskolnikov pulled away his hand. "No need," he said, "I came... that is, I had no lessons... I wanted to... in any case, I don't need lessons at all..."

"Do you know what?"
