He would just spit on someone, bite them, I suppose, if anyone talked to him...  
He suddenly stopped when he came out onto the embankment of the Little Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, by the bridge. "Here he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this, I came to Razumikhin on my own! It's the same story again, as before... It's very curious, indeed: did I come on my own or just walked and ended up here? Either way; I said... three days ago... that I would go to him the next day, well, I'll go! As if I can't even visit now..."  
He went up to Razumikhin's apartment on the fifth floor. He was home, in his room, and at that moment he was occupied, writing, and opened the door himself. It's been four months since they last saw each other.  
Razumikhin was sitting in his own room in a tattered, threadbare robe, in socks on his bare feet, tousled, unshaven, and unclean. His face showed surprise.
