The scribe began dictating to him the standard form of a withdrawal in such a case, that is, I cannot pay, but I promise then—when ever—never to leave the city, not to sell or give away my property, and so on. — You cannot write at all, your pen is slipping from your hand, — remarked the scribe, looking curiously at Raskolnikov. — Are you ill? — Yes... my head is spinning... go on! — Yes, everything; sign your name. The scribe took the paper and started working with the others. Raskolnikov handed over the pen, but instead of standing up and leaving, he placed both elbows on the table and clutched his head with his hands. It felt as if a nail was being driven into his forehead. A strange thought suddenly came to him: to stand up now, go to Nikodim Fomich, and tell him everything from yesterday, every last detail, then go together with them to the apartment and show them the items in the corner, in the hole. The impulse was so strong that he had already risen from his seat, ready to carry it out.
