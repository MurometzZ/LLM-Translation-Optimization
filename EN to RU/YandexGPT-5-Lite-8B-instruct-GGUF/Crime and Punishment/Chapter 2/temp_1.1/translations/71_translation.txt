 She seemed to be about thirty years old and was certainly an odd wife for Marmeladov.... She hadn't heard them and didn't notice them coming in. She appeared to be lost in thought, hearing and seeing nothing. The room was stuffy, but she hadn't opened the window; a stench rose from the staircase, but the door leading to it wasn't closed. From the inner rooms clouds of tobacco smoke floated in, she kept coughing, but did not close the door. The youngest child, a girl of six, was asleep, sitting curled up on the floor with her head resting on the sofa.
