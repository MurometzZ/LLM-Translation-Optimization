Louisa Ivanovna with a benevolent smile began to squat down in all directions and, squatting, reached the doors; but at the doors she bumped her backside into one of the prominent officers, with an open fresh face and with excellent thick fair mustaches. This was Nikodim Fomich, the quarter's overseer. Louisa Ivanovna hastened to sit down almost to the floor and with frequent small steps, jumping, flew out of the office. — Again thunder, again storm and lightning, tornado, hurricane! — said Nikodim Fomich kindly and affably to Ilya Petrovich, — again they have disturbed my heart, again it has boiled over! I even heard from the stairs. — What? — with noble nonchalance said Ilya Petrovich (and not what, but somehow: "Da-a sh-ta-a!"), moving with some papers to another table and dramatically flinging his shoulders with each step; wherever he stepped, there was his shoulder; — here you see: the gentleman writer, that is, the student, who used to be, does not pay money, gave out IOUs, does not clean the apartment, constant complaints come in about them, and they have taken it upon themselves to make a claim, saying that I lit a cigarette among them! They are inciting, but look at them: here they are in their most attractive appearance now. — Poverty is no vice, friend, well, what can you do?
