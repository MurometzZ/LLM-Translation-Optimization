"—Is that how they hide it? My mind is leaving me!" Collapsing in exhaustion, he sat on the couch, and immediately an unbearable chill shook him. Mechanically, he pulled up the old winter coat he had used as a student, warm but already in tatters, covered himself with it, and once again sleep and delirium seized him. He fell into oblivion. No more than five minutes later, he suddenly jumped up again, and immediately, in a frenzy, rushed back to his dress. "How could I have fallen asleep again, with nothing done! That's exactly it, that's exactly it: I still haven't taken off the necktie under my armpit! I forgot, I forgot about such a thing! Such a clue!" He pulled the necktie and quickly began tearing it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow in the bedclothes. "Pieces of torn flannel will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with a strained, painful attention, he began once again to scrutinize everything around him, on the floor and everywhere, to make sure he hadn't forgotten anything else?"
