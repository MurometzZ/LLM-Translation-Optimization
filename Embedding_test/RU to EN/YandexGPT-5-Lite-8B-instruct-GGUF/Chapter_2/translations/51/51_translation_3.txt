 A small depression formed under the stone; immediately he began to throw everything from his pocket into it. The wallet ended up at the very top, and still there was room left in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one turn to its original side, and it just fell into its original place, perhaps a little higher, barely noticeable. But he raked up some earth and pressed down on the edges with his foot. Nothing could be seen. Then he went out and headed towards the square. Again a strong, almost unbearable joy, as before in the office, took hold of him for a moment. “The ends are hidden! And who, who would think to look under this stone? It has been there perhaps since the house was built and will lie there for just as long.”
