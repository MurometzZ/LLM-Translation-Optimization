Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely open. He walked quickly and firmly, and though he felt completely shattered, his consciousness was with him. He feared pursuit, feared that within half an hour, within a quarter of an hour, someone would already come out, probably an instruction to monitor him; therefore, no matter what, he had to disappear before the time came. He had to deal with it while there was still some strength and some reasoning left… Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the ditch, and the ends into the water, and that's that." He had decided this even earlier, during the night, in a delirium, during those moments when he remembered it, several times trying to stand up and go: "as soon as possible, as soon as possible, and throw everything away." But throwing it away turned out to be very difficult. He had been wandering along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, maybe more, and several times looked at the access points into the ditch where they had met. But it was impossible to carry out the intention: either the rafts were at the very access points with laundry being washed on them, or the boats were moored, and people were everywhere, and from all sides, from the embankments, from all around, one could see and notice that it was suspicious that a person intentionally went down, stopped, and threw something into the water. What if the cases didn't sink but floated away?
