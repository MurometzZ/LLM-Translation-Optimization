A small depression formed under the stone; he immediately began throwing everything from his pocket into it. The wallet landed on top and yet there was still room left in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one rotation to its original side, and it just fit back into its original place, except that it seemed a bit higher. He pushed some earth under it and pressed down on the edges with his foot. Nothing could be seen. Then he went out and headed towards the square. Again, strong and barely bearable joy took hold of him for an instant, just like in the office before. "The ends are buried! And who would even think to look for them under this stone? It may have been lying here since the house was built and will lie here for just as long again."
