This was some particularly disheveled person with an immobile idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from him because he doesn't care," thought Raskolnikov. — Go there, to the clerk, said the scribe and pointed forward with his finger, showing the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), a narrow one tightly packed with people - the common folk, somewhat better dressed than those in the other rooms. Between the visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at a table opposite the clerk and was writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and red-faced, with spots, a noticeable woman, and extremely lavishly dressed, with a brooch on her chest, the size of a tea saucer, stood aside waiting for something. Raskolnikov handed the clerk his summons. He glanced briefly at it, said: "wait" and continued working with the lady in mourning. He took a freer breath.
