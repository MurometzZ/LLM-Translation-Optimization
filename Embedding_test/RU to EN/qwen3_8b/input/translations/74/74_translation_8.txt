Then he would try to get up, wanting to run away, but someone would always stop him by force, and he would again fall into helplessness and confusion. Finally, he fully came to himself. This happened in the morning, at ten o'clock. At this hour of the morning, on clear days, the sun always cast a long strip of light along his right wall, illuminating the corner by the door. By his bed stood Nastasia and another person, who was looking at him very curiously and completely unknown to him. This was a young man in a kaftan, with a beard, and from his appearance, he looked like an artel member. The landlady was peeking through the half-open door. Raskolnikov raised himself. — Who is this, Nastasia? — he asked, pointing at the young man.
