Louisa Ivanovna with a benevolent smile began to squat down in all directions and, squatting, reached the doors; but at the doors she bumped her backside into one of the prominent officers, with an open fresh face and with magnificent thick fair mustaches. This was Nikodim Fomich himself, the quarter's overseer. Louisa Ivanovna hastened to sit down almost to the floor and with frequent small steps, jumping, flew out of the office. — Again thunder, again storm and lightning, tornado, hurricane! — Nikodim Fomich kindly and affably addressed Ilya Petrovich, — again they have disturbed my heart, again it has boiled over! I even heard from the stairs. — What? — with noble carelessness said Ilya Petrovich (and not what, but somehow: "Da-a sh-ta-a!"), moving with some papers to another table and dramatically flinging his shoulders with each step; wherever he stepped, there was a shoulder; — here you see, if you please: the gentleman writer, that is, the student, who used to be, does not pay money, issued IOUs, does not clean the apartment, constant complaints are coming in about them, and they have taken it upon themselves to make a complaint that I lit a cigarette among them! They themselves are whining, but here you see: take a look at them; they are now in their most attractive appearance. — Poverty is not a vice, friend, well, what can be done?
