 Louise Ivanovna began curtsying in all directions with hurried любезностью and, while curtsying, backed up to the door; but at the door she bumped into a prominent officer from behind, with an open fresh face and excellent thick blond sideburns. It was Nikodim Fomich himself, the district supervisor. Louise Ivanovna hastened to curtsy almost to the floor and, taking frequent small steps, bounced out of the office.  
“Thunder again, thunder and lightning again, tornado, hurricane!” — Nikodim Fomich addressed Ilya Petrovich in a friendly and amicable manner, — “again they stirred up my heart, again I boiled over! I heard it from the stairs.”  
“Yes what!” — said Ilya Petrovich with noble negligence (and even not “shto”, but somehow: “Da-a shta-a!”), moving to another table with some papers and dramatically shrugging his shoulders with each step, where he steps, there goes the shoulder; — here you see, please look: the gentleman writer, that is, the student, former that is, does not pay money, has given bills, does not clear out the apartment, complaints are constantly received about them, and you took offense when I lit a cigarette in their presence! They themselves commit fraud, but here, please take a look at them: this is their most attractive appearance now!”  
“Poverty is not a vice, friend, well, what of it!”
