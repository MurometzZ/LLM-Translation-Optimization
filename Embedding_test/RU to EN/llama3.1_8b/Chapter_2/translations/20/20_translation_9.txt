This was some especially disheveled person with a motionless idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from him because he doesn't care," thought Raskolnikov. — Go there, to the clerk, said the scribe and pointed forward with his finger, showing the last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), cramped and packed with people - a crowd of people, somewhat better dressed than those rooms. Between visitors were two ladies. One was in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at the table opposite the clerk and wrote something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and reddish-pink, with spots, a noticeable woman, and something extremely lavishly dressed, with a brooch on her chest, the size of a tea saucer, stood aside and waited for something. Raskolnikov handed the clerk his summons. He glanced briefly at it, said: "wait" and continued to work with the mourning lady. He took a freer breath.
