The air was stifling. Sweepers with notebooks under their arms and passersby of both sexes, visitors, were going up and down. The door to the office was also wide open. He entered and stopped in the entrance hall. Everyone stood there waiting for some men. The air was stifling here too, and besides, a fresh, still wet paint smell from newly painted rooms made his nose feel queasy. After a little while, he thought it was time to move on again, into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low-ceilinged. A terrible impatience was driving him further and further. No one noticed him.
