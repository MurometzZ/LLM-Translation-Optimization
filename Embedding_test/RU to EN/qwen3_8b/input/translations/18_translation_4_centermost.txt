There was a terrible stuffiness because of that. Up and down, janitors with books under their arms came and went, strutting and various people of both sexes—visitors. The door to the very office was also left open. He entered and stopped in the hallway. Here, everyone stood and waited for some men. Here, too, there was an extraordinary stuffiness, and additionally, it was so nauseating that the fresh, still wet paint on the old, rancid oil of the newly painted rooms hit the nose. After waiting a bit, he decided to move further forward into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low. Terrible impatience was pulling him further and further. Nobody noticed him.
