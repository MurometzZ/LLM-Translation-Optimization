"The third hour already! He sat on the sofa—and then he suddenly remembered everything! All at once, in an instant, he remembered everything! In the first moment, he thought he was going mad. A terrible cold gripped him; but the cold was also from the fever, which had already begun with him in his dream. Now, however, a sudden chill struck him so strongly that his teeth almost jumped out, and everything inside him was shaking. He opened the door and began to listen: everything in the house was completely asleep. He looked at himself in astonishment and everything around the room, not understanding: how could he, entering yesterday, not lock the doors with the hook and fling himself onto the sofa, not only without undressing, but even with his hat on? It had slipped down and lay on the floor, near the pillow. 'What would someone think if they came in? That I'm drunk, but...' He rushed to the window. There was enough light, and he quickly began to examine himself, from head to toe, all his clothes: were there any traces?"
