Yes, he remembered later that he had laughed nervously, with a small, inaudible, prolonged laugh, and everything made him laugh the whole time as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto K Boulevard, where two days ago he met that girl, his laughter suddenly stopped. Other thoughts came to his head. It seemed to him too that it was terribly unpleasant for him now to pass by that bench on which he sat and thought after the girl left, and it would be terribly hard to meet again that old man to whom he had given two grivnas: "Damn you!" He walked, looking around distractedly and sullenly. All his thoughts were now spinning around one main point, — and he himself felt that this was indeed a main point and there was one, and that now, exactly now, he was left alone with this main point, — and that it was even for the first time after these two months.
