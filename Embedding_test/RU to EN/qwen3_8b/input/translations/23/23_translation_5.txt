The plump lady jumped up from her seat upon seeing him and began sitting down with some special enthusiasm; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared sit in his presence. He was a sublieutenant, assistant quartermaster, with reddish mustaches sticking out horizontally on both sides and exceptionally small facial features, nothing in fact except some impudence. He looked at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with disdain: his costume was too shabby, and despite all his humility, his posture was still not fitting the costume. Raskolnikov, by carelessness, looked at him too straight and prolonged, so that the officer even got offended. "What's the matter with you?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged fellow didn't even flinch from his lightning glance. "I was summoned… by order…," Raskolnikov replied rather awkwardly. "It's about the case concerning the recovery of money from them, from the student," hurried the clerk, detaching himself from the papers.
