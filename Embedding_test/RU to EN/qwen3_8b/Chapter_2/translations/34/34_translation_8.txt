Louise Ivanovna began to sit down with affected politeness on all sides, and as she sat down, she managed to reach the doors; but she collided with an officer at the door, hitting him in the back. He had an open, fresh face and excellent thick golden mustaches. It was Nikodim Fomich, the quarter supervisor. Louise Ivanovna hurried to sit down almost to the floor and, leaping with frequent small steps, flew out of the office. "Again thunder, again lightning, tornado, hurricane!" Nikodim Fomich politely and friendly addressed Ilya Petrovich, "again disturbed the heart, again it boiled over! I even heard it from the stairs." "What's this!" Ilya Petrovich said with noble indifference (and even not "what's this," but rather: "Da-a sh-ta-a!") while moving from one desk to another with some papers and dramatically shrugging his shoulders with each step, wherever he stepped, there his shoulder went; "Here you are, you may see: the gentleman writer, that is, the student, who used to be, doesn't pay money, gave promissory notes, doesn't clean the apartment, constant complaints come in about them, and they entered a claim that I smoked a cigarette in their presence! They themselves are being dishonest, but here you are, please look at them: here they are in their most attractive appearance!" "Poverty is not a sin, friend, but what's to be done!"
