"Bits of torn cloth will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" he kept repeating, standing in the middle of the room, and with painful, agonizing attention, he began looking around again, on the floor and everywhere, wondering if he had forgotten something else? The certainty that everything, even memory, even the simplest thought, had left him began to unbearably torment him. "What, is it really beginning? Is it really the execution that's about to start? There it is, there it is!" Indeed, the frayed edges of the fringe he had cut from his pants lay scattered on the floor, in the middle of the room, to be seen at first glance! "What is happening to me!" he exclaimed again, as if lost. At that moment, a strange thought came to him: perhaps all his clothes were covered in blood, perhaps there were many stains, but he just didn't see them, didn't notice them, because his thoughts had weakened, crumbled... his mind was clouded. Suddenly he remembered that there was also blood on his wallet. "Bah! So, there must also be blood in the pocket, since I had put the wet wallet into my pocket then!" In an instant, he turned his pocket inside out, and—there it was—there were traces, stains! "So, I haven't completely lost my wits, there is still thought and memory, since I managed to realize it myself and figure it out!" he thought triumphantly, deeply and joyfully inhaling with his whole chest, "it's just a weakness from fever, a momentary delirium," and he tore out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants.
