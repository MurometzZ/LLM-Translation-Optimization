Louise Ivanovna, with affected politeness, began to sit down on all sides, and as she sat down, she managed to reach the doors; but she collided with a prominent officer at the door, who had an open, fresh face and excellent thick golden mustaches. It was Nikodim Fomich himself, the quarter supervisor. Louise Ivanovna hurried to sit down almost to the floor and, with frequent small steps, leaping about, flew out of the office. "Again thunder, again lightning, tornado, hurricane!" Nikodim Fomich politely and friendlily addressed Ilya Petrovich, "again disturbed the heart, again it boiled over! I even heard it from the stairs." "What's this!" Ilya Petrovich said with noble indifference (and even not "what's this," but rather: "Da-a shcha-a!") while moving from one desk to another with some papers and dramatically shrugging his shoulders with each step, wherever he stepped, there his shoulder went; "Here you are, you may see: the gentleman writer, that is, the student, who used to be, doesn't pay money, gave promissory notes, doesn't clean the apartment, and there are constant complaints about them, and they even entered a claim that I smoked a cigarette in their presence! They themselves are the ones who are rude, but here you are, you may look at them: here they are in their most attractive appearance!" "Poverty is not a sin, friend, but what's to be done!"
