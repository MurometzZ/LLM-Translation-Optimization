The air was stifling. Sweepers with notebooks under their arms, passersby of all sorts, and visitors were going up and down the stairs. The door to the office was wide open too. He entered and stopped in the entrance hall. Everyone stood there waiting for some men. It was just as stifling here, and besides, a fresh, still wet paint smell from newly painted rooms made his nose feel queasy. After a bit, he thought it was time to move on again, into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low-ceilinged. A terrible impatience kept drawing him further and further in. Nobody noticed him.
