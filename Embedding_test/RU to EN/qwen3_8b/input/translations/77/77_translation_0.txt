Well, then, who are you? — I'm a partner in our office, from the merchant Shelopaev, and I've come here on business. — Please sit on this chair, — Razumikhin himself took the other chair, on the opposite side of the table. — You did well, brother, to have regained consciousness, — he continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — You've barely eaten or drunk for four days. They even gave you tea with a spoon. I brought Zosimov to see you twice. Do you remember Zosimov? He examined you thoroughly and immediately said that everything was trivial—something like a blow to the head. It's just nervous nonsense, he said. Your rations were poor, they gave little beer and sausage, that's why you got sick, but nothing, it will pass and settle down.
