There was enough light, and he quickly began to examine himself thoroughly, from head to toe, all his clothes: are there any traces? But that wasn't possible: shivering from chills, he began to take off everything and examine around again. He turned everything over, to the last thread and scrap, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there was nothing, it seemed, any traces; only on that spot where the pants at the bottom were cut off and hung as fringe, on this fringe remained thick traces of congealed blood. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the fringe. It seemed there was nothing else. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and items he had taken from the old woman's trunk are still lying in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to take them out and hide them yet! He didn't even remember them now, as he examined the dress! What is this?
