Louisa Ivanovna with a benevolent smile began to sit down on all sides and, sitting, reached the doors; but in the doorway she bumped her backside into one of the prominent officers, with an open fresh face and with excellent thick light blond whiskers. This was Nikodim Fomich himself, the quarter's overseer. Louisa Ivanovna hastened to sit down almost to the floor and with frequent small steps, jumping, flew out of the office. — Again thunder, again storm and lightning, tornado, hurricane! — Nikodim Fomich kindly and affably addressed Ilya Petrovich, — again they have disturbed my heart, again it has boiled over! I even heard from the stairs. — What? — with noble nonchalance said Ilya Petrovich (and not what, but somehow: "Da-a sh-ta-a!"), moving with some papers to another table and dramatically swaying his shoulders with each step; wherever he stepped, there was his shoulder; — look here, you see: the gentleman writer, that is, the student, who used to be, does not pay money, gave out IOUs, does not clean the apartment, constant complaints come in about them, and they have taken it upon themselves to make a claim, saying that I lit a cigarette among them! They are whining, but look here: let you take a glance at them; look at them in their most attractive appearance now. — Poverty is not a vice, comrade, well, what can be done?
