— Yes, she is my mistress, isn't she? — Well, so what if she is your mistress? The clerk looked at him with a compassionate smile tinged with pity, yet also a touch of triumph, as if he were a new recruit who had just begun to be bombarded: "So, what do you feel now, my dear?" But what did it matter now to him about borrowed writing, about reprimands! Was it worth even a hint of worry, let alone any attention at all! He stood, read, listened, answered, even asked questions himself, but all this was mechanical. The triumph of self-preservation, salvation from the crushing danger—this filled his entire being at that moment, without forethought, without analysis, without future speculation or riddles, without doubts or questions. It was a moment of complete, immediate, purely animal joy. But in that very moment, something like thunder and lightning occurred in the office. The ensign, still shaken by the disrespect, burning with anger, and obviously wishing to support the wounded ambition, launched himself at the unfortunate "puffy lady," who had been looking at him with the most idiotic smile since he had entered. — You, such a silly and peculiar one, — he suddenly shouted at the top of his lungs (the mourner lady had already left), — what happened to you last night?
