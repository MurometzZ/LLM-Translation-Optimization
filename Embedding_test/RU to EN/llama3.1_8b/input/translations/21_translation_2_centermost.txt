He took a deeper breath and said to himself: "Not that, probably!" He gradually began to calm down, trying with all his might to compose himself and regain his senses. "Some foolishness, some tiny carelessness, and I can be caught off guard! Ah... it's a pity there's no air here - the stuffiness... My head is spinning even more... and my mind too..."

He felt an awful mess inside him. He was afraid of losing control over himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything at all, but completely unrelated things. The scribe greatly interested him: he wanted to guess something from his face, figure out what was going on in his head. This was a very young man, twenty-two years old, with a dark and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than his age, dressed in the latest fashion and foppishly, with a parting on the back of his head, combed and pomaded, with numerous rings and cuffs on his white-gloved fingers and golden chains on his waistcoat. With one former foreigner here he even spoke two words in French, very satisfactorily. - Louise Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said briefly to the brightly dressed red-haired woman who stood there as if not daring to sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her.
