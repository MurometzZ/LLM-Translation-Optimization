"Pieces of torn flannel will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with painful intensity began scanning around, on the floor and everywhere, wondering if he had forgotten something else? The certainty that everything, even memory, even simple reasoning, left him began to torment him intolerably. "What, is it really beginning? Is this punishment upon me? There, there, that's exactly it!" Indeed, the frayed trim he had cut from his pants lay on the floor, in the middle of the room, to be seen by the first person! "What is happening to me!" he exclaimed again, as if lost. A strange thought came to him: perhaps his entire outfit was bloodstained, perhaps there were many stains, but he just didn't see them, didn't notice them, because his reasoning had weakened, fragmented… his mind was clouded. Suddenly he remembered that there was also blood on his wallet. "Ah! So there must be blood in the pocket too, since I had put the wet wallet into the pocket!" He immediately turned the pocket inside out, and—there it was—there were traces, stains, on the pocket lining! "So, I haven't completely lost my senses, so there is still reasoning and memory, since I realized it myself and guessed it!—he thought triumphantly, taking a deep and joyful breath from his entire chest—just a feverish weakness, a momentary delirium," and he pulled out the entire lining from the left pocket of his pants.
