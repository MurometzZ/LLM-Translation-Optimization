He didn't respond and held a piece of paper in his hands, not printing it out. — Yes, don't get up, Nastasya continued, feeling sorry for him and seeing that he was pulling his legs off the couch. — You're sick, so don't go: you won't burn yourself out. What's all this in your hands? He looked: there were cut-off pieces of braid, a sock, and scraps torn from the pocket in his right hand. And he slept with them like that. Later, thinking about it, he remembered that, even half-awake in the heat, he had tightly clenched everything in his hand and fallen asleep again. — Look at all these rags he's gathered and sleeps with them, just like a treasure... — Nastasya let out her nervous, sickly laugh. In an instant, he stuffed it all under his greatcoat and stared intently into it with his eyes. Although he could hardly make sense of anything at that moment, he felt that people wouldn't treat him the way they would when they came to take him away.
