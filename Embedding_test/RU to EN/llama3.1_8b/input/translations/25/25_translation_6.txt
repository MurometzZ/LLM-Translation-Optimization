"Don't shout! I'm not shouting, I'm speaking quite calmly, and it's you who are shouting at me; and I am a student, so I won't allow anyone to shout at me. The clerk was so flustered that in the first minute he couldn't even get out a word, and only some sort of spittle flew from his lips. He jumped up from his seat. 'Let's have it, ma-a-aa-lch! You're in my presence. Don't g-r-r-rumble, sir!' 'And you are too in my presence,' Raskolnikov shouted back, 'and besides, what's more, you're shouting and smoking a cigar, so we're all equally guilty.' Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable sense of pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile."
