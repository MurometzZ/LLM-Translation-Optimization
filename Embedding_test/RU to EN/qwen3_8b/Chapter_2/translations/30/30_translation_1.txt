— Ilya Petrovich! — began the scribe carefully, but stopped to wait for the time, since the boiling corporal couldn't be restrained except by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the plump lady, at first she trembled from thunder and lightning; but strange thing: the more numerous and fierce the curses, the more amiable her demeanor became, and the more charming her smile became, directed at the fierce corporal. She shuffled in place and kept bowing continuously, impatiently waiting for her to be allowed to speak her piece, and she waited. — There's no noise and fights here, sir captain, — she suddenly chattered, as if popping peas, with a strong German accent, though fluently in Russian, — and no, no scandal, they came drunk, and I'll tell you everything, sir captain, and I'm not to blame… I have a noble house, sir captain, and noble manners, sir captain, and I've always, always wanted no scandal. They came completely drunk and then asked for three more shots, and then one raised his legs and started playing the piano with his foot, and that's very bad in a noble house, and he broke the entire piano, and there's absolutely no manners here, and I said so. He took a shot and started pushing everyone from behind with it. And then I quickly called the butler and Karl came, he took Karl and hit him in the eye, and Henrietta also got hit in the eye, and I was hit five times on the cheek. And that's very indecent in a noble house, sir captain, and I shouted. He opened the window to the ditch and started howling like a little pig from the window; and that's a disgrace.
