 Further, in a recess of the yard, the corner of a low, sooty, stone barn peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some kind of workshop. There must have been some kind of establishment here, coachmaking or locksmithing, or something like that; everywhere, almost from the very gate, there was a lot of coal dust visible. “That’s where I could throw it all and leave!” — he suddenly thought. Not noticing anyone in the yard, he stepped through the gate and immediately saw a trough attached to the fence near the gate (as is often done in such houses where there are many factory workers, artel members, cab drivers, etc.), and above the trough, right there on the fence, was written in chalk the ever-present joke in such cases: “Stopping of carts is prohibited here.” So much the better, since there would be no suspicion that he had come and stopped. “I could throw it all into a heap somewhere and leave!”

After looking around once more, he already stuck his hand in his pocket when suddenly he noticed a large rough stone near the outer wall, between the gate and the trough, where the entire distance was about an arshin wide (about 70 centimeters), adjacent directly to the stone street wall. Beyond this wall was the street, the sidewalk, and you could hear passers-by снующих around, of which there were always many; but no one could see him from behind the gate, unless someone came from the street, which, however, was very likely to happen, so he had to hurry. He bent down to the stone, grabbed its top firmly with both hands, gathered all his strength and turned the stone over. Under the stone there was a small depression; immediately he began to throw everything from his pocket into it.
