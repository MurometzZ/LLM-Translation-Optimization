—I'll bring it. Raskolnikov looked at everything with deep wonder and dull, senseless fear. He decided to remain silent and wait: what would happen next? "It seems I'm not dreaming," he thought, "it seems this is really..." 

Two minutes later, Nastasya returned with soup and announced that tea would come soon. With the soup came two spoons, two plates, and the entire set of utensils: salt, pepper, horseradish for beef, and other things that had long since not been seen in such order. The tablecloth was clean. —Not bad, Nastasya, you should send off two beer bottles from Praskovya Pavlovna. We'll have a drink. —Well, you, my dear!— Nastasya muttered and went to carry out the command.
