His head was spinning and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with cunning and then knock me out completely," he thought to himself as he stepped onto the staircase. "It's terrible that I'm almost delirious... I can make up some nonsense..." On the stairs, he remembered leaving all his belongings scattered around, "and here they'll probably search for them on purpose without me," he recalled and stopped. But a sudden despair and cynicism about his fate took hold of him, so he waved his hand in dismissal and continued further. "Just get it over with quickly..."

On the street, the heat was unbearable again; just one drop of rain would have been welcome these past few days. Again there was dust, brick, and plaster, again the stench from the shops and bars, again drunkards, Chuvash vendors, and half-ruined cab drivers. The sun blazed brightly in his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely around - a familiar feeling of feverish dizziness when suddenly stepping out into a bright sunny day. Having reached the turn onto yesterday's street, he looked at it with agonizing anxiety, at that house... and immediately turned away his eyes. "If they ask, I might say so," he thought as he approached the office.
