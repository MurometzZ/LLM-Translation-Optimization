— Do not shout! — I am not shouting, but speaking very calmly, and it is you who are shouting at me; I am a student, and I will not allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant was so enraged that for the first moment he could not utter a word, and only some spittle flew from his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. — Do you dare be silent! You are in the presence. Do not behave like a rascal, sir! — Yes, and you are in the presence, — cried Raskolnikov, — and besides shouting, you are smoking a cigarette, so you are treating us all improperly. — Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
