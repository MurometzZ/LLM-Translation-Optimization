So he lay there for a very long time. Sometimes it seemed as if he were waking up, and during these moments he noticed that it was already night, yet the thought of getting up never occurred to him. Finally, he noticed that it was already daylight. He lay on the couch, still stunned from his recent forgetfulness. The terrible, desperate cries from the street suddenly reached him sharply, which he had heard every night under his window at three o'clock. It was these cries that woke him up now. "Ah! Here they come again, the drunkards from the taverns," he thought, "it's three o'clock— and suddenly he jumped up as if someone had dragged him off the couch. 'What! It's already three o'clock!' He sat down on the couch, and then everything came back to him!"
