"It will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" he kept repeating, standing in the middle of the room, and with painful, strained attention, he began to scrutinize everything around him, the floor, everywhere, not forgetting anything? The certainty that everything, even memory, even the simplest thought, left him began to torment him intolerably. "What, is it really beginning? Is it really the execution? There it is, there it is!" Indeed, the frayed edges of the fringe he had cut from his pants lay scattered on the floor, in the middle of the room, to be seen at once! "What is happening to me!" he cried out again, as if lost. Then a strange thought came to him: perhaps all his clothes were stained with blood, perhaps there were many stains, but he just didn't see them, didn't notice them, because his thoughts had weakened, crumbled... his mind was clouded. Suddenly he remembered that there was also blood on his wallet. "Bah! So, there must also be blood in the pocket, since I had put the wet wallet into my pocket then!" He immediately turned out his pocket, and—there it was—there were traces, stains, on the pocket lining! "So, I haven't completely lost my wits, there is still thought and memory, since I have just realized and guessed it myself!" he thought triumphantly, taking a deep, joyful breath from his entire chest, "it's just the weakness of fever, a momentary delirium!" And he tore out the entire pocket lining from the left pocket of his pants.
