— Don't shout! — I am not shouting, but speaking very calmly, and it is you who are shouting at me; I am a student, and I will not permit myself to shout. The assistant was so enraged that for the first moment he could not utter a word, and only some splutters came out of his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. — You are in the presence, so be quiet! Don't behave like a boor, sir! — And you are also in the presence, — Raskolnikov shouted, — besides, you are shouting and smoking a cigarette, so you are treating us all badly. — Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
