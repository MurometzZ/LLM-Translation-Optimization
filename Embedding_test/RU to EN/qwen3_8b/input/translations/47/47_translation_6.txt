Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely open. He walked quickly and firmly, and though he felt completely shattered, his consciousness was with him. He feared pursuit, feared that within half an hour, within a quarter of an hour, someone would already come out, perhaps an instruction to monitor him; therefore, regardless of what happened, he had to hide the ends until the time came. He had to deal with it while there was still some strength left and some kind of reasoning... Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the ditch, and the ends into the water, and that's that." He had decided this even during the night, in a delirium, in those moments when, as he remembered, he several times tried to get up and go: "As soon as possible, as soon, and throw everything away." But throwing it away proved very difficult. He had been wandering along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, maybe more, and several times looked at the access to the ditch where they had met. But it was impossible to fulfill the intention: either the rafts were standing right at the access and laundry women were washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and people were everywhere, and from all sides, from the embankments, one could see, notice: suspiciously, that a person intentionally went down, stopped, and threw something into the water. What if the cases don't sink but float away?
