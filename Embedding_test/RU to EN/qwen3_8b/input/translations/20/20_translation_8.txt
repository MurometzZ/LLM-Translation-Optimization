This was some particularly disheveled person with a fixed idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from this, because he doesn't care," thought Raskolnikov. "Go there, to the clerk," said the clerk, and pushed forward with his finger, pointing to the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), a cramped and crowded one filled with people—more neatly dressed than in those rooms. Among the visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning clothes, poorly dressed, sat at the desk facing the clerk, writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and flushed red with spots, a noticeable woman, dressed extremely elaborately, with a brooch on her chest the size of a tea saucer, stood aside and waited for something. Raskolnikov slipped his summons to the clerk. The clerk glanced at it briefly, said, "Wait," and continued working with the mourning lady. He breathed more freely.
