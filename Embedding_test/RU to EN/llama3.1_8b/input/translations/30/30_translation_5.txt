Ilya Petrovich! — began the clerk carefully, but stopped to wait for a moment, because the boiling officer could not be restrained otherwise than by holding him by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the lush lady, at first she was so startled by the thunder and lightning; but it's a strange thing: the more numerous and fierce her curses became, the more charming her appearance became, the more enchanting her smile turned out to be, directed at the stern officer. She was hopping on one foot and constantly squatting, impatiently waiting for them finally to allow her to speak up, and she waited. — There will be no noise or fighting in my house, Mr. Captain, she suddenly babbled like a burst of beans with a strong German accent, although speaking quickly in Russian, — and there won't be any scandal, none at all, but they came drunk, and I'll tell you everything, Mr. Captain, and I'm not to blame... My house is noble, Mr. Captain, and my upbringing is noble, Mr. Captain, and I always, always didn't want any scandal. But they completely came drunk and then asked three times for the bill, and then one raised his legs and started playing the piano with his feet, which is not good at all in a noble house, and he's breaking the piano, and there's absolutely no manners here, and I said so. And he took the pipe and started pushing everyone behind him with it. And then as soon as I quickly called the janitor to call Karl, he took Karl and poked his eye, and Henriette too poked her eye, but they hit me five times on the cheek. And this is not delicate in a noble house, Mr. Captain, and I shouted. And he opened the window onto the canal and stood in the window like a little pig, squealing; and this is shameful.
