—"All these sensitive details, my good sir, have nothing to do with us," Ilya Petrovich cut in brusquely, "you must give your statement and commitment, and as for all these tragic places where you happened to be in love, it's entirely irrelevant to us." —"Well, you're being cruel..." muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He suddenly felt ashamed. —"Write down," said the scribe to Rasikolnikov. —"What should I write?" —asked the latter rather rudely. —"I'll dictate to you," said the scribe. Rasikolnikov felt as if the scribe had become more careless and disrespectful toward him after his confession, but strangely enough, he suddenly felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change happened in an instant, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago, even imposing his feelings on them. And where did these feelings come from?
