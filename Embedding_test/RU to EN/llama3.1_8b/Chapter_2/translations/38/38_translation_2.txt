And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if suddenly the room were filled with his first friends, rather than quarterlies, then there would not have been a single human word for them in him, so suddenly had his heart emptied out. A dismal feeling of painful and endless isolation and alienation suddenly became conscious to his soul. It was not the baseness of his heart's confessions before Ilya Petrovich, nor the poruchik's triumph over him that turned his heart around like this all of a sudden. Oh, what business did he have now with his own baseness, with all these ambitions, poruchiks, Germans, demands, offices and so on! If they had even sentenced him to be burned at the stake in this moment, then he wouldn't have stirred, and probably wouldn't have listened to the verdict attentively. Something was happening to him that was completely unfamiliar, new, sudden, and never before experienced. Not that he understood it, but he clearly felt with all the force of feeling that not only could he no longer address these people in the quarter's office with his sensitive expansiveness as before, but even with anything at all; if they were all his own brothers and sisters, rather than quarterlies, then he would have had no need to address them at all, and it was pointless for him to do so in any case; he had never experienced a similar strange and terrifying feeling before. And what is most tormenting - this was more of an experience than a consciousness, more of a sensation than a notion; a direct sensation, the most tormenting sensation of all those that he had experienced in his life so far. The clerk began dictating to him the form of a usual response in such cases, i.e., I cannot pay, I promise to do it sometime, I won't leave the city, I won't sell or give away my property and so on.
