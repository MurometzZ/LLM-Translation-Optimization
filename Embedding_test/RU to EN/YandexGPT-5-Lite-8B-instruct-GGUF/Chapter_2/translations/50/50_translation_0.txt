 Further, in a recess of the yard, the corner of a low, sooty, stone barn peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some kind of workshop. There must have been some kind of establishment here, coachmaking or слесарный, or something like that; everywhere, almost from the gate itself, a lot of coal dust could be seen blackened. “That’s where I would throw it all and leave!” — he suddenly thought. Not noticing anyone in the yard, he stepped through the gate and immediately saw, right near the gate, a trough attached to the fence (as is often done in such houses where there are many factory workers, artisans, cab drivers, etc.), and above the trough, right there on the fence, was written in chalk the ever-present witticism in such cases: “Stopping of carts is prohibited here.” So much the better, since there would be no suspicion that he had come and stopped. “Here I could throw everything all at once into a heap and leave!”

Looking around once more, he already stuck his hand in his pocket when suddenly, near the outer wall, between the gate and the trough, where the distance was about an arshin wide, he noticed a large rough stone, perhaps weighing about a pound and a half, adjacent directly to the stone street wall. Beyond this wall there was a street, a sidewalk, you could hear passers-by scurrying around, of which there were always many; but no one could see him behind the gate, unless someone came from the street, which, however, was very likely to happen, so he had to hurry. He bent down to the stone, grabbed its top firmly with both hands, gathered all his strength and turned the stone over. Under the stone a small depression formed; immediately he began to throw everything out of his pocket into it.
