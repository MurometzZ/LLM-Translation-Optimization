Not at all!...”

He stopped suddenly; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question struck him and amazed him bitterly:

“If indeed everything was done consciously, not carelessly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how did you manage to look into the wallet and not know what you got, which caused all your suffering and deliberately went for such a vile, disgusting, low thing? You wanted to throw the wallet in the water now, along with all the things you haven't seen yet... How is this?"

Yes, it's like that; everything is like that. He, however, knew this before, and this was not a new question for him; and when it was decided to throw everything into the water at night, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if it had to be so, as if otherwise it would be impossible... Yes, he knew all this and remembered it; maybe it was even decided yesterday, in that very moment when he sat on the trunk and pulled out the cases from it... And indeed! “It's because I'm very sick,” he finally decided gloomily, “I've tormented and tortured myself, and I don't know what I'm doing... I tormented myself yesterday, and two days ago, and all this time... I'll recover and... won't torment myself anymore... But if I don't recover at all? God! How tired I am of it all!..”

He walked without stopping. He desperately wanted to distract himself somehow, but he didn't know what to do or what to try. A new, insurmountable feeling was taking hold of him more and more with almost every minute: it was an endless, almost physical disgust for everything that met his eyes and surrounded him, a stubborn, malicious, hateful one. All the people he met were disgusting to him - their faces, gait, movements were disgusting. He would have spat on someone, bitten them, it seemed, if anyone had spoken to him...

He stopped suddenly when he came out onto the Malaya Nevka embankment, on Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge.
