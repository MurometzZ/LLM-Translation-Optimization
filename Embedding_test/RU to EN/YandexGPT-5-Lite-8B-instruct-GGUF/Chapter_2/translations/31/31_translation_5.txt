 And he opened the window onto the canal and stuck his head out like a little pig, squealing; and that's shameful. And how can one stick one's head out of the window onto the street, like a little pig, squealing; and that's shameful. Fie-fie-fie! And Karl was pulling him by his tailcoat from the window behind him, and here, it's true, Mr. Captain, they tore his coat. And then he shouted that he had to pay a fine of fifteen silver coins. And I myself, Mr. Captain, paid him five silver coins for the coat. And this is an ungracious guest, Mr. Captain, who makes all sorts of scandals! I said, sir, there will be a big satire about you, because I can write about you in all the newspapers. — From writers, then? — Yes, Mr. Captain, and what kind of ungracious guest is that, Mr. Captain, when he comes to a noble house…
