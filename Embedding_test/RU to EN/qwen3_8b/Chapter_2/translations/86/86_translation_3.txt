— Go on, hurry up and get the tea, Nastasya, since, I suppose, you can manage without a faculty. Here's the beer! — he moved to his chair, brought the soup and meat closer, and started eating with such an appetite as if he hadn't eaten for three days. — Brother Rodia, I now have lunch here every day, — he muttered, as much as his meat-filled mouth allowed, — and it's all Pashenka, your housekeeper, who's taking care of me wholeheartedly. I, of course, don't insist, nor do I protest. Here's Nastasya with tea. What a quick one! Nastya, do you want some beer? — And by the devil! — And a cup of tea?
