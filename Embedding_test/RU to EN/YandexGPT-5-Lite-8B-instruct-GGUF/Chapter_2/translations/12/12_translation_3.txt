 He was not responding and holding a piece of paper in his hands without opening it. “Well, don’t get up,” Nastasya continued, feeling sorry for him and seeing that he was lowering his legs from the sofa. “If you're sick, then stay home: nothing will burn down. What do you have there in your hands?” He glanced: in his right hand he had cut pieces of fringe, a sock and scraps of a torn pocket. He slept with them like that. Later, reflecting on this, he recalled that when half-awake from the fever, he would tightly clench all this in his hand and fall asleep again. “Look at him, collecting rags and sleeping with them as if they were treasure...” And Nastasya burst into her painfully nervous laughter. He quickly shoved everything under his overcoat and stared intently at her. Although he could not fully comprehend it clearly at that moment, he felt that people would treat a person differently when they came to take him.
