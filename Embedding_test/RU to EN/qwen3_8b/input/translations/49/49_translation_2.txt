He was becoming extremely scattered and forgetful, and he knew it. He had to act decisively! He went to the Neva along the B Prospect; but on the way, another thought suddenly came to him: "Why go to the Neva? Why into the water? Isn't it better to go somewhere very far, perhaps again to the Islands, and there somewhere, in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush—bury all this and the tree, perhaps it would be noticed?" Although he felt that he was not capable of clearly and soundly discussing everything at this moment, the thought seemed to him infallible. But it was not destined for him to reach the Islands, and another thing happened: as he was leaving the B Prospect onto the square, he suddenly saw a left entrance into the courtyard, surrounded by completely soundproof walls. To the right, immediately after entering the gates, a soundproof, unpainted wall of the neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. To the left, parallel to the soundproof wall and also immediately from the gates, a wooden fence ran twenty steps into the courtyard, and then it made a turn to the left. This was a soundproof, walled-off place where some materials lay. Further, in the depth of the courtyard, a corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
