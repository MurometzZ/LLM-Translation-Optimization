The scribe began dictating to him the form of an ordinary withdrawal in such a case, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then—when-ever, I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on.—You cannot write, your pen is slipping from your hand," remarked the scribe, looking curiously at Raskolnikov. "Are you ill?"—Yes... my head is spinning... go on!—Yes, everything; sign. The scribe took the paper and began dealing with others. Raskolnikov handed over the pen, but instead of getting up and leaving, he placed both elbows on the table and clutched his head with his hands. It was as if a nail was being driven into his forehead. A strange thought suddenly came to him: to stand up now, go to Nikodim Fomich, and tell him everything from yesterday, everything down to the last detail, then go together with them to the apartment and show them the things, in the corner, in the hole. The impulse was so strong that he had already risen from his seat to carry it out.
