And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if the room were suddenly filled with his first-rate friends, he would not have a single human word for them, so suddenly had his heart emptied itself. A dismal feeling of tormenting and endless isolation and estrangement suddenly became conscious to his soul. It was not the baseness of his heart's outpourings before Ilya Petrovich, nor the poruchik's triumph over him that turned his heart around like this. Oh, what a matter now for him to worry about his own baseness, all these ambitions, poruchiks, Germans, demands, offices and so on! If he were even sentenced to be burned at the stake in this moment, he would not have stirred, and it's unlikely that he would have listened carefully to the sentence. Something was happening to him that was completely unknown to him, new, sudden, and never before experienced. Not that he understood, but he clearly felt with all the force of feeling that not only could he no longer address these people in the quarter office with his sensitive expansiveness as before, but even with anything at all; it would be pointless for him to turn to them, even if they were his own brothers and sisters, rather than quarter poruchiks. And so there was no need for him to turn to them, not in any case, and he had never experienced a similar strange and terrifying feeling before this moment. And what is most tormenting - it was more of an experience than a consciousness, more of a sensation than a concept; a direct, most tormenting sensation of all the sensations he had experienced in life so far. The clerk began to dictate to him the usual form of such a response, that is, I cannot pay, I promise to do this sometime, I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away my property and so on.
