Meanwhile, Razumikhin moved to the couch, awkwardly like a bear, wrapped his left hand around his head, despite the fact that he could have lifted himself up, and with his right hand brought a spoon of soup to his mouth, blowing on it several times beforehand to prevent him from burning. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov greedily swallowed one spoon, then another, the third. But after bringing several spoons, Razumikhin suddenly paused and announced that they needed to consult with Zosimov regarding the further steps. Nastasya entered, carrying two bottles of beer. — Do you want tea? — I want. — Hurry up and make tea, Nastasya, because, regarding tea, it seems we can do without the faculty.
