And there, to his greatest astonishment, he suddenly caught his mistress's voice. She was screaming, wailing, and sobbing, rushing and hurrying, spewing words so fast that nothing could be understood, pleading—obviously, begging them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the attacker had become so terrifying from hatred and fury that it was barely more than a rasp, yet the attacker was still speaking incoherently, rushing, struggling to get words out. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf—he recognized this voice; it was Ilyi Petrovich. Ilyi Petrovich was here, beating his mistress! He was kicking her, hitting her head against the steps—this was clear, audible from the sounds, the screams, the blows! What was this—had the world turned upside down? One could hear people gathering on all floors, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, doors slamming, people converging. "But why, why, and how could this be!"—he kept repeating, seriously thinking that he had completely lost his mind. But no, he was hearing everything too clearly...
