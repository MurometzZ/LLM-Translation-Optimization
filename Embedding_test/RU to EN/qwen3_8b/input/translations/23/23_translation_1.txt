The plump lady sprang up from her seat the moment she saw him, and with some special delight began to sit down again; but the officer paid her absolutely no attention, and she no longer dared sit down in his presence. He was a sublieutenant, assistant quartermaster, with russet-colored mustaches sticking out horizontally in both directions and with extremely small facial features, nothing in particular except for some impudence. He looked at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with indignation: his clothes were too shabby, and despite all his humility, his posture was still not appropriate for his clothes. Raskolnikov, by his carelessness, looked at him too straight and for too long, so that the officer even got offended. "What do you want?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged person didn't even flinch from his lightning glance. "He was summoned... by order..." answered Raskolnikov somewhat awkwardly. "It's about the case of collecting money from them, from the student," hurried the clerk, detaching himself from the papers. "Ah-ha!" and he tossed Raskolnikov the notebook, indicating the place in it,—"Read it! 'Money?'
