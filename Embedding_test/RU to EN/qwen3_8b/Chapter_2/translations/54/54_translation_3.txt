Quite the opposite!...

All of a sudden he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question immediately threw him off and greatly surprised him:

"If everything was indeed done intentionally, not foolishly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how come you haven't even looked into the wallet yet and don't know what you've received, for which you've endured all this suffering and willingly went through such a mean, nasty, low act? You even wanted to throw it into the water now, the wallet together with all the things you haven't even seen yet... How is that possible?"

Yes, that's true; everything is like that. He, however, had known this before, and it was not a new question for him; and when it was decided at night to throw it into the water, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if that was the way it should be, as if there was no other way... Yes, he knew all of this and remembered everything; perhaps it was already decided yesterday, at the very moment when he was sitting over the chest and taking out the cases from it... That's exactly it!... 'It's because I'm very sick,' he finally decided gloomily, 'I've tortured and torn myself apart myself, and I don't even know what I'm doing... And yesterday, and three days ago, and all this time I've tormented myself... I'll recover and... stop tormenting myself... What if I never recover at all? Lord! How tired I am of all this!...' He kept walking without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow relieve himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to take action. One new, insurmountable feeling was taking over him more and more, almost with every minute: it was some kind of infinite, almost physical repulsion towards everything he encountered and surrounded him, persistent, spiteful, hateful. He found everyone he met repulsive—repulsive were their faces, their gait, their movements. He simply wanted to spit on someone, maybe bite someone if they spoke to him...
