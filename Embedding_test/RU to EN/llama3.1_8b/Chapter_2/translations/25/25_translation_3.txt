— Don't shout! I'm not shouting, I'm speaking quite calmly, and it's you who are shouting at me; and I am a student and won't allow anyone to shout at me. The clerk was so flustered that in the first minute he couldn't even get out a word, and only some sort of spluttering came from his lips. He jumped up from his seat. — Let's have a bit of quiet! You're in my presence. Don't grrr...grrr...grumble, sir! — Yes, and you too are in my presence, — shouted Raskolnikov, — and besides what you're shouting about, you're smoking a cigar, so we're all equally guilty. — Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable sense of pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
