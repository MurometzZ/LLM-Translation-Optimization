Not at all!...

Suddenly he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question immediately threw him off and greatly surprised him:

"If indeed all this was done intentionally, not foolishly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how come you haven't even looked into the wallet yet and don't know what you've gotten, because of which you endured all the suffering and intentionally went through such a mean, nasty, low thing? Indeed, you wanted to throw it into the water now, the wallet, along with all the things you haven't even seen yet… What on earth?"

Yes, that's true; everything is like that. He, however, had known this before, and it was not a new question for him; and when it was decided at night to throw it into the water, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if it was only natural, as if otherwise it was impossible… Yes, he knew all this and remembered everything; almost as if it had been decided yesterday, in that very moment when he was sitting by the chest and taking out the cases from it… And that's exactly what happened! "This is because I am very ill," he decided gloomily at last, "I have tortured and torn myself apart, and I don't even know what I'm doing… And yesterday, and three days ago, and all this time I've been tormenting myself… I'll recover and… stop tormenting myself… What if I never recover at all? Lord! How tired I am of all this!..." He kept walking without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow relieve himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to take action. One new, insurmountable feeling was taking over him more and more, almost with every minute: it was some kind of infinite, almost physical repulsion toward everything he encountered and surrounded him, persistent, spiteful, hateful. He found everyone he met repulsive—repulsive were their faces, their gait, their movements. He just wanted to spit on someone, bite someone, perhaps, if anyone spoke to him…

He suddenly stopped when he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on the Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge.
