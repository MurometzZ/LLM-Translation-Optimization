Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, although he felt that he was completely broken, but his consciousness remained with him. He feared pursuit, he feared that in half an hour, perhaps a quarter of an hour, the instruction to follow him would already come out; therefore, no matter what, he had to bury the ends for now. He had to manage it while there were still some strength and some reasoning left... Where to go? This was already decided long ago: "Throw everything into the gutter, and the ends in the water, and that's it." So he had resolved last night, in a delirium, in those moments when, as he remembered it, he several times tried to get up and go: "as quickly as possible, as quickly as possible, and throw everything out." But throwing everything out turned out to be very difficult. He had been walking along the Embankment of Catherine's Canal for over half an hour, maybe more, and several times looked at the stairs into the canal where he met them. But it was impossible to think about carrying out his intention: either the rafts stood right next to the stairs and on them washerwomen were washing laundry, or boats were moored, and everywhere people swarmed, and from all sides you could see that a person had deliberately gone down, stopped, and thrown something into the water. And what if the boxes don't sink, but float?
