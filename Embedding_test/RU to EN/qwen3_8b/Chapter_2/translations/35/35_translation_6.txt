— Poverty is not a sin, my friend, well, what can you do! It's known that gunpowder can't bear an insult. You must have offended him somehow and couldn't help it, — continued Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov — but you're wasting your time: he's the most honorable man, I'll tell you, but gunpowder, gunpowder! He got angry, boiled over, burned out — and that's it! Everything passed! And in the end, only a heart of gold! They even called him in the regiment: "Lieutenant Gunpowder"...

— What a regiment indeed! — exclaimed Ilyá Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so nicely teased, but still pretending to be offended. Raskolnikov suddenly felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — Please, captain, — he began in a very carefree manner, suddenly addressing Nikodim Fomich — take a closer look at my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I've done anything wrong on my part.
