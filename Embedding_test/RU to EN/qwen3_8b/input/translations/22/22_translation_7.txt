— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, — said he to the brightly dressed crimson lady, who stood still as if unable to sit herself, although a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said, and quietly, with a silk-like sound, sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim, like a balloon, spread around the chair and occupied almost half the room. It carried a scent. But the lady, obviously, felt timid about occupying almost half the room and the scent that emanated from her, although she smiled timidly and boldly at the same time, but with evident unease. The mourning lady finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very manly and somehow particularly twisting her shoulders with every step, entered the officer, threw his hat with a cockade on the table, and sat in the chair. The voluminous lady jumped from her seat upon seeing him, and with some special enthusiasm began to sit down; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared to sit in his presence.
