The plump lady sprang up from her seat upon seeing him, and with some special enthusiasm began to sit down; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared to sit down in his presence. He was a lieutenant, assistant to the quarter master, with horizontally sticking-out reddish-brown mustaches on both sides and extremely small facial features, nothing, in fact, special except for some impudence, which they did not express. He looked at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with indignation: his costume was too shabby, and despite all the lowering, the posture was still not appropriate for the costume; Raskolnikov, by carelessness, looked at him too straight and long, so that he even got offended. — What do you want? — he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged person didn't even think of hiding from his lightning glance. — Required… by order… — Raskolnikov answered somewhat awkwardly. — This is about the case of collecting money from them, from the student, — hurried the clerk, detaching himself from the papers. — Here you are! — and he threw Raskolnikov the notebook, indicating the place in it, — read it! «Money? /think
