"— Is that how they hide it? My mind is truly leaving me!" Collapsing in exhaustion, he sat on the sofa, and immediately an unbearable chill shook him again. Mechanically, he pulled up the old winter coat he had used as a student, warm though it was now almost in tatters, covered himself with it, and once more sleep and delirium seized him. He fell into oblivion. No more than five minutes later, he suddenly jumped up again and, in a frenzy, rushed once more to his clothes. "How could I have fallen asleep again, with nothing done! That's exactly it, that's exactly it: I still haven't taken off the tie under my armpit! I forgot, I forgot about such a thing! Such a clue!" He pulled out the tie and quickly began tearing it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow in his underwear. "Pieces of torn linen will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with intense, almost painful attention, he began once more to scan around, on the floor and everywhere, to make sure he hadn't forgotten anything else?"
