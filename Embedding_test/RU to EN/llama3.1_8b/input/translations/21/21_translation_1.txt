He took a deeper breath and said to himself: "It's probably not that." He gradually began to calm down, trying with all his might to compose himself and regain his wits. "Some silly thing, some tiny carelessness, and I'll be caught! Ah... it's a pity there's no air here - the stuffiness... My head is spinning even more... and my mind too..."

He felt an awful mess inside him. He was afraid of losing control over himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything at all, but completely unrelated things, but that didn't work out either. The clerk was very interesting to him: he wanted to guess something from his face, figure it out. This was a young man of twenty-two years old with a dark and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than his age, dressed in the latest fashion and fatigued, with a part on his head, combed and pomaded, with many rings and cuffs on his white, polished fingers and golden chains on his waistcoat. With one of the former foreigners here he even spoke two words in French, very satisfactorily. - Louise Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said briefly to a brightly dressed red-haired woman who stood there as if not daring to sit down herself, although a chair was next to her.
