 — Louise Ivanovna, why don't you sit down, — he said to the smartly dressed purplish-red lady, who was still standing as if not daring to sit down herself, although there was a chair nearby. “Ich danke,” she said and quietly sank onto the chair with a silky noise. Her light blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like a balloon, taking up almost half of the room. A scent drifted through the air. But the lady was obviously робкая about occupying so much space and being so heavily scented, although she smiled timidly and brazenly at the same time, but with явным беспокойство. The траурная lady finally finished and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, quite smartly and turning her shoulders in a particularly special way with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table and sat down in an armchair. The пышная дама jumped up from her seat as soon as she saw him, and began to curtsy with some peculiar enthusiasm; but the officer did not pay her the slightest attention, and she no longer dared to sit down in front of him.
