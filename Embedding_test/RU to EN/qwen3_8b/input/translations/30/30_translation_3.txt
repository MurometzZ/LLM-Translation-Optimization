—Ilya Petrovich!— the clerk began, solicitously, but stopped to wait, since there was no way to hold the boiling corporal except by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the plump lady, at first she trembled from the thunder and lightning; but strange thing: the more numerous and stronger the curses became, the more pleasant her demeanor grew, and the more charming her smile became, directed at the furious corporal. She stomped in place and kept bowing, impatiently waiting for her turn to speak, and finally did. —There's no noise and fighting here, sir captain, — she suddenly chattered, as if spilling peas, with a strong German accent, though fluently in Russian, — and no, no scandal, they came drunk, and I'll tell you everything, sir captain, and I'm not at fault… I have a noble house, sir captain, and noble manners, sir captain, and I always, always wanted no scandal. They came completely drunk and then asked three times for a path, and then one raised his legs and started playing the piano with his foot, and that's very bad in a noble house, and he broke the piano, and there's absolutely no manners here, and I said so. He took a path and started pushing everyone from behind with it. And then I quickly called the butler and Karl came, he took Karl and blacked out his eyes, and Henrietta also had her eyes blacked out, and I got hit five times on the cheek. And that's very indecent in a noble house, sir captain, and I shouted. He opened the window and started howling like a little pig from the window; and that's a disgrace.
