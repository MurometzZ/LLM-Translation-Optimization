Yes, he remembered later that he had laughed nervously, in a high-pitched, barely audible, prolonged laugh, and was laughing all the time as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto K Boulevard, where two days ago he met that girl, his laughter suddenly stopped. Other thoughts began to crowd into his head. It seemed to him too that it was now terribly unpleasant for him to pass by that bench on which he sat and thought after the girl left, and it would be terribly hard to meet again that old man to whom he had given two grivnas: "Damn you!" He walked, looking around distractedly and sullenly. All his thoughts were now revolving around one main point, — and he himself felt that this was indeed a main point and that there was nothing else, — and that it was even for the first time after these two months.
