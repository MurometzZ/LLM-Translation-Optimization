— Poverty is not a sin, my friend, well, what can you do! It is known, gunpowder cannot bear an insult. You, perhaps, have offended him and could not restrain yourself—continued Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov— but you are wasting your time: he is the most noble person, I will tell you, but gunpowder, gunpowder! He flared up, boiled over, burned out—and that's it! Everything passed! And in the end, only a heart of gold! And in the regiment they called him: "Ensign-Gunpowder"...

— What regiment could it be!— exclaimed Ilyá Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so nicely teased, but still buzzing with irritation. Raskolnikov suddenly felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — Please, captain— he began, addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly, in a very free manner— consider also my situation... I am even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I have erred on my part.
