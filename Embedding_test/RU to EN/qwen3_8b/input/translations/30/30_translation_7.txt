—Ilya Petrovich!— the clerk began, solicitously, but stopped to wait, since there was no way to hold the boiling corporal except by the hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the dignified lady, at first she trembled from thunder and lightning; but strange thing: the more numerous and stronger the curses became, the more amiable her demeanor grew, and the more charming her smile became, directed at the furious corporal. She stamped in place and kept bowing continuously, impatiently waiting for her turn to speak, and finally did. —There's no noise or fight here, Sir Captain, — she suddenly burst out, as if spilling peas, with a strong German accent, though fluently Russian, — and no, no scandal at all, they came drunk, and I'll tell you everything, Sir Captain, and I'm not to blame… I have a noble home, Sir Captain, and noble manners, Sir Captain, and I've always, always wanted no scandal. They came completely drunk and then asked for three drinks, and then one raised his legs and started playing the piano with his foot, and that's very bad in a noble home, and he broke the piano entirely, and there's absolutely no manners here, and I said so. He took the stick and started pushing everyone from behind with it. And then I quickly called the stable boy and Karl came, he took Karl and hit him in the eye, and Henrietta also got hit in the eye, and I got my cheek hit five times. And that's very indecent in a noble home, Sir Captain, and I shouted. He opened the window and started screaming like a little pig through the window; and that's a disgrace.
