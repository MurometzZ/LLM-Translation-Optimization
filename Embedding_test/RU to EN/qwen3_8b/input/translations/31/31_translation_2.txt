And he opened the window, climbed in, and stood by the window like a squealing little pig; and that's a disgrace. And how can one squeal like a little pig by the window; and that's a disgrace. Fuh-fuh-fuh! And Karl dragged him by the coat from behind, and indeed, Mr. Captain, he tore his noble coat. And then he shouted that he wanted fifteen shillings as a fine. And I myself, Mr. Captain, paid five shillings as a fine. And this is an unworthy guest, Mr. Captain, and a troublemaker! I, I say, will write a big satire about you, because I can write about you everything in all the newspapers. — From the writers, then? — Yes, Mr. Captain, and what an unworthy guest, Mr. Captain, when in a noble house…
