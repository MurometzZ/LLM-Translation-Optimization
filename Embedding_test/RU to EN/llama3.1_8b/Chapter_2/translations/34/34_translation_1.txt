Louisa Ivanovna with a benevolent smile began to squat down in all directions and, squatting, reached the doors; but at the doors she bumped her backside into one of the prominent officers, with an open fresh face and with excellent thick fair mustaches. This was Nikodim Fomich, the quarter's overseer. Louisa Ivanovna hastened to sit down almost to the floor and with frequent small steps, jumping, flew out of the office. — Again thunder, again storm and lightning, tornado, hurricane! — Nikodim Fomich addressed Ilya Petrovich in a benevolent and friendly manner, — again they have disturbed my heart, again it has boiled over! I even heard from the stairs. — What? — Ilya Petrovich said with noble nonchalance (and not what, but somehow: "Da-a sh-ta-a!") as he passed some papers to another table and dramatically flung his shoulders with each step, wherever he stepped, there his shoulder was; — look here, you see: the gentleman writer, that is, the student, who used to be, doesn't pay money, issued IOUs, doesn't clean the apartment, constant complaints are coming in about them, and they have come into a claim, saying I lit a cigarette among them! They themselves are whining, but look here, take a glance at them: there they are in their most attractive appearance now. — Poverty is not a vice, friend, well, what can you do?
