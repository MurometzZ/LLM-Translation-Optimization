Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely ajar. He walked quickly and firmly, though feeling thoroughly broken, yet still conscious. He feared pursuit, feared that within half an hour, perhaps within a quarter of an hour, someone would already come out, probably with instructions to keep an eye on him; therefore, no matter what, he had to hide the ends until the appointed time. He had to deal with it while there was still some strength left and some reasoning left… Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the ditch, and the ends into the water, and that's that." He had made this decision the previous night, in a delirium, during those moments when, as he remembered, he several times tried to get up and go: "Hurry, hurry, and get rid of everything." But it turned out to be very difficult to get rid of it. He had been walking along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, perhaps even longer, and several times looked at the wharves in the ditch where he had been met. But it was impossible to carry out the intention: either the rafts were standing right at the wharves and the washerwomen were washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and everywhere people were crowding, and from all sides, from all directions, one could see, notice: it would look suspicious that a person deliberately went off, stopped, and threw something into the water. What if the cases didn't sink but floated away?
