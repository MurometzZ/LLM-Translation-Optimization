His face expressed surprise. "What's this?" he shouted, examining the entered comrade from head to foot; then fell silent and whistled. "Is it really that bad? You've outdone our brother," he added, looking at Raskolnikov's tattered clothes. "Sit down, you must be tired!" And when Raskolnikov collapsed onto a worn-out Turkish divan, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly realized that his guest was ill. "Are you seriously ill, do you know it?" He began to feel his pulse; Raskolnikov pulled away his hand. "Don't bother," he said, "I came... here's the thing: I don't have any lessons... I wanted to... but I don't need lessons at all..." "Do you know what?"
