"Bits of torn cloth will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" he kept repeating, standing in the middle of the room, and with painful intensity once again scanned his surroundings, the floor, everywhere, to make sure he hadn't forgotten anything else? The certainty that everything, even memory, even the simplest thought, had left him began to torment him unbearably. "What, is it really beginning? Is it really the execution that's about to start? There it is, there it is!" Indeed, the frayed edges of the fringe he had cut from his pants lay scattered on the floor, in the middle of the room, to be seen at first glance! "What is happening to me!" he cried out again, as if lost. At that moment, a strange thought came to him: perhaps all his clothes were covered in blood, perhaps there were many stains, but he just didn't see them, didn't notice them, because his reasoning had weakened, crumbled... his mind was clouded. Suddenly he remembered that there was also blood on his wallet. "Bah! So, there must also be blood in the pocket, since I had put the wet wallet into my pocket then!" In an instant, he turned his pocket inside out, and—there it was—there were traces, stains, on the pocket lining! "So, I haven't completely lost my wits, so there is still reasoning and memory, since I managed to realize it myself and figure it out!" he thought triumphantly, taking a deep, joyful breath from his entire chest, "it's just the weakness of fever, a momentary delirium," and he tore out the entire pocket lining from the left pocket of his pants.
