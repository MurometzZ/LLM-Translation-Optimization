Meanwhile, Razumikhin sat down on the couch beside him clumsily, like a bear, took hold of his head with his left hand, despite the fact that he himself could have raised it, and with his right hand brought a spoonful of soup to his mouth, blowing on it several times beforehand to prevent him from burning. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov greedily swallowed one spoonful, then another, then a third. But as he was about to take several more spoonfuls, Razumikhin suddenly paused and announced that they would have to consult Zosimov about further steps. Nastasya entered, carrying two bottles of beer. — Would you like some tea? — I would. — Hurry up, Nastasya, and bring some tea, because, apparently, one can manage without the faculty for tea.
