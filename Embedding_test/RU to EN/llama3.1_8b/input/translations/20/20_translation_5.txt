This was some particularly disheveled person with a motionless idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from him because it's all the same to him," thought Raskolnikov. — Go there, to the clerk, said the scribe and pointed forward with his finger, showing the last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), a narrow one tightly packed with people - the common folk, somewhat better dressed than those in the other rooms. Between the visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning, poorly dressed, sat at the table opposite the clerk and was writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and red-faced, with spots, a noticeable woman, and extremely lavishly dressed, with a brooch on her chest, the size of a tea saucer, stood aside waiting for something. Raskolnikov handed the clerk his summons. He glanced at it briefly, said: "wait" and continued working with the lady in mourning.
