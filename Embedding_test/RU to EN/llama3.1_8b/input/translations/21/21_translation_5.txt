He took a deeper breath and said to himself: "Not that, probably!" He gradually began to calm down, trying with all his might to compose himself and come to his senses. "Some foolishness, some tiny carelessness, and I'll be caught for sure! Ah... too bad there's no air here - the stuffiness... My head is spinning even more... and my mind too..."

He felt a terrible disorder in everything around him. He was afraid of losing control over himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything, but it didn't work at all. The scribe interested him greatly: he wanted to guess something from his face, figure out what was going on behind those eyes. This was a very young man, twenty-two years old, with a dark and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than his years, dressed in the latest fashion and foppishly, with a parting on the back of his head, combed and pomaded, with numerous rings and cuffs on his white-gloved fingers and golden chains on his waistcoat. He even spoke two words to one of the foreigners who was there, and did it quite successfully. - Louise Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said briefly to a brightly dressed red-haired woman who stood as if she couldn't bring herself to sit down, although a chair was right next to her.
