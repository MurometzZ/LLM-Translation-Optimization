He took a deeper breath and said to himself: "Not that, probably!" He gradually began to calm down, trying with all his might to compose himself and come to his senses. "Some foolishness, some tiny carelessness, and I can betray myself completely! Ah... it's a pity there's no air here - the stuffiness... My head is spinning even more... and my mind too..."

He felt an awful mess inside him. He was afraid of losing control over himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything at all, but that didn't work out either. The clerk was very interesting to him: he wanted to guess something from his face, figure it out. This was a young man of twenty-two years old with a dark and lively face that seemed older than his age, dressed in the latest fashion and foppishly, with a part on his head, combed and pomaded, wearing many rings and cuffs on his white-gloved fingers and golden chains on his waistcoat. With one former foreigner he even spoke two words in French, very satisfactorily. - Louise Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said briefly to the half-dressed red-haired lady who stood there as if she didn't dare to sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her.
