Poverty is not a vice, my friend, but indeed what can you do! It's well known that he couldn't stand an insult. You must have offended him somehow and not been able to hold back yourself, continued Nikodim Fomich, addressing Raskolnikov with courtesy - but it was all in vain: the most non-blasphemous, I'll tell you, person, but powder, powder! He blew up, boiled over, burned out - and that's it! And everything passed! And as a result, only gold from his heart! They even nicknamed him "lieutenant-powder"...

— What an army unit was that! exclaimed Ilia Petrovich, very pleased that they had tickled him so pleasantly, but still half-asleep. Raskolnikov suddenly wanted to say something unusually pleasant to all of them. — Forgive me, Captain, he began quite freely, addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly - take an interest in my situation... I'm even ready to ask for their apologies if I made any mistakes on my part.
