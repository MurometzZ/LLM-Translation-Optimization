— All these sensitive details, my good sir, have nothing to do with us, — Ilya Petrovich rudely cut in, — you must give your statement and commitment, and as for all these tragic places where you happened to be in love, it's entirely none of our business. — Well, you're being awfully cruel… — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He somehow felt ashamed. — Write down, — said the scribe to Raskelev. — What to write? — asked the latter rather rudely. — I'll dictate to you. Raskelev felt that the scribe had become more careless and contemptuous toward him since his confession, but strangely enough, — suddenly he himself felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change occurred somehow in an instant, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago, and even insist on his feelings? And where did these feelings come from?
