He took a deeper breath and said to himself: "It can't be!" He gradually began to calm down, trying with all his might to compose himself and come to his senses. "Some silly thing, some tiny carelessness, and I'll be caught! Ah... it's a pity there's no air here - the stuffiness... My head is spinning even more... and my mind too..."

He felt a terrible disorder in everything around him. He was afraid of losing control himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything at all, but completely unrelated things, but that didn't work out. The scribe strongly interested him: he wanted to guess something from his face, figure it out. This was a very young man, twenty-two years old, with a dark and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than his years, dressed in the latest fashion and fat, with a part on the back of his head, combed and pomaded, with many rings and cuffs on his white, polished fingers and golden chains on his vest. With one former foreigner here, he even spoke two words to him in French, very satisfactorily. - Louise Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said briefly to the brightly dressed red-haired woman who stood there as if not daring to sit down herself, although a chair was next to her.
