 The door leading to the other rooms, or rather cupboards, into which Amalia Lippevechsel’s flat was divided stood half open, and there were shouting, uproar and laughter inside. People seemed to be playing cards and drinking tea there. Words of the most unceremonious kind flew out from time to time. Raskolnikov immediately recognised Katerina Ivanovna. She was a rather tall, slim and graceful woman, terribly emaciated, with magnificent dark brown hair and a feverish flush on her cheeks.
