But no, he hears it too clearly!... But then, if that's the case, they will come to him now too, because... it's all the same... because of yesterday's... Lord! He wanted to lock himself in, but his hand wouldn't raise... and it was useless! Fear, like ice, had encased his soul, tormented him, paralyzed him... But at last, after this commotion, which had lasted for sure ten minutes, began to gradually subside. The mistress groaned and moaned, Ilia Petrovich still raged and cursed... But at last, it seemed, he had finally quieted down; now he was no longer heard; "Has he gone? Lord!" Yes, the mistress was leaving too, still groaning and weeping... and now her door slammed shut... And now the crowd is dispersing down the stairs to their apartments, shouting, arguing, calling out, raising their voices to a shout, then lowering them to a whisper. There must have been quite a number of them; perhaps the whole building had gathered. "But God, is all this really possible! And why, why did he come here!" 

Raskolnikov, in helplessness, fell onto the sofa, but could no longer close his eyes; he lay for half an hour in such suffering, in such unbearable feeling of boundless terror, which he had never experienced before. Suddenly, a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a plate of soup. Looking at him attentively and noticing that he was awake, she placed the candle on the table and began to arrange the things she had brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
