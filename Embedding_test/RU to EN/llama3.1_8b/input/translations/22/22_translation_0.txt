— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, he said to the hastily dressed red and pink lady who stood there as if she was too afraid to sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her. — Ich danke, I thank you, she said and quietly, with a soft rustling sound, sat down on the chair. Her light blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like an air balloon and occupied almost half the room. It wafted scents of perfume. But the lady was clearly nervous about taking up so much space and that her perfume was overpowering, although she smiled timidly and shamelessly at the same time, but with a clear sense of unease. The mourning lady finally finished and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, very bravely and somehow especially swinging his shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table and sat down in an armchair. The plump lady jumped right out of her seat when she saw him and with a special excitement began to curtsy; but the officer didn't even glance at her, and she was no longer able to sit next to him.
