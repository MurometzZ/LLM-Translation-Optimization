— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, he said to the slightly dressed-up red-haired lady who was standing there as if she didn't dare to sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her. — Ich danke, I thank you, she said and quietly, with a silk rustling sound, sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like an air balloon and occupied almost half of the room. It wafted perfumes. But the lady was clearly nervous about occupying half of the room and that it smelled so strongly of perfume from her, although she smiled timidly and shamelessly at the same time, but with a clear sense of unease. The mourning lady finally finished and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, very bravely and somehow especially turning his shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table and sat down in the armchair. The puffy lady jumped right out of her seat when she saw him and with a special excitement began to squat; but the officer didn't pay any attention to her at all, and she no longer dared to sit next to him.
