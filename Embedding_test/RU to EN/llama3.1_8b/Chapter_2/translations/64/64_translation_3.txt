But Raskolnikov had already stepped out onto the street. On Nikolsky Bridge he was forced to wake up once again due to a very unpleasant incident for him. A coachman from one of the carriages gave him a tight lash on his back with his whip because he almost got under the horses' hooves, despite the fact that the coachman had shouted at him three or four times. The blow from the whip infuriated him so much that he, jumping away to the railing (he was walking in the middle of the bridge, where vehicles pass, not pedestrians), snarled and snapped his teeth with malice. Of course, laughter echoed all around: "And what's the matter?!" "Some kind of beggar." "It's well known that drunks will deliberately run under the wheels; and you're responsible for them." "That's how they make a living, sir, that's how they make a living..." But at that moment, as he stood by the railing and still looked after the departing carriage with a senseless and malicious gaze, rubbing his back, he suddenly felt someone slipping some money into his hands.
