 — Don't bother shouting!
— I'm not shouting, I'm speaking quite evenly, and it's you who are shouting at me; but I'm a student and I won't allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant got so angry that for the first minute he couldn't say anything, only some spluttering came out of his mouth. He jumped up from his seat.
— Be s-s-silent! You are in the presence. Not r-u-b-bing, sir!
— And you are in the presence, — shouted Raskolnikov, — and besides, while shouting, you're smoking a cigarette, therefore you're showing disrespect to all of us. Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an inexpressible pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
