His head spun and ached from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me with a trick and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued to himself as he stepped onto the stairs. "The worst thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the stairs, he remembered that he always left everything in the wall cavity, "—and perhaps this time they deliberately searched without it," he thought, and stopped. But such despair and a sort of cynicism about death suddenly took hold of him that he waved his hand and went on. "Only if it's quick...!"  

Again, unbearable heat stood on the street; not a drop of rain for all these days. Again dust, bricks, and lime, again the stench from benches and taverns, again drunk beggars and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun shone brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely— the usual sensation of a feverish person suddenly stepping out into bright sunlight. Reaching the corner of the street from yesterday, he looked with agonizing dread at it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, maybe I'll say," he thought, approaching the office.
