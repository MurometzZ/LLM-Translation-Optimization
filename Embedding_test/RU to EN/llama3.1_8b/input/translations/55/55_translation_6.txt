He would have just spat on someone and bitten them if anyone had spoken to him...

He stopped suddenly when he came out onto the embankment of the Little Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near a bridge. "That's where he lives, in that house," he thought. "What is it, but I've come here myself! The same old story as before... But very interesting: did I really come to see him or was I just walking by and happened to stop here? Doesn't matter; I said I'd go the next day... after that, so I'll go now! As if I couldn't go in now..."

He went up to Razumikhin's place on the fifth floor. He was at home, in his little room, and was writing at this moment, and he himself let him in. They hadn't seen each other for four months. Razumikhin sat in a tattered dressing gown, in slippers with one foot bare, disheveled, unshaven, and unwashed. A look of surprise appeared on his face.
