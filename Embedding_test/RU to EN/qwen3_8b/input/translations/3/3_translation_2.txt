What's this? He dashed to take them out and throw them on the table. Having selected everything, even turning inside out his pockets to make sure there wasn't anything left, he moved all this pile to the corner. There, in the very corner, at the bottom, in one place, the wallpaper that had peeled off the wall was torn: at once he began to stuff everything into this hole, under the paper: "It's in! Everything out of sight and the wallet too!" — he happily thought, standing up and staring blankly at the corner, the hole now even more gaping. Suddenly he shuddered all over with fear: "My God," — he whispered in despair, — "what's happening to me? Is this hidden? Do they hide things like this?" Indeed, he hadn't expected anything but money; that's why he hadn't prepared a place in advance — "But now, now, what am I happy about?" — he thought. — "Do they hide things like this?"
