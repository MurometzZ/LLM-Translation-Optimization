Well, then, who are you? — I'm a partner in our firm, from the merchant Shelopaev, and I came here on business. — Please sit down on this chair, — said Razumikhin, sitting on the other side of the table. — You did well, brother, to recover, — he continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — You've barely eaten and drunk for four days. Honestly, they gave you tea with a spoon. I brought Zosimov to see you twice. Do you remember Zosimov? He examined you carefully and immediately said that it was all nonsense — as if something hit you on the head. Some kind of nervous disorder, he said, you had poor food, they gave you little beer and garlic, that's why you got sick, but nothing serious, it will pass and be over.
