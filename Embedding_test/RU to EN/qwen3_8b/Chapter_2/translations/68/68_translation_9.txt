And lo, to his greatest astonishment, he suddenly caught his mistress's voice. She was howling, screaming, and wailing, rushing, hurrying, spilling out words so fast that it was impossible to make sense of what she was saying, pleading—obviously, begging them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the one beating her had become so terrifying from hatred and fury that it was barely more than a rasp, yet the one beating her was also saying something, albeit in a mumbled, hurried manner, gasping and struggling to speak. Suddenly, Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf—he recognized this voice; it was Ily Petrovich's voice. Ily Petrovich was here, beating his mistress! He was kicking her with his feet, hitting her head against the steps—this was clear, audible from the sounds, the screams, the blows! What was this? Had the world turned upside down? One could hear people gathering on all floors, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, doors slamming, people rushing down. "But why, why, and how could this be!"—he kept repeating, seriously thinking that he had completely lost his mind. But no, he was hearing it all too clearly...
