 The door leading to the other rooms, or rather cupboards, into which Amalia Lippevechsel’s flat was divided stood half open, and there were shouts, uproar and laughter inside. It looked like people were playing cards and drinking tea there. Words of an unceremonious kind flew out from time to time. Raskolnikov immediately recognised Katerina Ivanovna. She was a rather tall, slim and graceful woman, terribly emaciated, with magnificent dark brown hair and with a hectic flush on her cheeks.
