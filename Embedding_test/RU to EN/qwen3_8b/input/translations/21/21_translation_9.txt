In the sentence **"He is fat,"** the word **"fat"** is used to describe someone's **body weight**. In this context, **"fat"** translates to **"obese"** or **"overweight"** in English. It refers to a person having an excessive amount of body fat, which is typically associated with health concerns.

### Key Points:
1. **Meaning in Context**:  
   - **"He is fat"** means the person has a high body fat percentage, often indicating obesity.  
   - This usage is common in everyday language to describe physical appearance or health status.

2. **Other Meanings of "Fat"**:  
   - In **cooking or food**, "fat" refers to oils, butter, or other substances rich in lipids.  
   - In **slang or informal contexts**, "fat" can sometimes mean "greasy" or "rich in fat" (e.g., "a fat steak").  
   - However, in the context of a person, the primary meaning is **body weight**.

3. **Cultural/Regional Notes**:  
   - The term "fat" can carry different connotations depending on cultural norms or personal preferences. Some may use it neutrally, while others might find it offensive. Context and tone matter.

### Summary:
In the sentence **"He is fat,"** **"fat"** means **"obese"** or **"overweight"**, describing someone with excessive body fat. Always consider context and tone when using or interpreting the word.
