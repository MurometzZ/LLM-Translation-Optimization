His head spun and ached from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me with deceit and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued to himself as he stepped onto the stairs. "The worst thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the stairs, he remembered that he always left everything in the upholstery hole—"and perhaps there's a deliberate search here," he thought, and stopped. But such despair and a sort of cynical acceptance of his impending death suddenly seized him that he waved his hand and went on. "Just hurry up..."  

Outside, the heat was unbearable again; even a drop of rain would have been welcome these days. Again, dust, bricks, and lime, again the stench from the benches and drinking places, again the constant presence of drunk beggars and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun shone brightly into his eyes, causing pain and making his head spin again—this was the usual sensation of a feverish person suddenly stepping out into bright sunlight. Reaching the corner of the street from yesterday, he looked with agonizing dread at it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, maybe I'll say," he thought, approaching the office.
