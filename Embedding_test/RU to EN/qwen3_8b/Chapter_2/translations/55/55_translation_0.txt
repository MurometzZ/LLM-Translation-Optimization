Just spit on someone, bite them, I guess, if someone talked to him...  
He stopped suddenly as he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, by the bridge. "Here he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this, I came here myself to Razumikhin! The same story again, as before... But it's very interesting: did I come myself or just walked here by accident? Anyway; I said three days ago... that I would go to him the next day, well, and I'll go! As if I can't even visit now..."  
He went up to Razumikhin's fifth floor. The latter was at home, in his room, and at that moment was occupied with writing, and opened the door himself. They hadn't seen each other for four months. Razumikhin was sitting in his tattered-to-rags robe, socks on bare feet, disheveled, unshaven, and dirty. Surprise was expressed on his face.
