Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, although he felt that he was completely broken, but his consciousness remained with him. He feared pursuit, he feared that in half an hour, perhaps in a quarter of an hour, instructions would already come out to follow him; therefore, no matter what, one had to bury the ends for the time being. One had to get through it while there was still some strength and some reasoning left... Where to go? This had been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the gutter, and the ends in the water, and that's it." He had resolved this even last night, in a delirium, in those moments when he remembered it several times and wanted to get up and go: "as quickly as possible, as quickly as possible, and throw everything out." But throwing everything out turned out to be very difficult. He had been walking along the embankment of Ekaterininsky Canal for half an hour already, or maybe more, and several times he glanced at the stairs into the gutter, where they met him. But one couldn't even think about carrying out his intention: either the rafts stood right next to the stairs and on them washerwomen were washing laundry, or boats were moored, and everywhere people swarmed, and from all sides you could see that a person had deliberately gone down, stopped, and was throwing something into the water. And what if the boxes don't sink, but float?
