The air was stifling. Sweepers with notebooks under their arms and passersby of both sexes, visitors, were going up and down. The door to the office was also wide open. He entered and stopped in the entrance hall. Everyone stood there waiting for some men. Here too the air was stifling and besides, a fresh, still unhardened paint on rotten oil in newly painted rooms hit his nose with nausea. After a while he thought it was time to move forward again, into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low-ceilinged. A terrible impatience pulled him further and further. No one noticed him.
