— Poverty is not a sin, my friend, but well, what can you do! It's known that Gunpowder couldn't bear an insult. You, I suppose, have taken offense against him and couldn't help it—continued Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov— but you're wasting your time: he's the most noble person, I'll tell you, but Gunpowder, Gunpowder! He got angry, boiled over, burned out—and that's it! Everything passed! And in the end, only a heart of gold! In the regiment, they called him: "Ensign Gunpowder"…

— What regiment could it be! — exclaimed Ilyas Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so nicely tickled, but still a bit tipsy. Raskolnikov suddenly felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them. — Do forgive me, Captain, — he began, very casually, suddenly addressing Nikodim Fomich — take my situation into account… I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I've done anything wrong on my part.
