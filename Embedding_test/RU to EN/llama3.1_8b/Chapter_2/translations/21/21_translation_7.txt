He took a deeper breath and said to himself: "Not that, probably!" He gradually began to calm down, trying with all his might to compose himself and regain his wits. "Some foolishness, some tiny carelessness, and I'll be caught for sure! Ah... it's a pity there's no air here - the stuffiness... My head is spinning even more... and my mind too..."

He felt an awful mess inside him. He was afraid of losing control over himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything, but completely unrelated things, but that didn't work out at all. The clerk was very interesting to him: he wanted to guess something from his face, figure it out. This was a young man of twenty-two years old, with a dark and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than his age, dressed in the latest fashion and foppishly, with a part on his head, combed and pomaded, with numerous rings and cuffs on his white-gloved fingers and golden chains on his waistcoat. With one of the former foreigners here, he even spoke two words in French, very satisfactorily. - Louise Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said briefly to a brightly dressed red-haired woman who stood as if she dared not sit down herself, although there was a chair next to her.
