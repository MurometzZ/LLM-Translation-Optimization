He thought triumphantly, taking a deep and joyful breath from his entire chest, "It's just feverish weakness, a temporary delusion," and he pulled out all the lining from the left pocket of his pants. At that moment, a sunbeam illuminated his left boot: on the sock peeking out of the boot, as if signs appeared. He took off the boot: "Indeed, signs! The entire tip of the sock is soaked in blood"; he must have stepped into that puddle carelessly then... "But what now to do with this? Where to put this sock, the fringe, the pocket?" He gathered all this in his hand and stood in the middle of the room. "Into the stove? But first, they'll start digging through the stove. Burn it? And with what? There's not even a match."
