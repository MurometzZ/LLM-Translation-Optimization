— Go ahead quickly and get the tea, Nastasya, because, as for the tea, it seems one can do without the faculty. But here's the beer! — he moved to his chair, brought the soup and meat closer, and started eating with such an appetite as if he hadn't eaten in three days. — Brother Rodia, I now have lunch here every day, — he muttered as much as his mouth full of meat allowed, — and that's all Pashenka, your housekeeper, taking charge, entertaining me wholeheartedly. I, of course, don't insist, nor do I protest. And here's Nastasya with the tea. How quick she is! Nastenka, do you want some beer? — And by the devil! — And a cup of tea?
