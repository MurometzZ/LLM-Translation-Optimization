His head spun and throbbed from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with a trick and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued thinking to himself as he descended the stairs. "The worst thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the stairs, he remembered that he always left everything in the wall cavity—"and here, perhaps, they deliberately searched without it,"—and then stopped. But such despair and a sort of cynical acceptance of death suddenly seized him that he waved his hand and went on. "Just hurry up..."  

Outside, the heat was unbearable again; even a drop of rain would have been welcome during all these days. Again, dust, bricks, and plaster, again the stench from benches and taverns, again drunken peddlers and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun blazed into his eyes so brightly that it hurt to look, and his head spun again—this was the usual sensation of a feverish person suddenly stepping out into a bright sunny day. Reaching the corner leading back to yesterday's street, he looked with agonizing anxiety into it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, I might say something," he thought, approaching the office.
