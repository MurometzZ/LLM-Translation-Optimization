But no, he hears it all too clearly!... But then they will come to him now, if so, "because... yes, everything is the same as yesterday's... God!" He wanted to lock himself in, but his hand didn't rise... and what's the use anyway! Fear, like ice, wrapped itself around his soul, tormented it, petrified it... But finally, after this whole commotion that had been going on for ten minutes, began to subside gradually. The landlady was still wailing and screaming, Ilya Petrovich was still threatening and cursing... But finally, it seems, he calmed down; now you can't even hear him; "has he left! God!" Yes, the landlady is leaving too, still wailing and crying... and her door slammed shut. And the crowd disperses from the stairs to the apartments, exclaiming, arguing, calling out, sometimes raising their voices to a shout, sometimes lowering them to a whisper. There must have been many of them; almost the whole house gathered. "But God, is all this possible! And why did he come here at all!"

Raskolnikov was exhausted and fell onto the divan, but couldn't even close his eyes; he lay there for half an hour in such suffering, in such unbearable feeling of boundless horror that he had never experienced before. Suddenly a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a bowl of soup. Looking at him attentively and noticing that he wasn't sleeping, she put the candle on the table and began to unpack what she brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
