And he opened the window and climbed in, like a squealing pig, squealing; and that's a disgrace. And how can one squeal like a little pig in the window on the street; and that's a disgrace. Ugh-ugh-ugh! And Karl pulled him by the coat from the window, and indeed, Mr. Captain, he broke the rock. And then he shouted that he had to pay fifteen shillings as a fine. And I myself, Mr. Captain, paid five shillings as the fine. And this is an unworthy guest, Mr. Captain, and a scoundrel who causes a scandal! I, saying, will write a big satire about you, because I can write everything about you in all the newspapers. — From writers, then? — Yes, Mr. Captain, and what an unworthy guest, Mr. Captain, when in a noble house… — Nu-nu-nu!
