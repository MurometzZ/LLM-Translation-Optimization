A small depression formed under the stone; he immediately started throwing everything from his pockets into it. The wallet ended up at the top, yet there was still room left in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it back to its original side with one twist, and it landed exactly in its previous place, only slightly, just a bit, seeming higher. He scooped up the soil and pressed it down with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he left and headed to the square. Again, a strong, barely bearable joy, like the time in the office, seized him for a moment. "The ends are hidden! And who would ever think to look under this stone? It's been here since the house was built and will remain here for just as long."
