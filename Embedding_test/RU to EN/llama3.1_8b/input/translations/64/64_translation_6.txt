But Raskolnikov had already stepped out onto the street. On Nikolsky Bridge, he was forced to wake up again completely due to a rather unpleasant incident for him. A coachman from one of the carriages gave him a tight lash on the back with his whip because he almost got under the horses' hooves, despite the fact that the coachman had shouted at him three or four times. The blow from the whip infuriated him so much that he, jumping away to the railing (he was walking in the middle of the bridge, where they ride, not walk), maliciously clicked his teeth and growled. Of course, laughter echoed all around: "And what's the matter?!" - "Some kind of beggar." - "It's known, a drunkard will come up to you on purpose and run under the wheels; and you'll be responsible for him." - "They make their living that way, sir. They make their living that way..." But at that moment, as he stood by the railing and still looked after the departing carriage with a senseless and angry expression, rubbing his back, he suddenly felt that someone was shoving money into his hands.
