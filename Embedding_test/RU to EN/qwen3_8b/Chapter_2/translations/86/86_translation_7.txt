— Go on, hurry up and get the tea, Nastasya, since, I suppose, you don't need a faculty for that. But here comes the beer! — he moved to his chair, brought the soup and meat closer, and started eating with such an appetite as if he hadn't eaten for three days. — Brother Rodya, I've been having such meals here every day now, — he muttered, as much as his meat-filled mouth allowed, — and it's all Pashenka, your housekeeper, who's entertaining me wholeheartedly. I, of course, don't insist, nor do I protest. Here comes Nastasya with tea. What a quick one! Nastya, do you want some beer? — By the devil! — And a cup of tea?
