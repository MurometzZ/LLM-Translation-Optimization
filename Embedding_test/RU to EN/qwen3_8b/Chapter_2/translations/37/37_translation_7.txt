— All these sensitive details, my lord, have nothing to do with us, — Ilya Petrovich cut off rudely, — you must give your testimony and make a commitment; as for all these tragic places and your being in love, it has nothing to do with us. — Well, you… cruelly… — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. Some sort of shame came over him. — Write down, — said the scribe to Rasputin. — What to write? — asked he rather coarsely. — I shall dictate to you. Rasputin felt that the scribe had become more careless and disrespectful toward him after his confession, but, strange thing, — suddenly he himself felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change occurred somehow in an instant, in one minute. If he had only thought a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago, and even insist on expressing his feelings? And where did these feelings come from?
