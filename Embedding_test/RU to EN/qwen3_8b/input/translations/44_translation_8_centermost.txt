— Where to, may I ask? — Along the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered sharply, abruptly, entirely pale like a cloth, not lowering his black bloodshot eyes from Ilya Petrovich's gaze. — He's barely standing on his feet, but you... — Nikodim Fomich was about to remark. — Nothing! — Ilya Petrovich said, particularly emphatically. Nikodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the scribe, who was also intently staring at him, he remained silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
