—Ilya Petrovich!— began the scribe, solicitously, but stopped to wait, since there was no other way to hold back the boiling corporal than by taking his hands, which he knew from his own experience. As for the plump lady, at first she trembled from the thunder and lightning; but strange thing: the more numerous and stronger the curses became, the more amiable her appearance grew, and the more charming her smile became, directed at the furious corporal. She stomped in place and kept bowing continuously, impatiently waiting for her turn to speak, and finally did. —There's no noise or fighting here, Mr. Captain, — she suddenly chattered, as if spilling peas, with a strong German accent, though fluently in Russian, — and no scandal at all, they came drunk, and I'll tell you everything, Mr. Captain, and I'm not to blame… I have a noble home, Mr. Captain, and noble manners, Mr. Captain, and I've always, always wanted no scandal. They came completely drunk and then asked for three bottles again, and then one raised his legs and started playing the piano with his foot, and that's very bad in a noble home, and he broke the piano entirely, and there's absolutely no manner here, and I said so. He took a bottle and started pushing everyone from behind with it. And then I quickly called the steward and Karl came, he took Karl and hit his eyes, and Henriette's eyes too, and hit me five times on the cheek. And that's very indecent in a noble home, Mr. Captain, and I shouted. He opened the window and started screaming like a little pig from the window; and that's a disgrace.
