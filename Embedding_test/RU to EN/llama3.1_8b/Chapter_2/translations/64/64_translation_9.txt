But Raskolnikov had already stepped out onto the street. On Nikolsky Bridge, he was forced to wake up again completely due to one very unpleasant incident for him. A coachman from a carriage tightly whipped him with his whip on the back because he almost got under the horses' hooves, despite the fact that the coachman shouted at him three or four times. The blow of the whip infuriated him so much that he, jumping away to the railing (he was walking in the middle of the bridge, where they ride, not walk), maliciously clicked his teeth and hissed through them. Of course, laughter echoed all around: "And what's the matter?!" - "Some kind of madman." - "Known, a drunkard will come up to you on purpose and run under the wheels; and you're responsible for him." - "They make their living that way, sir, they make their living..." But at that moment, as he stood by the railing and still looked after the departing carriage with a senseless and malicious gaze, rubbing his back, he suddenly felt that someone was shoving money into his hands.
