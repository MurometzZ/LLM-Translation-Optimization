— Please don't shout! — I am not shouting, but speaking very calmly, and it is you who are shouting at me; and I am a student, and I will not allow myself to shout. The assistant was so enraged that for the first moment he could not utter a word, and only some spittle was spitting from his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. — Please be silent! You are in the presence. Don't be a rude fellow, you scoundrel! — And you are also in the presence, — Raskolnikov shouted, — and besides shouting, you are smoking a cigarette, so you are being rude to all of us. — Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
