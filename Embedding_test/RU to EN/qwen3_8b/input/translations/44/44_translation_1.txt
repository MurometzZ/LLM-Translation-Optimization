— Where to, may I ask? — By the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered sharply, abruptly, pale as a cloth, without lowering his black inflamed eyes before Ilya Petrovich's gaze. — He's barely standing on his feet, but you... — Nikiphor Fomich was about to remark. — Nothing! — Ilya Petrovich said in a particularly emphatic tone. Nikiphor Fomich wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the scribe, who was also intensely staring at him, he remained silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
