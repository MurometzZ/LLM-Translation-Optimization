His head was spinning and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with cunning and then knock me out completely," he continued to himself as he stepped onto the staircase. - It's bad that I'm almost delirious... I can tell some silly lie..." On the stairs, he remembered leaving all his belongings scattered around, "and here, perhaps, they're deliberately searching for them without me," he thought and stopped. But such despair and cynicism about death suddenly took hold of him that he waved his hand and continued on. "Just get it over with quickly..."

On the street, the heat was unbearable again; just a single drop of rain in all these days. Again dust, brick, and plaster, again the stench from the shops and drinking dens, again drunkards, Chuvash peddlers, and half-ruined cab drivers. The sun blazed brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely around - a common feeling of someone with a fever suddenly stepping out into the bright sunlight. Having reached the turn onto yesterday's street, he looked at it with tormenting anxiety, at that house... and immediately turned away his eyes. "If they ask, I might say so," he thought as he approached the office.
