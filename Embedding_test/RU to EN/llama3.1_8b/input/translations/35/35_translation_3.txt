Poverty is not a vice, my friend, but anyway! It's well known that he couldn't stand an insult. You must have offended him somehow and you yourself couldn't help it, continued Nikodim Fomich, addressing Raskolnikov with courtesy - but this is pointless: the most non-blasphemous person, I'll tell you, but powder, powder! He blew up, boiled over, burned out - and that's it! And everything passed! And as a result, only gold in his heart! They even nicknamed him "lieutenant-powder"...

- And what another regiment was! exclaimed Ilia Petrovich, very pleased that they had been so pleasantly teased, but still stirring Raskolnikov. Suddenly, Raskolnikov wanted to say something unusually pleasant to all of them. - Ah, forgive me, Captain, he began very freely, addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly, - take an interest in my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I made any mistakes on my part.
