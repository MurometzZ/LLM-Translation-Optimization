"Money? Which money?" thought Raskolnikov, "but... surely, it's not that!" And he trembled with joy. Suddenly, it became terribly, inexpressibly easy for him. Everything fell from his shoulders. "When are you supposed to come, sir?" shouted the ensign, becoming more and more offended without knowing why, "you're supposed to come at nine, but now it's already twelve o'clock!" "I was brought here only a quarter of an hour ago," answered Raskolnikov loudly, from behind, also suddenly and unexpectedly to himself getting angry and even finding some pleasure in it. "That's enough, I came here as a feverish patient." "Don't shout!"
