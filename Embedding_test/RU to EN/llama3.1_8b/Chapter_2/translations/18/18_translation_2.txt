The air was stifling. Sweepers with notebooks under their arms and passersby of both sexes, visitors, were going up and down the stairs. The door to the office was also wide open. He entered and stopped in the entrance hall. Everyone stood there waiting for some men. Here too the air was stifling, and besides, a fresh, still wet paint smell from newly painted rooms made his nose feel sick. After a while he thought it was time to move on again, into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low-ceilinged. A terrible impatience kept drawing him further and further. Nobody noticed him.
