— Poverty is not a sin, my friend, well, what can you do! It's well known, gunpowder, couldn't bear an insult. You, perhaps, have taken offense against him and couldn't restrain yourself—continued Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov— but you're wasting your time: he's the most noble and honorable person, I'll tell you, but gunpowder, gunpowder! He flared up, boiled over, burned out—and that's it! Everything passed away! And in the end, only a heart of gold! And in the regiment they called him: "Ensign Gunpowder"...

— What a splendid regiment! — exclaimed Ilyá Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so nicely tickled, but still buzzing with excitement. Raskolnikov suddenly felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — Please, captain, — he began, very casually, suddenly addressing Nikodim Fomich — take into account my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I've done anything wrong on my part.
