— Don't shout! — I am not shouting, but speaking very calmly, and it is you who are shouting at me; I am a student and will not allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant was so enraged that for the first moment he could not utter a word, and only some droplets were spilling from his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. — You are in the presence, so be quiet! Don't be a gr-r-rude, sir! — "And you are also in the presence," cried Raskolnikov, "and besides shouting, you are smoking a cigarette, so you are being rude to everyone." — Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
