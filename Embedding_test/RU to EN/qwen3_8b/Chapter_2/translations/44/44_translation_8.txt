— Where to, may I ask? — Along the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered sharply, in clipped sentences, pale as a cloth, not lowering his black bloodshot eyes from Ilya Petrovich's gaze. — He's barely standing on his feet, but you... — Nikodim Fomich began to remark. — Nothing! — Ilya Petrovich said, particularly emphatically. Nikodim Fomich had wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the scribe, who was also intently watching him, he remained silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
