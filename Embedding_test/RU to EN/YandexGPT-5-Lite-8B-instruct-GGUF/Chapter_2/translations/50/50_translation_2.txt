 Further, in a recess of the courtyard, the corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some kind of workshop. There must have been some kind of establishment here, coachmaking or locksmithing, or something like that; almost from the very gate, a lot of coal dust could be seen everywhere blackened. “That’s where I would throw it all and leave!” — he suddenly thought. Not noticing anyone in the yard, he stepped through the gate and immediately saw a trough attached to the fence right near the gate (as often happens in such houses where there are many factory workers, artel members, cab drivers, etc.), and above the trough, right there on the fence, was written in chalk the usual joke in such cases: “Stopping a wagon is forbidden here.” Therefore, it’s already good that there’s no suspicion that he came and stopped. “Here I could throw everything all at once into a heap somewhere and leave!”

Looking around once more, he had already stuck his hand in his pocket when suddenly he noticed a large rough stone near the outer wall, between the gate and the trough, where the entire distance was about an arshin wide (about 70 centimeters), adjacent directly to the stone street wall. Beyond this wall was the street, the sidewalk, and you could hear passers-by снующих around, of which there were always many; but no one could see him behind the gate, unless someone came from the street, which, however, was very likely to happen, so he had to hurry. He bent down to the stone, grabbed its top firmly with both hands, gathered all his strength and turned the stone over. Under the stone a small depression formed; immediately he began to throw everything from his pocket into it.
