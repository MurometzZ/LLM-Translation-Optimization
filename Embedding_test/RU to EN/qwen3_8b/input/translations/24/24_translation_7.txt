"Money? What money? — thought Raskolnikov, — but... surely not that!" And he trembled with joy. Suddenly, it became to him immeasurably light, unbearable. Everything fell from his shoulders. — "At what time are you supposed to come, sir?" — shouted the officer, growing increasingly offended, — You're supposed to come at nine, but it's already twelve o'clock now! — "I was brought only a quarter of an hour ago," — answered Raskolnikov loudly from behind, having suddenly and unexpectedly become angry with himself, even finding some pleasure in it. — "That's enough, I came here as a feverish patient." — "Don't shout!"
