But Raskolnikov had already stepped out onto the street. On Nikolsky Bridge he was forced to come to his senses once again due to a rather unpleasant incident for him. A coachman from one of the carriages gave him a tight lash on the back with his whip because he almost got under the horses' hooves, despite the fact that the coachman had yelled at him three or four times. The blow from the whip infuriated him so much that he, jumping away to the railing (he was walking in the middle of the bridge, where vehicles pass, not pedestrians), maliciously clicked his teeth and hissed through them. Of course, laughter echoed all around: "And what's the matter?!" "Some kind of beggar." "It's known that a drunkard will deliberately run under the wheels; and you're responsible for him." "They make their living that way, sir, they make their living..." But at that moment, as he stood by the railing and still looked after the departing carriage with a senseless and angry gaze, rubbing his back, he suddenly felt someone slipping some money into his hands.
