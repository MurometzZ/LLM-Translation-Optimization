The light was on, and he quickly began to inspect himself from head to toe, checking his clothes for any signs of blood. But that couldn't be done: shivering with cold, he started taking off all his clothes again and examining them around him. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and shred, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there seemed to be nothing; only on the place where the pants had torn at the bottom and hung in fringes, the fringe itself still had thick streaks of dried blood. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the fringe. There was probably nothing else now. Suddenly he remembered that his wallet and things he had pulled out from the old woman's trunk were all still in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to take them out yet, let alone hide them! He didn't even think of it now as he inspected his clothes! What is this?
