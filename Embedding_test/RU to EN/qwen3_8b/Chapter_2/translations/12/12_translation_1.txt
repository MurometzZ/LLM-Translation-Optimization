He didn't answer and held the paper in his hands without unfolding it. "Don't get up," Nastasya continued, softening and noticing that he was lowering his legs from the couch. "You're ill, so just stay in bed; it won't burn you. What's in your hands?" He looked and saw that in his right hand he held pieces of fringe, a sock, and scraps from a torn pocket. He slept with them. Later, when thinking about it, he remembered how, half-asleep in the heat, he tightly clutched all of it in his hand and then fell asleep again. "What a mess of rags he's gathered and sleeps with them, as if with a treasure..." And Nastasya laughed in her painfully nervous way. He quickly tucked everything under his overcoat and stared intently at it. Though he could hardly make much sense of the situation at that moment, he felt that people would not treat him kindly when they came to take him.
