His face showed surprise. — What are you? — he shouted, inspecting the incoming companion from head to toe; then fell silent and whistled. — Is it really that bad? You, brother, have outdone our brother, — he added, looking at Raskolnikov's tattered clothes. — Sit down, you must be tired! — and when he collapsed onto the crepe Turkish divan, which was worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly noticed that his guest was ill. — You're seriously ill, you know that? — He started feeling his pulse; Raskolnikov pulled away his hand. — No need, — he said, — I came… that is: I have no lessons… I wanted to… in any case, I don't need lessons at all… — Do you know what?
