— Hurry up and make tea, Nastasya, because, as for tea, it seems one can do without the faculty. But here's the beer! — he moved to his chair, brought the soup and meat closer, and started eating with such an appetite as if he hadn't eaten for three days. — I, brother Rodia, have lunch here every day now, — he muttered, as much as his mouth full of meat allowed, — and that's all Pashenka, your housekeeper, is taking charge, entertaining me wholeheartedly. I, of course, don't insist, nor do I protest. And here's Nastasya with the tea. How quick she is! Nastenka, do you want some beer? — And, by the devil! — A cup of tea?
