Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely ajar. He walked quickly and firmly, though feeling that he was entirely broken, but his mind was with him. He feared the pursuit, feared that within half an hour, within a quarter of an hour, someone would already come out, perhaps with orders to keep an eye on him; therefore, no matter what, he had to hide everything before time ran out. He had to deal with it while there was still some strength left and some reasoning left… Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the ditch, and the ends into the water, and that's that." He had decided this already during the night, in a delirium, in those moments when, as he remembered, he several times tried to get up and go: "As quickly as possible, as quickly as possible, and throw everything away." But throwing everything away proved very difficult. He had been wandering along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, maybe more, and several times looked at the wharves in the ditch where they had met him. But it was impossible to carry out the intention: either the boats were right at the wharves and the laundresses were washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and everywhere people were crowding, and from all sides, from all around, one could see, notice: suspiciously, that a person intentionally stepped off, stopped, and threw something into the water. What if the cases didn't sink but floated?
