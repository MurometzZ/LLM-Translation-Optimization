But the door swung open again at this moment, and Razzhivin, slightly bending because he was tall, entered. "What a sea cabin!" he shouted as he entered, "I always bump my forehead; it's called an apartment too! And you, brother, have you woken up? I just heard from Pashenka." "I've woken up now," Nastasya said. "We've all woken up now," the arrester chimed in again with a smile. "And who are you yourself?" Razzhivin suddenly asked him, turning to him. "You see me here, I am Vrazumikhin; not Razzhivin as everyone calls me, but Vrazumikhin, a student and the son of a nobleman, and he's my friend."
