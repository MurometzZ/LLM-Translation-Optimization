Poverty is not a vice, my friend, but anyway! It's well known that he couldn't bear an insult. You must have offended him somehow and you yourself couldn't help it, continued Nikodim Fomich, addressing Raskolnikov with courtesy - but this is pointless: the most non-blasphemous person, I'll tell you, but powder, powder! He blew up, boiled over, burned out - and that's it! And everything passed! And as a result, only gold in his heart! They even nicknamed him "lieutenant-powder"...

— And what an army was there! — exclaimed Ilia Petrovich, very pleased that they had tickled him so pleasantly, but still stirring Raskolnikov. Suddenly Raskolnikov wanted to say something unusually pleasant to all of them. — Forgive me, Captain, he began quite freely, addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly - take an interest in my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I made any mistakes on my part.
