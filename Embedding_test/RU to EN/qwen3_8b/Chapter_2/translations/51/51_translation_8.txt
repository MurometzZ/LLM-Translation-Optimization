A small depression formed under the stone; immediately he began throwing everything from his pockets into it. The wallet ended up at the very top, yet there was still space left in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it with one twist back to its original side, and it once again fell into its former place, slightly higher, just a little bit. But he pushed the soil down and pressed it with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he went out and headed to the square. Again, a strong, barely bearable joy, like the time in the office, seized him for a moment. "The ends are hidden! And who would ever think to look under this stone? It has been lying here since the house was built and will remain here for just as long."
