Yes, he remembered later that he laughed nervously, quietly, almost inaudibly, for a long time, and was laughing all the time as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto K Boulevard, where two days ago he met that girl, his laughter suddenly stopped. Other thoughts came to him. It seemed to him too that it was terribly disgusting now to pass by that bench on which he sat and thought after the girl left, and it would be terribly hard to meet again that old man to whom he had given a two-grivna tip: "Damn you!" He walked, looking around distractedly and sullenly. All his thoughts were now spinning around one main point - and he himself felt that this was indeed such a main point and that now, precisely now, he was left alone face to face with it - and that this was even for the first time after these two months.
