— No need, — he said, — I came… here's the thing: I have no lessons… I wanted to… in any case, I don't need lessons at all… — Do you know what? You're talking nonsense! — remarked Razumikhin, who had been watching him intently. — No, I'm not raving… — Raskolnikov stood up from the sofa. As he rose to face Razumikhin, he didn't realize he would have to confront him directly. Now, in an instant, he suddenly understood, through experience, that he was least inclined, at this moment, to face anyone in the entire world. All his bile rose within him. He almost choked on his own wrath the moment he stepped over Razumikhin's threshold. — Goodbye! — he said suddenly and went to the door.
