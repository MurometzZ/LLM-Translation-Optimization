— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, — said he to the brightly dressed crimson-red lady, who stood still as if not daring to sit herself, although a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said, softly, with a silk-like whisper, and sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim, like an air balloon, spread around the chair and occupied almost half the room. It carried the scent of fragrances. But the lady, obviously, was timid about taking up almost half the room and the scent that emanated from her, although she smiled timidly and boldly at the same time, but with evident unease. The mourning lady finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very manly and somehow particularly twisting her shoulders with every step, entered the officer, threw his hat with a cockade on the table, and sat in the chair. The plump lady jumped up from her place upon seeing him, and with some special enthusiasm began to sit down; but the officer paid her no slightest attention, and she no longer dared sit down in his presence.
