"Money? Which money? — thought Raskolnikov, — but... surely it's not that!" And he trembled with joy. Suddenly, it became to him terribly, immeasurably easy. Everything fell from his shoulders. — When are you supposed to come, sir? — shouted the officer, increasingly and inexplicably offended, — you are written to come at nine, but now it's already twelve o'clock! — They brought me only a quarter of an hour ago, — answered Raskolnikov loudly from behind, also suddenly and unexpectedly for himself getting angry and even finding some pleasure in it. — And that's enough that I came here as a feverish patient. — Don't shout!
