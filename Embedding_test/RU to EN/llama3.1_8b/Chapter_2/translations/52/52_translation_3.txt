Yes, he remembered later that he laughed nervously, quietly, almost inaudibly, for a long time, and everything was laughing all the time as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto K Boulevard, where he met that girl three days ago, his laughter suddenly stopped. Other thoughts came to him. It seemed to him too that it was terribly unpleasant now to pass by that bench on which he sat and thought after the girl left, and it would be terribly hard to meet again that old man who had given him a two-grosh tip: "Damn you!" He walked, looking around distractedly and angrily. All his thoughts were now spinning around one main point - and he himself felt that this was indeed such a main point and that now, exactly now, he was left alone with it for the first time after these two months.
