— All these sensitive details, my lord, have nothing to do with us, — Ilya Petrovich cut off rudely, — you must give your review and obligation, and as for your being in love and all these tragic places, it has nothing to do with us. — Well, you... are cruel... — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He somehow felt ashamed. — Write down, — said the clerk to Raskolnikov. — What to write? — asked the latter rather rudely. — I'll dictate to you. Raskolnikov felt that the clerk had become more careless and contemptuous toward him after his confession, but strangely enough, — he suddenly felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change occurred somehow in an instant, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago, and even insist on his feelings? And where did these feelings come from?
