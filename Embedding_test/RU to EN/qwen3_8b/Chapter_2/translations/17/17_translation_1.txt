"If they ask, maybe I'll say," he thought, approaching the office. The office was a quarter of a verst away. It had just moved to a new apartment, a new building, to the fourth floor. He had once briefly visited the old apartment, but that was a long time ago. Entering under the gates, he saw a staircase to the right, down which a man was coming with a book in his hand: "a janitor, that means; that means the office is here," and he started going up randomly. He didn't want to ask anyone anything. "I'll go in, kneel down, and tell everything..." he thought, entering the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and full of filth. All the kitchens of all apartments in all four floors opened onto this staircase and stood there almost all day. That's why there was a terrible heat.
