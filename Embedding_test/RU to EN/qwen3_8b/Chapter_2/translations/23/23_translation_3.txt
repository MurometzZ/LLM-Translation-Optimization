The plump lady sprang up from her seat upon seeing him and began to sit down with some special enthusiasm; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared sit in his presence. He was a lieutenant, assistant to the quarter master, with reddish mustaches sticking out horizontally in both directions and with extremely small facial features, nothing, in fact, special except for some impudence. He looked at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with indignation: his clothes were too wretched, and despite all the humiliation, his posture still did not match his attire. Raskolnikov, by carelessness, looked at him too straight and for too long, so that the officer even got offended. "What's the matter with you?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged person didn't flinch from his lightning glance. "They required… by order…" Raskolnikov answered rather awkwardly. "It's about recovering money from them, from the student," hurried the clerk, detaching himself from the papers. "Here you are!" and he threw Raskolnikov the notebook, indicating the place in it, "Read it! 'Money?'"
