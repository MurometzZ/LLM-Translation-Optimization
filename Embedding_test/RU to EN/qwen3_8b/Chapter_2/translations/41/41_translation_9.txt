— But allow me, how did they end up with such a contradiction: they themselves claim they knocked and the door was locked, but three minutes later, when they came with the janitor, it turned out the door was unlocked? — The trick is that the killer was definitely there, locked in; and they would have caught him for sure if not for Koch's mistake, if he hadn't gone himself to fetch the janitor. And he exactly managed to descend the stairs and slip past them somehow during that time. Koch crosses himself with both hands: "If I had stayed there, he would have jumped out and killed me with an axe," he says. A Russian prayer wants to be served, heh-heh... — But no one saw the killer? — Where would you see him? The house is like Noah's ark, — noted the clerk, listening from his place.
