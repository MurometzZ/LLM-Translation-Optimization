What's this? He dashed to take them out and throw them on the table. Having selected everything, even turning out his pockets to make sure there wasn't anything left, he carried all this pile to the corner. There, in the very corner, at the bottom, in one place, the peeling wallpaper was torn: he immediately started stuffing everything into this hole, under the paper: "It's in! Everything out of sight and the wallet too!" — happily thought he, standing up and staring blankly at the corner, at the hole that had become even bigger. Suddenly he shuddered all over with fear: "My God," — he whispered in despair, — "what's happening to me? Is this hidden? Do they hide it like this?" Indeed, he hadn't expected anything but money; he thought there would be only money, so he hadn't prepared a place in advance — "but now, now what am I happy about?" — he thought. — "Do they hide it like this?"
