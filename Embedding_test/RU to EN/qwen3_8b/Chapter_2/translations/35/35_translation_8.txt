— Poverty is not a sin, my friend, well, what can you do! It's known that gunpowder can't bear an insult. You must have offended him somehow and couldn't help it, — continued Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov — but you're wasting your time: the most excellent, I tell you, person, but gunpowder, gunpowder! He flared up, boiled over, burned out — and that's it! Everything passed! And in the end, only a heart of gold! And in the regiment they called him: "sergeant-firebrand"...

— What a regiment! — exclaimed Ilyá Petrovich, very pleased that they had tickled him so nicely, but still buzzing. Raskolnikov suddenly felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — Goodness me, captain, — he began, addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly in a very flippant manner — consider also my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I've erred on my part.
