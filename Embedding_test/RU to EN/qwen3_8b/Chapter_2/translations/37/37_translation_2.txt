— All these sensitive details, my lord, have nothing to do with us, — Ilya Petrovich cut off rudely, — you must give your testimony and make a commitment; as for all these tragic places and your being in love, it has nothing to do with us. — Well, you… cruelly… — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He suddenly felt ashamed. — Write down, — said the scribe to Raskelev. — What to write? — asked the latter rather rudely. — I'll dictate to you. Raskelev felt that the scribe had become more careless and disrespectful toward him after his confession, but strangely enough, — suddenly it became entirely indifferent to him what anyone thought, and this change occurred somehow in an instant, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago, even imposing his feelings on them. And where did these feelings come from?
