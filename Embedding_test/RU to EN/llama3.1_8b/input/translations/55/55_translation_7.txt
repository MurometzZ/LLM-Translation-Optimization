He would have just spat on someone and bitten them if anyone had spoken to him... He stopped suddenly when he came out onto the embankment of the Little Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near a bridge. "This is where he lives in this house," he thought. "What's it to me? I've come here myself! The same old story as before... But very interesting: did I come here or was I just walking and happened to stop by? Doesn't matter; I said I'd go see him the day after tomorrow, so I'll go! As if I couldn't go in now..." He went up to Razumikhin's place on the fifth floor. He was at home, in his little room, and was writing at that moment, and he himself let him in. They hadn't seen each other for four months. Razumikhin sat in a tattered dressing gown, in slippers with one foot bare, disheveled, unshaven, and unwashed. A look of surprise appeared on his face.
