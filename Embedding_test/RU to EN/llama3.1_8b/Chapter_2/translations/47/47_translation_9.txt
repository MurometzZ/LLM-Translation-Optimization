Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, although he felt that he was completely broken, but his consciousness remained with him. He was afraid of being chased, he was afraid that in half an hour, in a quarter of an hour, perhaps, instructions would come out to follow him; therefore, no matter what, it was necessary to bury the ends for the time being. It was necessary to get through while there were still some strength and some reasoning left... Where to go? This had already been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the gutter, and the ends in the water, and that's it." He had made this decision even last night, in a delirium, in those moments when he remembered it several times and was about to get up and go: "as quickly as possible, as quickly as possible, and throw everything out." But throwing everything out turned out to be very difficult. He had been walking along the embankment of Ekaterininsky Canal for half an hour already, or maybe more, and he had looked at the stairs into the canal several times, where they met him. But it was impossible even to think about carrying out his intention: either the rafts stood right next to the stairs and the washerwomen were washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and everywhere people swarmed, and from all sides you could see that a person had deliberately gone down, stopped, and thrown something into the water. And what if the boxes don't sink, but float?
