His head spun and throbbed from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with deceit and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued thinking to himself as he stepped onto the stairs. "The worst thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the stairs, he remembered that he always left everything exactly where it was, in the wall cavity—"and here, perhaps deliberately left it out," he recalled, and stopped. But such despair and this, if one could say so, cynical acceptance of death suddenly took hold of him, and he waved his hand and went on. "Only if it's quick...!"  

Again, unbearable heat stood on the street; not a drop of rain for all these days. Again, dust, bricks, and lime, again the stench from the benches and taverns, again the drunken beggars and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun gleamed brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely—this was the usual sensation of a feverish person stepping out into bright sunlight. Reaching the corner leading to yesterday's street, he looked with a painfully anxious gaze into it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, I might say," he thought, approaching the office.
