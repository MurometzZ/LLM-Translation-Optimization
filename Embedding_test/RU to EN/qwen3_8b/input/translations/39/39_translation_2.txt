The scribe began dictating to him the standard form of a withdrawal in such a case, that is, I cannot pay; I promise then (sometime), I won't leave the city, I won't sell or give away my property, and so on.— You can't write, the pen is slipping from your hand," noted the scribe, looking at Raskolnikov with curiosity.— Are you ill? — Yes… my head is spinning… continue speaking! — Yes, everything; sign. The scribe took the paper and started with the others. Raskolnikov handed over the pen, but instead of standing up and leaving, he placed both elbows on the table and clutched his head with his hands. It felt as if a nail was being driven into his forehead. A strange thought suddenly came to him: to stand up now, approach Nikodim Fomich, and tell him everything from yesterday, every last detail, then go together with them to the apartment and point out the items in the corner, in the hole. The impulse was so strong that he had already risen from his seat to carry it out.
