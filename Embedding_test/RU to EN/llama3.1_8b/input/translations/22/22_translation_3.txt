— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, he said to the hastily dressed red and pink lady who was standing as if she dared not sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her. — Ich danke, I thank you, she said and quietly, with a soft rustling sound, sat down in the chair. Her light blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like an air balloon and occupied almost half the room. It carried a scent of perfume. But the lady was clearly nervous about taking up so much space and that her perfume was wafting through the air, although she smiled timidly and shamelessly together, but with obvious distress. The mourning lady finally finished and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, very bravely and somehow especially turning his shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table and sat in the armchair. The lavish lady jumped out of her seat at the sight of him and with a particular excitement began to squat; but the officer did not pay any attention to her whatsoever, and she was no longer able to sit down next to him.
