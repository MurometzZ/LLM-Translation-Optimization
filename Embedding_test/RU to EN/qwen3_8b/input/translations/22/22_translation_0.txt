— Louise Ivanovna, please sit, — he said briefly to the lady dressed in blood-red, who stood still as if unable to sit herself, although a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 — Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said, and quietly, with a silken whisper, sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim spread around the chair like an air balloon, taking up almost half the room. It carried a scent. But the lady, obviously timid, was afraid of occupying almost half the room and of the scent emanating from her, although she smiled nervously and arrogantly, but with evident anxiety. The widow finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very swaggering and somehow particularly twisting her shoulders with every step, entered the officer, threw his cockade hat on the table, and sat in the chair. The plump lady jumped up from her seat upon seeing him and began to sit down with some special enthusiasm; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared to sit in his presence.
