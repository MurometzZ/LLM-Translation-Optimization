 “— he asked, pointing to the guy. — Look at that, he woke up! — she said. — He woke up, — replied the foreman. Having guessed that he had woken up, the landlady, peeping from the door, immediately shut it and hid. She was always shy and found it hard to bear conversations and explanations; she was about forty years old, and she was stout and fat, dark-browed and black-eyed, kind from her plumpness and laziness; and she was even very pretty in appearance. Shy beyond necessity. — You… who are you? — he continued to question, turning to the foreman himself. But at that moment the door opened wide again, and, bending down a little because he was tall, Razumikhin entered.”
