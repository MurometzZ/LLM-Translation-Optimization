— All these sensitive details, my good sir, are none of our business, — rudely cut in Ilya Petrovich, — you must give your statement and commitment, and as for all these tragic places and your being in love, that's entirely none of our concern. — Well, you're being awfully harsh… — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. Suddenly he felt ashamed. — Write down, — said the scribe to Rasikolnikov. — What to write? — asked the latter especially rudely. — I'll dictate to you. Rasikolnikov felt that the scribe had become more careless and contemptuous toward him after his confession, but strangely enough, — suddenly he himself felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change occurred somehow in an instant, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago, and even insist on expressing his feelings. And where did these feelings come from?
