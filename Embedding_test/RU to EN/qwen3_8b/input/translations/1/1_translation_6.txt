"The third hour already! He sat on the sofa—and then he suddenly remembered everything! All at once, in an instant, he remembered everything! At first, he thought he was going mad. A terrible cold gripped him; but the cold was also from the fever, which had already begun with him in his sleep. Now, however, a sudden chill struck him so strongly that his teeth almost came out, and everything inside him was churning. He opened the door and began to listen: everything in the house was completely asleep. He looked at himself in astonishment and everything around the room, not understanding: how could he, yesterday, entering, not lock the doors on the hook and fling himself onto the sofa, not only not undressing, but even with his hat on? It had slipped down and immediately lay on the floor, near the pillow. 'What would someone think if they came in? That I'm drunk, but...' He rushed to the window. There was enough light, and he quickly began to examine himself, from head to toe, all his clothes: were there any traces?"
