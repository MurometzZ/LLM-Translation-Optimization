Just spit on someone, take a bite out of them, I guess, if someone talked to him...  
He suddenly stopped when he came out onto the Neva Embankment on Vasilyevsky Island, by the bridge. "He lives here, in this house," he thought. "What's this, I came to Razumikhin on my own! It's the same story again, like before... But it's very interesting, indeed: did I come on my own or just walked here? Anyway; I said... three days ago... that I would go to him the next day, well, I'll go! As if I can't even go now..."  
He went up to Razumikhin's fifth floor. He was at home, in his room, and at that moment he was occupied, writing, and opened the door himself. It's been four months since they last saw each other.  
Razumikhin was sitting in his own room in a tattered, threadbare robe, in socks on his bare feet, tousled, unshaven, and unclean. Surprise was expressed on his face.
