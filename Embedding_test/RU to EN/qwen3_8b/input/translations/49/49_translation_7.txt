He became extremely distracted and forgetful and knew it. He had to hurry decisively! He went to the Neva along the B-Street; but on the way, another thought suddenly came to him: "Why go to the Neva? Why into the water? Isn't it better to go somewhere very far, again to the Islands, and there somewhere, in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush—bury all this and the tree, perhaps notice?" Although he felt that he was not able to clearly and soundly discuss everything at this moment, the thought seemed infallible to him. But even reaching the Islands was not destined for him, but another thing happened: as he was leaving the B-Street onto the square, he suddenly saw to the left an entrance to the courtyard, surrounded by completely solid walls. To the right, immediately after entering the gates, a solid, unpainted wall of the neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. To the left, parallel to the solid wall and also immediately from the gates, a wooden fence ran twenty steps into the courtyard, and then made a turn to the left. This was a soundproof, enclosed place where some materials lay. Further, in the recess of the courtyard, a corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
