Just spit on someone, bite them, I guess, if someone talked to him...  
He suddenly stopped when he came out onto the embankment of the Little Neva, on Vasilevsky Island, near the bridge. "Here he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this, I came to Razumikhin on my own! It's the same story again, like before... But it's very interesting, indeed: did I come on my own or just walked and happened to come here? Anyway; I said... three days ago... that I would go to him the next day, well, I'll go! As if I can't even visit him now..."  
He went up to Razumikhin's fifth floor. He was at home, in his room, and at that moment he was occupied, writing, and opened the door himself. It's been four months since they last saw each other. Razumikhin was sitting in his room in a tattered robe, in slippers, disheveled, unshaven, and unclean. His face showed surprise.
