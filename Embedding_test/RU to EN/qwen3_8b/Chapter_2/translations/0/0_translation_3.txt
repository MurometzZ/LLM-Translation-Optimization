So he lay there for a very long time. It happened that he seemed to wake up, and during these moments he noticed that it was already night, yet the thought of getting up never crossed his mind. Finally, he realized it was already daylight. He lay on the couch on his back, still stunned from recent sleep. The sharp, desperate cries from the street reached him, which he had heard every night under his window at three o'clock. These were the cries that woke him now. "Ah! Here come the drunkards from the taverns," he thought, "it's three o'clock," and suddenly jumped up as if someone had pulled him off the couch. "What! It's already three o'clock!" He sat on the couch, and then everything rushed back to him!
