The light was enough, and he quickly began to examine himself, from head to toe, his entire outfit: were there any traces? But that wasn't possible: shivering from a chill, he began to remove everything from himself and look around again. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and scrap, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there was nothing, it seemed, no traces; only on the spot where the pants had been cut off and hung as frayed fringe, there were thick traces of dried blood on the fringe. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the fringe. More, it seemed, nothing else was there. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and items he had taken from the old woman's trunk were still lying in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to take them out and hide them yet! He hadn't even remembered them now, while examining the dress! What was this?
