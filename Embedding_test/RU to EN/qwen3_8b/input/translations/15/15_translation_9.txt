"Put on the sock!" — it suddenly occurred to him, "it will get even more dusty, and the traces will disappear." But just as he put it on, he immediately pulled it off with disgust and horror. He pulled it off, but realizing there was no other option, he took it again and put it on — and laughed again. "All of this is conditional, all is relative, all are just forms," — he thought briefly, with only a fleeting thought, while trembling all over, "here, he put it on! Here, he ended up by putting it on!" Laughter, however, immediately turned into despair. "No, it's not within his strength…" — he thought. His legs were trembling. "From fear," — he muttered to himself. His head was spinning and aching from the heat.
