And where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if suddenly the room were filled not with his quarter's friends, but with his closest friends, then, it seems, he would still have no human word for them, his heart having suddenly grown so empty. A dark sensation of tormenting, endless loneliness and alienation suddenly consciously struck his soul. Not the baseness of his heart's outpourings to Ilyá Petrovich, nor the baseness and the triumph of the poruchik's over him suddenly turned his heart upside down. Oh, what business does he have now with his own baseness, with all these ambitions, poruchiks, Germans, grievances, offices, and so on and so forth! Even if he were sentenced to be burned at this very moment, he would still not move, and perhaps would not even listen attentively to the verdict. Something entirely unfamiliar, new, sudden, and unprecedented was happening to him. Not that he understood it, but he clearly felt, with all the force of his feeling, that not only with his sensitive expansiveness, as before, but even with anything at all, he could no longer address these people in the quarter's office. And even if these were all his own brothers and sisters, and not the quarter's poruchiks, then still he would have had absolutely no need to address them, and even in no case of his life; he had never before experienced such a strange and terrible sensation. And what was most tormenting of all—this was more a feeling than a consciousness, than a concept; an immediate, most tormenting sensation among all the sensations he had ever experienced in his life. The clerk began dictating to him the usual form of such a report, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on.
