— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, he said to the hastily dressed red and pink lady who stood there as if she dared not sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her. — Ich danke, [17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said and quietly, with a soft rustling sound, sat down on the chair. Her light blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like an air balloon and occupied almost half the room. It was carried by perfumes. But the lady, obviously nervous about occupying half the room and being carried away by perfumes, although she smiled timidly and shamelessly together, but with obvious anxiety. The mourning lady finally finished and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, very bravely and somehow especially turning her shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table and sat in the armchair. The puffy lady jumped out of her seat at the sight of him and with a special excitement began to bend down; but the officer did not pay any attention to her, and she was no longer able to sit next to him.
