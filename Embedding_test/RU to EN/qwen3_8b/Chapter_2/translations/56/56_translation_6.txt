His face showed surprise. "What are you?" he shouted, examining the incoming comrade from head to toe; then he paused and whistled. "Is it really that bad? You, brother, have outdone our brother," he added, looking at Raskolnikov's tattered appearance. "Sit down, you must be tired!" And when the latter collapsed onto the Turkish crepe paper divan, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly noticed that his guest was ill. "You're seriously ill, you know that?" he said, starting to feel his pulse; Raskolnikov pulled away his hand. "No need," he said, "I came... that is, I didn't come for any lessons... I wanted to... in any case, I don't need lessons at all..."

"Do you know what?"
