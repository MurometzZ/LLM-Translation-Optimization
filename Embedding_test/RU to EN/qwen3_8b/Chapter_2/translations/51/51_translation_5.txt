A small depression formed under the stone; immediately he began throwing everything from his pockets into it. The wallet landed at the very top, and yet there was still space left in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one twist to its original side, and it just landed in its previous place, except that it seemed slightly higher. But he dug up some soil and pressed down on the edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he left and headed to the square. Again, a strong, barely bearable joy, like the one before in the office, seized him for a moment. "The ends are buried! And who would ever think to look under this stone? It's been lying here since the house was built and will lie there for just as long."
