— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, — said he to the woman dressed in a bright red gown, hastily, who stood as if unable to sit herself, though a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said, and quietly, with a silken whisper, sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim, like an air balloon, spread around the chair and occupied almost half the room. It carried a scent of perfume. But the lady, obviously, felt uneasy about occupying almost half the room and the scent that emanated from her, though she smiled timidly and boldly, yet with evident anxiety. The widow finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very swaggering and somehow particularly twisting his shoulders with each step, entered the officer, threw his hat with a cockade on the table, and sat in the chair. The plump lady jumped from her seat upon seeing him, and with some special enthusiasm began to sit down; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared sit in his presence.
