 She seemed to be about thirty years old and was certainly an odd wife for Marmeladov.... She hadn't heard them and didn't notice them coming in. She appeared to be lost in thought, hearing and seeing nothing. The room was stuffy, but she hadn't opened the window; a stench came up from the stairs, but the door to the stairs wasn't closed. Clouds of tobacco smoke drifted in from the inner rooms, she kept coughing, but didn't close the door. The youngest child, a six-year-old girl, was asleep, curled up on the floor with her head resting on the sofa.
