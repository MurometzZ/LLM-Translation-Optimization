The air was stifling. Sweepers with notebooks under their arms, pedestrians of all kinds, and visitors were going up and down the stairs. The door to the office was also wide open. He entered and stopped in the entrance hall. Everyone stood there waiting for some men. It was just as stifling here, and besides, a fresh, still wet paint smell from newly painted rooms made his nose feel queasy. After a bit, he decided to move forward again, into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low-ceilinged. A terrible impatience drove him further and further on. No one noticed him.
