He breathed more freely. "Probably not!" Gradually, he began to regain his courage, he admonished himself with all his strength to encourage himself and come to his senses. "Any kind of foolishness, any minor carelessness, and I might give myself away entirely! Hmm… it's a pity there's no air here—added he—stifling… My head spins even more… and my mind too…"

He felt a terrible disorder within himself. He himself feared not being able to control himself. He tried to fix his thoughts on something and think about something entirely unrelated, but it didn't work at all. The scribe, however, greatly interested him: he was always eager to guess something from his face, to decipher him. He was a very young man, about twenty-two, with a dark and lively face that seemed older than his years, dressed in the latest fashion and with a fatigued look, with a parting on the back of his head, combed and powdered, with numerous rings and bracelets on his white, brush-cleaned fingers and gold chains on his waistcoat. He even spoke two words in French to one of the former foreigners here, and quite satisfactorily. "Madame Louise Ivanovna, you would sit down," he said to the brightly dressed, flushed red lady who stood still, as if not daring to sit herself, although a chair was nearby.
