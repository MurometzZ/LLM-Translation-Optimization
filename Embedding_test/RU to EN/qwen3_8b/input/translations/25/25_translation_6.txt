— Don't shout! — I am not shouting, but speaking very calmly, and it is you who are shouting at me; I am a student, and I will not permit myself to be shouted at. The assistant was so enraged that for the first moment he could not utter a word, and only some droplets were spilling from his mouth. He jumped up. — Be quiet, please! You are in the presence. Don't be a grub, dear! — And you are also in the presence, — Raskolnikov shouted, — besides shouting, you are smoking a cigarette, so you are treating us all with contempt. — Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
