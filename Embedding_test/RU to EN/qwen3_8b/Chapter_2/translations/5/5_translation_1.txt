"Bits of torn cloth will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" he kept repeating, standing in the middle of the room, and with painful intensity he began to scrutinize everything around him, on the floor and everywhere, wondering if he had forgotten something else? The certainty that everything, even memory, even the simplest thought, had left him began to torment him unbearably. "What, is it really beginning? Is this punishment about to start? There it is, there it is!" Indeed, the frayed edges of the fringe he had cut from his pants lay scattered on the floor, in the middle of the room, to be seen at once! "What is happening to me!" he cried out again, as if lost. At that moment a strange thought came to him: perhaps all his clothes were stained with blood, perhaps there were many stains, but he just didn't see them, didn't notice them, because his thoughts had weakened, crumbled... his mind was clouded. Suddenly he remembered that there had also been blood on his wallet. "Bah! So there must also be blood in the pocket, since I had put the wet wallet into my pocket then!" In an instant he turned out his pocket, and—there it was—there were traces, stains, on the pocket lining! "So, I haven't completely lost my wits, there is still thought and memory, since I have just realized and guessed it myself!" he thought triumphantly, taking a deep, joyful breath from his entire chest, "it's just the weakness of fever, a momentary delirium!" And he pulled out all the lining from the left pocket of his pants.
