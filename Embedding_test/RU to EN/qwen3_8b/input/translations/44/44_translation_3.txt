— Where to, may I ask? — Along the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered sharply, abruptly, entirely pale like a cloth, without lowering his black inflamed eyes before Ilya Petrovich's gaze. — He's barely standing on his feet, but you... — Nikiphor Fomich was about to remark. — Nothing! — Ilya Petrovich said, particularly emphatically. Nikiphor Fomich wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the scribe, who was also very intently looking at him, he remained silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
