His head spun and ached from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me with deceit and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued to himself as he stepped onto the stairs. "The worst thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the stairs, he remembered that he always left all his belongings in the wall hole—"and here, perhaps, they intentionally searched without him," he thought, and stopped. But such despair and such, if one could say so, cynical acceptance of death suddenly seized him that he waved his hand and went on. "Only if it's soon...!"  

Again, unbearable heat stood on the street; not a drop of rain for all these days. Again, dust, bricks, and plaster, again the stench from benches and taverns, again the constant sight of drunk beggars and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun blazed brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely—usual sensation for a feverish person suddenly stepping out into bright sunlight. Reaching the corner of the street from yesterday, he looked with agonizing dread at it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, maybe I'll say," he thought, approaching the office.
