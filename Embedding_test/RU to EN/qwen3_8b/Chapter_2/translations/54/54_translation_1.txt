Not at all!...

Suddenly, he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question immediately confused him and greatly surprised him:

"If this whole thing was indeed done consciously, not foolishly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how come you haven't even looked into the wallet yet and don't know what you've received, for which you've endured all these sufferings and deliberately went through such a base, nasty, low deed? You even wanted to throw it into the water now, the wallet, together with all the things you haven't even seen yet... What's going on?"

Yes, that's true; everything is exactly like that. He, in fact, knew this before, and it's not a new question for him at all; and when it was decided at night to throw it into the water, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if it was only natural, as if it was impossible to be any other way... Yes, he knew everything and remembered everything; perhaps it was already decided yesterday, at the very moment when he was sitting over the chest and taking out the cases from it... That's exactly what happened!... 'It's because I'm very ill,' he finally decided gloomily, 'I've tortured and torn myself apart myself, and I don't even know what I'm doing... I've tormented myself yesterday, three days ago, and all this time... I'll recover and... stop tormenting myself... What if I never recover at all? Lord! I'm sick and tired of everything!' He kept walking without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow relieve himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to take action. One new, irresistible feeling was taking over him more and more, almost with every minute: it was some kind of infinite, almost physical repulsion towards everything he encountered and surrounded him, persistent, spiteful, hateful. Everything he met was repulsive to him—his faces, gait, movements. He just wanted to spit on someone, bite them, perhaps, if someone spoke to him...

He suddenly stopped when he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on Vasilevsky Island, near the bridge.
