And lo, to his greatest astonishment, he suddenly recognized the voice of his mistress. She was screaming, wailing, and lamenting, rushing, hurrying, spilling out words so fast that it was impossible to make sense of what she was saying, pleading—obviously, to stop beating her, because she was being unmercifully beaten on the stairs. The voice of the one hitting her had become so terrifying from hatred and fury that it was barely more than a rasp, yet even he, the one hitting her, was saying something, speaking hurriedly, incoherently, rushing and gasping. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf—he recognized this voice; it was Ilyi Petrovich. Ilyi Petrovich was here, beating the mistress! He was beating her with his feet, striking her head against the steps—this is clear, one can hear it by the sounds, the screams, the blows! What is this, has the world turned upside down, is it possible? One could hear a crowd gathering on all the floors, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, footsteps, doors slamming, people converging. "But why, why, and how could this be!"—he kept repeating, seriously thinking that he had completely lost his mind. But no, he hears it too clearly...
