The clerk began to dictate to him the usual form of such a letter, that is, I am unable to pay, I promise to do so sometime, I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away my property and so on. — You can't write, your pen slips from your hands, — observed the clerk, looking at Raskolnikov with curiosity. — Are you ill? — Yes... My head is spinning... Go on talking! — Yes, everything; sign it. The clerk took back the paper and busied himself with others. Raskolnikov handed over his pen but instead of standing up and leaving, he placed both elbows on the table and clutched his head in his hands. They were hammering a nail into his skull. A strange idea suddenly came to him: stand up now, approach Nikodim Fyodorovich and tell him everything from yesterday, all the details, then go with them to the apartment and point out the things, over there, in the corner, in the hole. The urge was so strong that he had already stood up from his seat to carry it out.
