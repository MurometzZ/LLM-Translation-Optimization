Well, then, who are you? — I'm a partner in our office, from the merchant Shelopayev, and I've come here on business. — Please sit on this chair, — Razumikhin himself sat on the other side of the table. — You did well, brother, to come to your senses, — he continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — You've barely eaten or drunk for four days. Honestly, they gave you tea with a spoon. I brought Zosimov to you twice. Do you remember Zosimov? He examined you carefully and immediately said that it was all nonsense—something like a blow to the head. Nervous nonsense, the ration was poor, they gave little beer and herring, that's why you got sick, but nothing, it will pass and settle down.
