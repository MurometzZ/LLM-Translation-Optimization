He took a deeper breath and said to himself: "Not that, probably!" He gradually began to calm down, trying with all his might to pull himself together and regain his composure. "Some foolishness, some tiny carelessness, and I can be caught off guard! Ah... it's a pity there's no air here - the stuffiness... My head is spinning even more... and my mind too..."

He felt a terrible disorder in everything around him. He was afraid of losing control himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything, but completely unrelated things, but that didn't work out at all. The scribe strongly interested him: he wanted to guess something from his face, figure it out. This was a very young man, twenty-two years old, with a dark and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than his years, dressed in the latest fashion and fat, with a part on the back of his head, combed and pomaded, with many rings and cuffs on his white, polished fingers and golden chains on his vest. With one former foreigner here he even said two words in French, very satisfactorily. - Louise Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said briefly to a brightly dressed red-haired woman who stood there as if not daring to sit down herself, although the chair was next to her.
