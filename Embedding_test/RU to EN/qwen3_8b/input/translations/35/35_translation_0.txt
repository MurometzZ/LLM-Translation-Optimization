— Poverty is not a sin, my friend, well, what can you do! It's known that powder couldn't bear an insult. You, probably, took offense against him and couldn't restrain yourself—continued Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov— but you're wasting your time: he's the most noble person, I'll tell you, but powder, powder! He flared up, boiled over, burned out—and there was none left! And everything passed away! In the end, only a heart of gold! They even called him in the regiment: "Ensign-Powder"...

— What regiment could it be!— exclaimed Ilyá Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so nicely teased, but still somewhat irritable. Raskolnikov suddenly felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — Goodness me, captain— he began, very casually addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly— take note of my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I was at fault on my side.
