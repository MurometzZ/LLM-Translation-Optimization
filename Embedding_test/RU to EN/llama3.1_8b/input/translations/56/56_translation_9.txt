His face expressed amazement. "What's this?" he shouted, examining the entered comrade from head to foot; then fell silent and whistled. "Is it really that bad?" he added, looking at Raskolnikov's tattered clothes. "You've outdone our brother, you know," he said, glancing at Raskolnikov. "Sit down, you must be tired!" And when Raskolnikov collapsed onto the worn-out Turkish divan, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly realized that his guest was ill. "You're seriously ill, don't you know it?" He began to feel Raskolnikov's pulse; Raskolnikov pulled his hand away. "No need," he said, "I came... well, I wanted to... but I don't need any lessons at all..." "Do you know what?"
