And suddenly, to his greatest amazement, he caught the sound of his landlady's voice. She was wailing, screaming and lamenting, rushing, hurrying, uttering words so indistinct that they could hardly be made out, begging for something - of course, for them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the beater had become so terrifying from malice and fury that it was already nothing but a croak, but still the beater too was saying something, and just as quickly, indistinctly, in a hurry and choking. Suddenly Raskolnikov quivered like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice! Ilya Petrovich is here and beating his landlady! He beats her with his feet, knocks her head against the step - that's clear, that can be heard from the sounds, cries, blows! What is this, has the light turned upside down? It could be heard how a crowd gathered in all the apartments, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, rising, knocking, banging on doors, rushing out. "But why, why, and how can it be?" he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he had gone completely mad. But no, he is hearing everything too clearly!
