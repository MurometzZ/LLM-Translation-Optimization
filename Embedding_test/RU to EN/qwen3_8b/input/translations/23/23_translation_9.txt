The plump lady sprang up from her seat upon seeing him and began to sit down with some special enthusiasm; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared sit in his presence. He was a lieutenant, assistant to the quarter master, with reddish-brown mustaches sticking out horizontally in both directions and with extremely small facial features, nothing in fact special except for some impudence. He looked at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with indignation: his clothes were too worn out, and despite all the humiliation, his posture was still not fitting his clothes; Raskolnikov, by carelessness, looked at him too straight and long, so that he even felt offended. — What do you want? — he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged person didn't even think of shrinking back from his lightning glance. — They required… by order… — Raskolnikov answered half-heartedly. — This is about the case of collecting money from them, from the student, — hurried the clerk, detaching from the papers. — Here you are! — and he threw over Raskolnikov the notebook, indicating the place in it, — read it! «Money?»
