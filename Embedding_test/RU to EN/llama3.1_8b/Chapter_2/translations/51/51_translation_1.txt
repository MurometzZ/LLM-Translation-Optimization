A small depression formed under the stone; he immediately began throwing everything from his pocket into it. The wallet landed on top and yet there was still room left in the depression. He then grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one rotation to its original side, and it just fit back into its original place, except that it seemed a bit higher. But he pushed earth towards it and pressed down on the edges with his foot. Nothing could be seen. Then he went out and headed for the square. Again, strong and barely bearable joy took hold of him for an instant, just as it had in the office before. "The ends are buried! And who would even think to look under this stone? It's probably been lying here since the house was built and will lie here for just as long again."
