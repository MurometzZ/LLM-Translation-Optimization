The light was enough, and he quickly began to examine himself, from head to toe, his entire outfit: were there any traces? But that wasn't possible: trembling from chills, he started taking everything off and examining the surroundings again. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and scrap, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there was nothing, it seemed, no traces; only on the spot where the pants were cut off at the bottom and hung as frayed tassels, on the frayed tassels remained thick traces of congealed blood. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the fringe. More, it seemed, there was nothing. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and items he had taken from the old woman's trunk were still lying in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to take them out and hide them yet! He didn't even remember them now, as he examined his attire! What was this?
