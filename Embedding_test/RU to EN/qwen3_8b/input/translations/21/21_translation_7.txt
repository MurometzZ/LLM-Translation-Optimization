He caught his breath more freely. "Probably, not that!" Gradually, he began to cheer himself up, he urged himself with all his strength to cheer himself up and come to his senses. "A certain foolishness, a certain most minor carelessness, and I might give myself away entirely!" Hmm… it's a pity there's no air here—stifling… My head spins even more… and my mind too…" 

He felt a terrible disorder throughout himself. He himself feared not being able to master himself. He tried to latch onto something and think about something completely unrelated, but it didn't work at all. The secretary, however, greatly interested him: he was always eager to guess something from his face, to decipher him. He was a very young man, twenty-two years old, with a dark and lively face, appearing older than his years, dressed in the latest fashion and with a fat, with a parting on the back, combed and powdered, with numerous rings and bracelets on his white, cleaned fingers and gold chains on his vest. He even said two words in French to one of the former foreigners here, and very satisfactorily. — "Luzhina Ivanovna, you would sit," he said to the briefly dressed crimson lady, who stood all the time as if not daring to sit herself, although a chair was nearby.
