—I'll bring it. Raskolnikov looked at everything with deep wonder and a dull, senseless fear. He decided to remain silent and wait: what would happen next? "It seems I'm not dreaming," he thought, "it seems this is really..."  

Two minutes later, Nastasya returned with soup and announced that tea would come soon. With the soup came two spoons, two plates, and the entire set of utensils: salt, pepper, horseradish for beef, and other items that had long since ceased to be used in such an order. The tablecloth was clean. — Nastasya, my dear, you should have Praskovya Pavlovna bring two beer bottles. We'll have a drink. — Well, you little rooster! — muttered Nastasya and went to carry out the order.
