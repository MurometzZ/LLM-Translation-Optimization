He was becoming extremely scattered and forgetful, and he knew it. He had to hurry! He went to the Neva along the B-avenue; but on the way, another thought suddenly came to him: "Why go to the Neva? Why into the water? Would it not be better to go somewhere very far, again to the Islands, and there somewhere, in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush—bury all this and the tree, perhaps, notice?" Although he felt that he was not able to clearly and soundly discuss everything at this moment, the thought seemed infallible to him. But even reaching the Islands was not destined for him; instead, something else happened: as he was leaving the B-avenue onto the square, he suddenly saw a gate to the courtyard on the left, surrounded by completely soundproof walls. To the right, immediately after entering the gates, a soundproof, unpainted wall of the neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. To the left, parallel to the soundproof wall and also close to the gates, ran a wooden fence extending twenty steps into the courtyard, and then made a turn to the left. This was a soundproof, walled-off place where some materials lay. Further, in the depth of the courtyard, a corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
