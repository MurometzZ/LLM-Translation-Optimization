 — Don't bother shouting!
— I'm not shouting, but speaking quite evenly, and it's you who are shouting at me; and I am a student and will not allow myself to be shouted at. The assistant got so angry that for the first minute he couldn't say anything, and only some splashes flew out of his mouth. He jumped up from his seat.
— Be s-s-silent! You are in the presence. Not r-r-raising your voice, sir!
— And you are in the presence, — shouted Raskolnikov, — and besides, while shouting, you smoke a cigarette, therefore you are showing disrespect to all of us. Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an inexpressible pleasure. The clerk looked at them with a smile.
