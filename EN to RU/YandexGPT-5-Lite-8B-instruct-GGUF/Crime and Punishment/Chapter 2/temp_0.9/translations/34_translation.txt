 Since she has become mature, she has read other books of a romantic nature. Recently she read with great interest a book she got through Mr. Lebeziatnikov, Lewes’ Physiology — do you know it? — and even recounted extracts from it to us. That's the whole of her education. 

And now may I address you, respected sir, with a personal question on my own behalf. Do you think that a respectable poor girl can earn much by honest work? She cannot earn more than fifteen farthings a day if she is respectable and has no special talent, even without putting her work down for an instant! Moreover, Ivan Ivanovich Klopstock, the civil counsellor — have you heard of him? — has not paid her to this day for the half-dozen linen shirts she made for him and drove her away roughly, stamping and abusing her under the pretext that the shirt collars were not made according to the pattern and were put in crookedly.
