And suddenly, to his greatest amazement, he caught a glimpse of the voice of his landlady. She was wailing, shrieking and lamenting, hurrying, rushing, uttering words so indistinct that they could not be made out, begging for something - certainly for them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the beater had become so terrible with malice and fury that it was almost a mere growl, but still the beater too was saying something, and just as quickly, incoherently, hurrying and choking. Suddenly Raskolnikov quivered like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice! And Ilya Petrovich is here beating his landlady! He is kicking her with his feet, pounding her head against the stairs - that's clear, that can be heard from the sounds, the screams, the blows! What is happening? Has the light turned upside down? It was audible how a crowd gathered in all the apartments, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, rising, knocking, banging on doors, rushing out. "But why, why, and how can this be?" he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he had gone mad. But no, he is hearing it too clearly!
