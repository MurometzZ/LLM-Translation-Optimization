The plump lady jumped up from her seat the moment she saw him, and with some special enthusiasm began to sit down; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared sit in his presence. He was a sublieutenant, assistant quartermaster, with reddish mustaches sticking out horizontally on both sides and with extremely small facial features, nothing in fact except some impudence. He looked at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with disdain: his costume was too shabby, and despite all his humility, his posture was still not befitting his costume. Raskolnikov, by carelessness, looked at him too straight and for too long, so that the officer even got offended. "What's the matter with you?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged fellow didn't even flinch from his lightning glance. "I was summoned… by order…," Raskolnikov replied rather awkwardly. "It's about the case concerning the recovery of money from them, from the student," hurried the clerk, detaching himself from the papers. "Ah-ha!" and he tossed Raskolnikov the notebook, indicating the place in it,—"Read! 'Money?'
