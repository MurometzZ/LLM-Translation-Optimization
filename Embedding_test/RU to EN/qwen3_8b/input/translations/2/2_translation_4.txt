The light was enough, and he quickly began to examine himself, from head to toe, his entire dress: were there any traces? But that wasn't possible: shivering from chills, he began to remove everything from himself and examine the surroundings again. He turned everything over, down to the last thread and patch, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there was nothing, seemingly, any traces; only on the spot where the pants were cut off at the bottom and hung as frayed edges, on this frayed edge remained thick traces of congealed blood. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the frayed edge. More, it seemed, there was nothing. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and items he had taken from the old woman's trunk were still lying in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to remove and hide them yet! He didn't even remember them now, while examining the dress! What is this?
