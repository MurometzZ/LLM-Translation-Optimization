The light was on and he quickly began to inspect himself from head to toe, checking his clothes for any signs of blood. But it couldn't be done like that: shivering with cold, he started taking off everything and examining it all over again. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and shred, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there seemed to be nothing, no signs at all; only on the part where his pants had torn and hung in fringes, on those fringes remained thick traces of dried blood. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the fringes. There was probably nothing else now. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and things he had pulled out from the old woman's trunk were still in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to take them out yet, let alone hide them! He didn't even think of it now as he inspected his clothes! What is this?
