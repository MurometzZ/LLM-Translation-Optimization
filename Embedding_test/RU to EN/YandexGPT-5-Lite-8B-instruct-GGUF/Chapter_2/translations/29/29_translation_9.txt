 — And you, you rascal and scoundrel, — he shouted all at once with might and main (the mourning lady had already left), — what happened to you last night? Eh? Again shame, a brawl on the whole street. Fighting and drinking again. You dream of being put in a straitjacket! After all, I've told you, I warned you ten times that I wouldn't tolerate it the eleventh time! And you again, again, you rascal and scoundrel! Even Raskolnikov's paper fell out of his hands, and he was looking wildly at the stout lady, who was being so unceremoniously dealt with; but soon, however, he realized what was going on, and immediately this whole story began to please him very much. He listened with pleasure, so much so that he wanted to laugh, laugh, laugh... All his nerves were jumping. — Ilya Petrovich!
