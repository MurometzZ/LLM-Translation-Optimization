And suddenly, to his greatest amazement, he caught the sound of his landlady's voice. She was wailing, screaming and lamenting, rushing, hurrying, uttering words so indistinguishable that they couldn't be made out, begging for something - of course, for them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the beater had become so terrifying from malice and fury that it could only rasp, but still the beater too was saying something, and also soon, indistinguishably, in a hurry and choking. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice! Ilya Petrovich is here and beating his landlady! He's kicking her with his feet, pounding her head against the step - that's clear, that can be heard from the sounds, the screams, the blows! What's going on? Has the light turned upside down? It was audible how a crowd gathered in all the apartments, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, rising, knocking, banging doors, rushing. "But why, why, and how can this be?" he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he had gone completely mad. But no, he's hearing it too clearly!
