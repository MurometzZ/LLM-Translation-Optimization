But no, he hears it all too clearly! But, apparently, they will come to him now, if so, "because... yes, everything is from the same... because of yesterday... God!" He wanted to lock himself in, but his hand didn't rise... and what's the use? Fear, like ice, wrapped itself around his soul, tormented it, petrified it... But finally, this whole commotion, which had been going on for a faithful ten minutes, began to subside. The landlady was wailing and shrieking, Ilya Petrovich was still threatening and cursing... But finally, it seems, he has calmed down; now you can't even hear him; "has he left! God!" Yes, the landlady is leaving too, still wailing and crying... and her door slammed shut. And the crowd disperses from the stairs to the apartments, exclaiming, arguing, calling out to each other, sometimes raising their voices to a shout, sometimes lowering them to a whisper. There must have been many of them; almost the whole house gathered. "But God, is all this possible! And why did he come here at all!"

Raskolnikov was overcome and fell onto the divan, but already couldn't close his eyes; he lay there for half an hour in such suffering, in such unbearable feeling of boundless horror that he had never experienced before. Suddenly a bright light illuminated his room: Nastasya entered with a candle and a bowl of soup. Looking at him attentively and noticing that he wasn't sleeping, she put the candle on the table and began to unpack what she had brought: bread, salt, a plate, a spoon.
