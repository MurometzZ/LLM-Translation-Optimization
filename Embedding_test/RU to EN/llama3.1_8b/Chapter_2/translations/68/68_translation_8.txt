And suddenly, to his greatest astonishment, he caught the sound of his landlady's voice. She was wailing, screaming and lamenting, rushing, hurrying, uttering words so indistinct that they could not be made out, begging for something - certainly for them to stop beating her, because she was being mercilessly beaten on the stairs. The voice of the beater had become so terrifying from malice and fury that it was already only a croak, but still the beater too was saying something, and just as quickly, indistinctly, in a hurry and choking. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilya Petrovich's voice. Ilya Petrovich is here and beating his landlady! He is kicking her with his feet, pounding her head against the step - that is clear, that can be heard from the sounds, the screams, the blows! What is happening? Has the light turned upside down? It could be heard how a crowd was gathering in all the apartments, on the entire staircase, voices were heard, exclamations rose up, people knocked and stamped their feet, slammed doors. "But why, why, how can this be?" he repeated to himself, seriously thinking that he had gone completely mad. But no, he is hearing it too clearly!
