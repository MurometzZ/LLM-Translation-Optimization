"— Is that how they hide? My mind is truly leaving me!" Collapsing in exhaustion, he sat on the sofa, and immediately, an unbearable chill shook him again. Mechanically, he dragged the lying nearby on the chair, his former student's winter coat, warm but already almost in rags, covered himself with it, and sleep and delirium once again overwhelmed him. He fell asleep. No more than five minutes later, he jumped up again and immediately, in a frenzy, ran again to his dress. "How could I have fallen asleep again, when nothing has been done! That's exactly it, that's exactly it: I haven't taken off the necktie under my armpit yet! I forgot, I forgot about such a thing! Such a clue!" He pulled the necktie off and quickly started tearing it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow in the bedding. "Pieces of torn flannel will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!"— he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with a strained, painful attention, he began to look around again, on the floor and everywhere, not having forgotten anything else?
