— Poverty is not a sin, my friend, but well, what can you do! It's known that Gunpowder couldn't bear an insult. You, I suppose, must have offended him somehow and couldn't help it—continued Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov— but you're wasting your time: he's the most noble, I'll tell you, person, but Gunpowder, Gunpowder! He got angry, boiled over, burned out—and there was none left! Everything passed! And in the end, only a heart of gold! In the regiment, they called him "Ensign Gunpowder"…

— What regiment could it be! — exclaimed Ily Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so nicely tickled, but still a bit irritable. Raskolnikov suddenly felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them. — Oh, please, captain, — he began, very casually, suddenly addressing Nikodim Fomich, — take my situation into account… I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I've done anything wrong on my part.
