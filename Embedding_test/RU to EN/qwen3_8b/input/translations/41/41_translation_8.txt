— But allow me, how could there be such a contradiction? They themselves claim they knocked and the door was locked, yet three minutes later, when the janitor arrived, it turned out the door was unlocked? — The trick is that the murderer was definitely sitting there and locked himself in; he would have been caught there for sure if it weren't for Koch's foolishness, if he hadn't gone off on his own to fetch the janitor. And he precisely managed to descend the stairs during that interval and slipped past them somehow. Koch crosses himself with both hands: "If I had stayed there, he would have jumped out and killed me with an axe." Hehe!
