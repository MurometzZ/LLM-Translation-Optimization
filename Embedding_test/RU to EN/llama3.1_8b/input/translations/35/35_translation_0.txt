Poverty is not a vice, my friend, but anyway! It's well known that he couldn't stand an insult. You probably took offense at him and didn't hold back yourself, continued Nikodim Fomich, addressing Raskolnikov with courtesy - but you're wrong to do so: he's the most noble of people, I'm telling you, but a powder keg! He exploded, boiled over, burned out – and that's it! And as a result, only gold in his heart remains. They even nicknamed him "lieutenant-powder"...

And what a regiment was that! exclaimed Ilia Petrovich, very pleased that they had been so pleasantly teased, but still half-asleep. Raskolnikov suddenly wanted to say something unusually pleasant to all of them. - Forgive me, Captain, he began quite freely, addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly, - take an interest in my situation... I'm even willing to ask for their apologies if I've made any mistakes on my part.
