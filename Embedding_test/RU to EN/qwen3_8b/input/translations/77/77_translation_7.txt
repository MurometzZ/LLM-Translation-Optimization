Well, then, who are you? — I'm a partner in our office, from the merchant Shelopayev, and I've come here on business. — Please sit on this chair, — Razumikhin himself sat on the other side of the table. — You did well, brother, to come to your senses, — he continued, addressing Raskolnikov. — You've barely eaten or drunk for four days. They even gave you tea with a spoon. I brought Zosimov to you twice. Do you remember Zosimov? He examined you thoroughly and immediately said it was all nonsense—something like a blow to the head. Nervous nonsense, he said, the ration was poor, they gave little beer and sausage, that's why you were ill, but nothing, it will pass and settle down.
