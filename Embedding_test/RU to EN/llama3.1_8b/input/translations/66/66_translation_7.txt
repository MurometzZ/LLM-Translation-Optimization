The pain from the whip had subsided, and Raskolnikov forgot about the blow; one anxious and not quite clear thought now occupied him exclusively. He stood and looked far off for a long time, intently; this place was especially familiar to him. When he went to university, it often happened that, returning home, he would stop exactly at this same spot, gaze intently at this indeed magnificent panorama, and each time almost be amazed by one unclear and insoluble impression of his own. An inexplicable chill always blew on him from this magnificent panorama; the spirit was mute and deaf to him in this lush picture... He marveled every time at his gloomy and enigmatic impression and put off solving it, not trusting himself, for the future. Suddenly he remembered now all these former questions and perplexities of his own, and it seemed to him that he hadn't recalled them by chance. One thing struck him as strange and wonderful: that he had stopped in exactly the same place as before, as if he really imagined that he could think about the very same things now as before, and be interested in the very same old themes and pictures as he was... just so recently. It even seemed a little funny to him, and at the same time it squeezed his chest with pain. In some depth, below, somewhere barely visible underfoot, everything from his past seemed to appear to him now: all those former thoughts, problems, topics, impressions, and this entire panorama, and he himself, and everything... It seemed that he was flying upwards somewhere, and everything disappeared from his eyes... Having made one involuntary movement with his hand, he suddenly felt a double kopeck coin clenched in his fist. He opened his hand, looked intently at the coin, swung it back and forth, and then threw it into the water; after that, he turned around and went home. It seemed to him as if he had cut himself off from everything and everyone with scissors just now.
