His head was spinning and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with cunning and then knock me out completely," he thought to himself as he stepped onto the staircase. "It's terrible that I'm almost delirious... I can make up some nonsense..." On the stairs, he remembered leaving all his belongings scattered about - "and now they'll probably search for them without me," he thought, and stopped. But a sudden despair and cynicism took hold of him, making him wave his hand in dismissal and continue on. "Just get it over with quickly..."

On the street, the heat was unbearable again; not even a single drop of rain had fallen in all these days. Again there was dust, brick, and plaster, again the stench from the shops and bars, again drunkards, Chuvash peddlers, and half-ruined cab drivers. The sun flashed brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely around - a familiar feeling of feverish dizziness that comes when suddenly stepping out into bright sunlight. Having reached the turn onto yesterday's street, he looked anxiously down it, at that house... and immediately turned away his eyes. "If they ask, I might just say so," he thought as he approached the office.
