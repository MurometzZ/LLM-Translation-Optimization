— Poverty is not a sin, my friend, well, what can you do! It's known that gunpowder can't bear an insult. You must have taken offense against him and couldn't help it, — continued Nikodim Fomich, politely addressing Raskolnikov — but you're wasting your time: he's the most noble, I'll tell you, person, but gunpowder, gunpowder! He flared up, boiled over, burned out — and that's it! And everything passed! In the end, only a heart of gold! They even called him in the regiment: "Sergeant-Major Gunpowder"...

— What a regiment indeed! — exclaimed Ilyia Petrovich, very pleased that he had been so nicely tickled, though still buzzing. Suddenly Raskolnikov felt like saying something unusually pleasant to them all. — Well, please, captain, — he began, very casually turning to Nikodim Fomich — take a closer look at my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I've done anything wrong on my part.
