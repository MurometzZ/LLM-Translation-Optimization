Not at all!...

Suddenly he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question immediately threw him off and greatly surprised him:

"If indeed all this was done intentionally, not by accident, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how come you haven't even looked into the wallet yet and don't know what you've received, for which you've endured all these torments and deliberately went through such a mean, nasty, low act? Indeed, you wanted to throw it into the water now, the wallet, along with all the things you haven't even seen yet… What's the deal here?"

Yes, that's right; everything is like that. He, however, had known this before, and it was by no means a new question for him; and when it was decided at night to throw it into the water, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if it was only natural, as if there was no other way it could be… Yes, he knew all of this and remembered everything; indeed, perhaps it was already decided yesterday, at the very moment when he was sitting over the chest and was taking out the cases from it… But that's exactly it!.. "It's because I'm very ill," he finally decided gloomily, "I've tortured and torn myself apart, and I don't even know what I'm doing… And yesterday, and the day before, and all this time I've tormented myself… I'll recover and… stop tormenting myself… What if I never recover at all? Lord! I'm tired of all this!.." He kept walking without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow relieve himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to take action. A single new, irresistible feeling was taking over him more and more almost with every minute: it was some kind of endless, almost physical revulsion towards everything he encountered and the surrounding environment, persistent, spiteful, hateful. He found everyone he met repulsive—repulsive were their faces, their gait, their movements. He would just spit on someone, perhaps bite them, if someone spoke to him…

He suddenly stopped when he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on the Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge.
