And he was opening the window onto the canal and sticking his head out like a little pig squealing; and that's shameful. And how can you stick your head out of the window on the street like a little pig squealing; and that's shameful. Fie upon it, fie upon it, fie! And Karl behind him was pulling him by the tailcoat from the window, and then, this is true, Mr Captain, he tore his jacket. And then he shouted that he had to pay fifteen whole marks in fines. And I myself, Mr Captain, paid five whole marks for his torn jacket. And that's a dishonorable guest, Mr Captain, and he causes all sorts of trouble! I'm saying, Mr Captain, you'll have big satire on me because I can write about you in all the newspapers. — From writers, then? — Yes, Mr Captain, and what kind of dishonorable guest is that, Mr Captain, when in a noble house... — Ah-ah-ah!
