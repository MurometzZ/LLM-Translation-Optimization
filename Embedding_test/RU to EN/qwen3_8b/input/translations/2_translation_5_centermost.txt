The light was enough, and he quickly began to examine himself, from head to toe, his entire outfit: were there any traces? But that wasn't possible: trembling from chills, he began to remove everything from himself and examine around again. He turned everything over, down to the last thread and scrap, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there was nothing, it seemed, no traces; only on the spot where the pants were cut off at the bottom and hung as frayed fringe, there remained thick traces of congealed blood on the fringe. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the fringe. More, it seemed, there was nothing. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and items he had taken from the old woman's trunk were still lying in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to take them out and hide them yet! He hadn't even remembered them now, as he examined the dress! What is this?
