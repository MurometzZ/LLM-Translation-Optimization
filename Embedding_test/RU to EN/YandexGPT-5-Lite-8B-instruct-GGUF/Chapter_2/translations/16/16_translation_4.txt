 His head was dizzy and aching from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with a trick and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued to himself as he went out onto the stairs. "The bad thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might blurt out some stupidity..."

On the stairs, he remembered that he was leaving all his things like that, in the wallpaper hole — "and they probably deliberately searched without him," he recalled and stopped. But such despair and such, if I can say, cynicism of doom suddenly seized him that he waved his hand and went on. "If only it would be quick!.."

Outside, the heat was unbearable again; there wouldn't have been a drop of rain in all those days. The sun shone brightly into his eyes, so much so that it hurt to look and his head became completely dizzy — an ordinary sensation for a feverish person stepping out onto the street on a bright sunny day. Having reached the turn to yesterday's street, he looked at it with excruciating anxiety, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, perhaps I will say," he thought, approaching the office.
