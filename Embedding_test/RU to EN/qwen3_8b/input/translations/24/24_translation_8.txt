"Money? What money? — thought Raskolnikov, — but… surely it's not that!" And he trembled with joy. Suddenly, he felt an unimaginable ease, everything fell from his shoulders. — "At what hour are you to come, sir?" — shouted the ensign, growing more and more offended without knowing why, — they wrote you at nine, and now it's already twelve! — "They brought me a quarter of an hour ago," — answered Raskolnikov loudly from behind, also suddenly and unexpectedly angry with himself, even finding some pleasure in it. — "It's enough that I came here feverish and ill." — "Don't shout!"
