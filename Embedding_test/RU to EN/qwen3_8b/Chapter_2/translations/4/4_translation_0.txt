"—Is that how they hide it? My mind is leaving me!" Collapsing in exhaustion, he sat on the sofa, and immediately an unbearable chill shook him again. Mechanically, he dragged the lying nearby on the chair, his former student winter coat, warm but already nearly in rags, covered himself with it, and sleep and delirium once more overwhelmed him. He fell into oblivion. No more than five minutes later, he jumped up again and immediately, in a frenzy, rushed back to his dress. "How could I fall asleep again, when nothing has been done! Exactly so, exactly so: the necktie under the armpit is still not removed! I forgot, I forgot about such a thing! Such a clue!" He pulled the necktie and quickly began tearing it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow in his underwear. "Pieces of torn linen will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with a strained, painful attention, he began once again to scan around, on the floor and everywhere, to make sure he hadn't forgotten anything else?
