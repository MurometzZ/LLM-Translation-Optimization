And lo, to his greatest astonishment, he suddenly caught his mistress's voice. She was screaming, wailing, and sobbing, rushing and hurrying, spewing words so fast that it was impossible to make out what she was saying, pleading—obviously, to stop beating her, because she was being brutally beaten on the stairs. The voice of the attacker had become so terrifying from hatred and fury that it was just a rasp, yet the attacker was also speaking incoherently, rushing, struggling to get words out. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilyi Petrovich's voice. Ilyi Petrovich is here, beating his mistress! He is kicking her, hitting her head against the steps—this is clear, one can hear it by the sounds, the screams, the blows! What is this, has the world turned upside down? One could hear people gathering on all floors, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, doors slamming, people converging. "But why, why, and how can this be!"—he kept repeating, seriously thinking that he had completely lost his mind. But no, he was too clearly hearing it!..
