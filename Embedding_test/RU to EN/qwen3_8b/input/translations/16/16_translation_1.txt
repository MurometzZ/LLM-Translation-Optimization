His head spun and ached from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me with deceit and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued thinking to himself as he stepped onto the stairs. "The worst thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the stairs, he remembered that he always left everything in the wall's hollow—"and here, perhaps deliberately, they'll search without it,"—and he stopped. But such despair and, if one could say so, cynical acceptance of death suddenly seized him that he waved his hand and went on. "Only if it's soon...!"  

Again, unbearable heat stood on the street; not a drop of rain for all these days. Again, dust, bricks, and lime, again the stench from benches and taverns, again the drunken beggars and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun blazed brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun again—this usual sensation of a feverish person suddenly stepping out into bright sunlight. Reaching the corner of the street from yesterday, he looked with agonizing anxiety at it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, maybe I'll say," he thought, approaching the office.
