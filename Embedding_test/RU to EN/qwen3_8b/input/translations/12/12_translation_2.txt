He didn't answer and held the paper in his hands without unfolding it. — Don't get up, — Nastasya continued, softening and seeing that he was putting his legs down from the sofa. — He's ill, so just stay in bed; it won't catch fire. What's in your hands? He looked: in his right hand were pieces of fringe, a sock, and scraps from a torn pocket. He slept with them. Later, thinking about it, he remembered how, half-asleep in the heat, he tightly clutched all of it in his hand and then fell asleep again. — Goodness, what a mess he's gathered and sleeps with it as if it were a treasure… — And Nastasya laughed her painfully nervous laugh. He quickly put everything under his overcoat and stared intently at it. Though he could hardly make much sense of the situation at that moment, he felt that people would not treat him kindly when they came to take him.
