A small depression formed under the stone; he immediately began throwing everything from his pocket into it. The wallet landed on top and yet there was still room in the depression. He then grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one rotation to its original side, and it just fit back into its original place, slightly higher than before. But he pushed earth towards it and pressed down with his foot around the edges. Nothing was noticeable. Then he left and headed for the square. Again a strong, barely bearable joy, as yesterday in the office, took hold of him for an instant. "The ends are buried! And who could think to look under this stone? It's probably been lying here since the house was built and will lie here for just as long again."
