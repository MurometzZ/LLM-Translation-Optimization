And lo, to his greatest astonishment, he suddenly caught his mistress's voice. She was screaming, wailing, and sobbing, rushing and hurrying, spewing words so fast that they couldn't be understood, pleading about something—obviously, to stop beating her, because she was being brutally beaten on the stairs. The voice of the attacker had become so terrifying from hatred and fury that it was just a rasp, but nevertheless, the attacker was also speaking incoherently, rushing, struggling to get words out. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf: he recognized this voice; it was Ilyi Petrovich's voice. Ilyi Petrovich is here, beating the mistress! He is hitting her with his feet, knocking her head against the steps—this is clear, you can hear it by the sounds, the screams, the blows! What is this, has the world turned upside down? You could hear a crowd gathering on all floors of the staircase, voices, exclamations, doors slamming, people converging. "But why, why, and how can this be!"—he kept repeating, seriously thinking that he had completely lost his mind. But no, he was too clearly hearing it...
