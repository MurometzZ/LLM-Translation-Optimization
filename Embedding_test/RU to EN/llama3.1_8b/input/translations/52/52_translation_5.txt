Yes, he remembered later that he laughed nervously, quietly, almost inaudibly, for a long time, and everything was laughing all the time as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto K Boulevard, where he met that girl three days ago, his laughter suddenly stopped. Other thoughts crept into his head. It seemed to him too that it was terribly repulsive to pass by that bench now, on which he sat and thought after the girl left, and it would be terribly hard to meet that old man again who he had given two grivnas to: "Damn you!" He walked, looking around distractedly and sullenly. All his thoughts were now spinning around one main point - and he himself felt that this was indeed a main point and that there was also the fact that now, exactly now, he was left alone with this main point for the first time after these two months.
