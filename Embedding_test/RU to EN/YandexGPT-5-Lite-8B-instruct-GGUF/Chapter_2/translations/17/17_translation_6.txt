 “If they ask, I might say,” he thought, approaching the office. The office was a quarter of a verst away from him. It had just moved to a new address, a new building, on the fourth floor. He had been there briefly once, but it was a long time ago at his previous apartment. Having entered through the gate, he saw a staircase to the right, up which a peasant was going down with a book in his hands: “the janitor, so this must be the office,” and he began to go upstairs by guess. He didn't want to ask anyone anything. “I will go in, kneel down and tell them everything...” he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep and covered in slops. All the kitchens of all the apartments on all four floors opened onto this staircase and remained there almost all day long. That's why it was terribly stuffy.
