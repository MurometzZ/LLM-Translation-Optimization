Put on the sock! — he suddenly thought, — it will get even more dusty, and the traces will disappear." But just as he put it on, he immediately pulled it off with disgust and fear. He pulled it off, but realizing there was no other option, he put it on again — and laughed once more. "Everything is conditional, everything is relative, all of this is just forms," — he thought briefly, with only a fleeting thought, while trembling all over, — "after all, he did put it on! After all, he ended up putting it on!" Laughter, however, immediately gave way to despair. "No, it's beyond my strength..." — he thought. His legs trembled. "From fear," — he muttered to himself. His head spun and ached from the heat.
