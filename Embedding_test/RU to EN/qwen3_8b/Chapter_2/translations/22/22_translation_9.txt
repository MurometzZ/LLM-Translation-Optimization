— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, — said he to the woman in a hastily dressed bright red dress, who stood still as if not daring to sit herself, although a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 — Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said, and quietly, with a silken sound, sat down on the chair. Her light blue dress with white lace trim spread around the chair like a balloon, taking up almost half the room. It carried the scent. But the lady, obviously, felt timid about occupying almost half the room and the scent emanating from her, although she smiled nervously and arrogantly, yet clearly with anxiety. The mourning lady finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very confidently and in a particularly manner, turning his shoulders with each step, entered the officer, threw his hat with a cockade on the table, and sat in the chair. The plump lady jumped up from her seat upon seeing him, and with some particular enthusiasm began to sit down; but the officer paid her no attention at all, and she no longer dared sit down in his presence.
