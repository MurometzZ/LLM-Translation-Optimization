Where did these feelings come from? On the contrary, now, if the room were suddenly filled not with his quarter comrades, but with his closest friends, then, it seems, he would still have no human word to say to them, his heart having suddenly grown empty. A dark sense of tormenting, endless loneliness and alienation suddenly consciously struck his soul. Not the baseness of his heart's confessions to Ilyusha Petrovich, not the baseness and the lieutenant's triumph over him suddenly turned his heart upside down. Oh, what business does he have now with his own baseness, with all these ambitions, lieutenants, Germans, grievances, offices, and so on and so forth! Even if he were sentenced to be burned at this very moment, he would still not move, and perhaps would not even listen attentively to the verdict. Something completely unfamiliar, new, sudden, and unprecedented was happening to him. Not that he understood it, but he clearly felt, with all the force of his feeling, that he could no longer address these people, whether they were quarter comrades or even if they were all his own brothers and sisters, and not quarter lieutenants; then, even in that case, he would have had absolutely no need to address them, and never, in any case of his life; he had never before experienced such a strange and terrible feeling. And what was most tormenting of all—this was more a feeling than a consciousness, than a concept; a direct, most tormenting feeling among all the feelings he had ever experienced in his life. The clerk began dictating to him the usual form of a statement in such a case, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then (sometime), I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on.
