He would have just spat on someone and bitten them if anyone had spoken to him...

He stopped suddenly when he came out onto the embankment of the Little Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near a bridge. "That's where he lives, in that house," he thought. "What is it, but I've come here myself! The same old story as before... But very interesting: did I come here or was I just walking and happened to stop by? Doesn't matter; I said three days ago that I would go see him the next day, so I'll go now! As if I couldn't go in now..."

He went up to Razumikhin's fifth floor. He was at home, in his little room, and was writing at this moment, and he himself let him in. They hadn't seen each other for four months. Razumikhin sat in his tattered dressing gown, in slippers on one foot, disheveled, unshaven, and unwashed. A look of surprise appeared on his face.
