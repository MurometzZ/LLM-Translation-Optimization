The plump lady jumped up from her seat at the sight of him and with some special excitement began to squat; but the officer paid no attention to her whatsoever, and she was no longer able to sit next to him. This was a lieutenant, an assistant to the quarter-master sergeant, with horizontally protruding reddish sideburns and extremely small facial features that expressed nothing in particular except for a certain impertinence. He glanced at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with indignation: his clothes were too shabby, and despite all the humiliation, he still didn't have the right posture; Raskolnikov, through carelessness, looked at him straight on for too long, so that the other even took offense. — What do you want? — he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragamuffin didn't even think to bow down from his lightning-fast glance. — They demanded... by summons... — Raskolnikov replied somehow awkwardly. — This is about collecting money from them, from the student, — the clerk hastened, tearing himself away from the papers. — Here! — and he tossed a notebook at Raskolnikov, pointing to a place in it, — read!
