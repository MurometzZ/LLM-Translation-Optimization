— You, so peculiar and unconventional!— he suddenly shouted at the top of his lungs (the widow lady had already left)— what happened last night? Ah? Another disgrace, another commotion on the whole street! Another fight and drunkenness. You're dreaming of peace! I've already told you, I've warned you ten times that I won't let you go out at eleven! And you again, again, you, so peculiar and unconventional! Even the paper slipped from Raskolnikov's hands, and he stared wildly at the wealthy lady being so rudely treated; but soon, however, he realized what was going on, and immediately this whole affair began to please him very much. He listened with pleasure, so much so that he longed to laugh, laugh, laugh… All his nerves were jumping.— Ilya Petrovich!
