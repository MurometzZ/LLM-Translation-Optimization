His head spun and ached from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me with deceit and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued to himself as he stepped onto the stairs. "The worst thing is that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the stairs, he remembered that he always left everything in the wall cavity, "—and here, perhaps deliberately without it, they'll conduct a search," he thought, and stopped. But such despair and this almost cynical acceptance of his impending death suddenly seized him, and he waved his hand and went on. "Only if it's quick...!"  

Again, unbearable heat stood on the street; not a drop of rain for all these days. Again, dust, bricks, and lime, again the stench from the benches and taverns, again the constant presence of drunk vendors and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun blazed brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun again—this usual sensation of a feverish person suddenly stepping out into a bright sunny day. Reaching the corner of the street from yesterday, he looked with agonizing anxiety into it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, maybe I'll say," he thought, approaching the office.
