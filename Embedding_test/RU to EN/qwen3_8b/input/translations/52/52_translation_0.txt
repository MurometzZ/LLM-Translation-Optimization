He was lying here, perhaps since the house was built and would lie there for just as long. And even if they did find him— who would think about me? Everything is over! No evidence!"—and he laughed. Yes, he remembered later that he had laughed with a nervous, small, inaudible, prolonged laugh, and kept laughing all the time, as he passed through the square. But when he stepped onto the Kth boulevard, where he had met that girl the day before, his laughter suddenly ceased. Other thoughts came into his head. Suddenly it seemed to him that it was terribly repulsive to pass by that bench on which he had sat after the girl left, pondering, and it would also be terribly hard to meet again that bearded man to whom he had given a kopeck: "By God!" He walked, looking around distractedly and angrily. All his thoughts now revolved around a certain main point—and he himself felt that this was indeed such a main point and that now, precisely now, he was left alone with this main point—and that this was even the first time after these two months. "By God, that's it!"
