And lo, to his greatest astonishment, he suddenly caught his mistress's voice. She was screaming, wailing, and sobbing, rushing and hurrying, spewing words so fast that they couldn't be deciphered, pleading—obviously, to stop beating her, because she was being brutally beaten on the stairs. The voice of the one hitting her had become so terrifying from hatred and fury that it was now just a rasp, but the hitter was also speaking incoherently, rushing, struggling to get words out. Suddenly Raskolnikov trembled like a leaf—he recognized this voice; it was Ilyi Petrovich. Ilyi Petrovich was here, beating his mistress! He was hitting her with his feet, knocking her head against the steps—clearly, this is audible from the sounds, the screams, the blows! What is this? Has the world turned upside down? One could hear crowds gathering on all floors, along the entire staircase, voices, exclamations, doors slamming, people converging. "But why, why, and how can this be!"—he kept repeating, seriously thinking he had gone mad. But no, he was too clearly hearing it...
