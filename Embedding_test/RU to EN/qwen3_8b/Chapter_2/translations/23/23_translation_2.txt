The plump lady leaped up from her place upon seeing him and began to sit down with some special enthusiasm; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared to sit down in his presence. He was a lieutenant, assistant to the quarter master, with reddish mustaches sticking out horizontally on both sides and extremely small facial features, nothing, however, except some impudence, expressed. He looked at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with indignation: his costume was too worn out, and despite all the lowering, his posture was still not befitting his attire; Raskolnikov, by carelessness, looked at him too straight and long, so that he even got offended. "What do you want?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged person didn't even think to hide from his lightning glance. "Required... by order..." answered Raskolnikov somewhat awkwardly. "This is about the case of collecting money from them, from the student," hurried the clerk, detaching himself from the papers. "Here it is!" and he threw Raskolnikov a notebook, indicating the place in it, "Read it! 'Money?'"
