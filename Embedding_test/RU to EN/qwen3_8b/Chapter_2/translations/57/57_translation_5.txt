— No need, — he said, — I came… here's the thing: I have no lessons… I wanted to… in any case, I don't need lessons at all… — Do you know what? You're talking nonsense! — remarked Razumikhin, who was watching him closely. — No, I'm not raving… — Raskolnikov stood up from the sofa. As he rose to approach Razumikhin, he didn't realize that he was supposed to face him directly. Now, in an instant, he understood, through experience, that at this moment he was least inclined to face anyone in the whole world. All his bitterness rose within him. He almost choked on his own anger at himself the moment he stepped over Razumikhin's threshold. — Goodbye! — he said suddenly and went to the door.
