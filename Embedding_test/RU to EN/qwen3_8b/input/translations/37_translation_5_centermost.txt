— All these sensitive details, my good sir, have nothing to do with us, — Ilia Petrovich cut in rudely, — you must give your statement and obligation, and as for all these tragic places where you happened to be in love, it's entirely irrelevant to us. — Well, you're being cruel, — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He suddenly felt ashamed. — Write down, — said the scribe to Rasikolnikov. — What to write? — asked the latter rather rudely. — I'll dictate to you. Rasikolnikov felt that the scribe had become more careless and disrespectful toward him after his confession, but strangely enough, — he suddenly felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change occurred in an instant, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago, and even insist on expressing his feelings? And where did these feelings come from?
