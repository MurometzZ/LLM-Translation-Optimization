What's this? He quickly began to pull everything out and throw it onto the table. Having gone through everything, even turning his pockets inside out to make sure nothing was left, he carried all this pile to the corner. There, in the very corner, at the bottom, in one spot, were the torn wallpaper that had peeled away from the wall: at once he began stuffing everything into this hole, under the paper: "It's in! Everything out of sight and the wallet too!" — he thought happily, standing up and staring blankly at the corner, at the hole that had stuck out even more. Suddenly he shuddered with fear: "My God," he whispered in despair, "what's happening to me? Is this hidden? Do they hide it like this?"

Indeed, he hadn't expected anything but money; he thought there would be only money, and therefore hadn't prepared a place in advance — "but now, now what am I happy about?" — he thought. — "Do they hide it like this?"
