— Where to, if I may ask? — Down the street. — Briefly and clearly. Raskolnikov answered curtly, abruptly, his whole face as white as a handkerchief and not lowering his black inflamed eyes from Ilia Petrovich's gaze. — He barely stands on his feet, and you... — Nikodim Fomich had noticed. — Nothing! — Ilia Petrovich said somehow especially. Nikodim Fomich wanted to add something else, but, glancing at the clerk, who was also very closely looking at him, he fell silent. Everyone suddenly fell silent. It was strange.
