— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, he said to the partially dressed red-haired lady who was standing there as if she didn't dare to sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her. — Ich danke, I thank you, she said and quietly, with a soft rustling sound, sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like an air balloon and occupied almost half of the room. It wafted perfumes. But the lady was clearly nervous about occupying half of the room and that it smelled so strongly of perfume from her, although she smiled timidly and shamelessly together, but with obvious anxiety. The mourning lady finally finished and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, very bravely and somehow especially turning his shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table and sat in the armchair. The plump lady jumped out of her seat at the sight of him and with a special excitement began to squat; but the officer didn't pay any attention to her whatsoever, and she was no longer able to sit down next to him.
