He became extremely scattered and forgetful, knowing it. He had to hurry decisively! He went to the Neva along the B-avenue; but on the way, a sudden thought came to him: "Why to the Neva? Why into the water? Isn't it better to go somewhere very far, again to the Islands, and there, somewhere in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush—bury all of this and the tree, perhaps notice?" Although he felt he was unable to discuss everything clearly and soundly at that moment, the thought seemed infallible to him. But even reaching the Islands was not destined for him, and something else happened: as he was exiting the B-avenue onto the square, he suddenly saw an entrance to the courtyard, surrounded by completely solid walls. To the right, immediately after entering the gates, a solid, unpainted wall of the neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. To the left, parallel to the solid wall and also right next to the gates, a wooden fence extended twenty steps into the courtyard, and then made a turn to the left. This was a secluded, walled-off place where some materials lay. Further, in the depth of the courtyard, a corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
