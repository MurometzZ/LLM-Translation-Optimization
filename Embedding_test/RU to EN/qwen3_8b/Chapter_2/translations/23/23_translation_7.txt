The plump lady jumped up from her seat at the sight of him, and with some peculiar enthusiasm began to sit down again; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared sit down in his presence. He was a sublieutenant, assistant to the quarter master, with reddish mustaches sticking out horizontally on both sides and with extremely small facial features, nothing in particular except for some impudence. He looked at Raskolnikov sideways and partly with indignation: his clothes were too shabby, and despite all his humility, his posture was still too upright. Raskolnikov, by his carelessness, looked at him too straight and for too long, so that the officer even got offended. "What do you want?" he shouted, probably surprised that such a ragged fellow didn't even flinch from his lightning glance. "He was summoned... by order..." answered Raskolnikov somewhat awkwardly. "It's about the case concerning the recovery of money from him, from the student," hastened the clerk, detaching himself from the papers.
