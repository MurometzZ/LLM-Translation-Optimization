And he opened the window and climbed in, like a squealing pig, squealing; that's a disgrace. And how can one squeal like a little pig in the window to the street; that's a disgrace. Ugh-ugh-ugh! And Karl pulled him by the coat from the window, and indeed, Mr. Captain, he broke his rock. And then he shouted that he had to pay fifteen shillings as a fine. And I myself, Mr. Captain, paid five shillings for the fine. And this is an unworthy guest, Mr. Captain, and a troublemaker! I, I say, will compose a big satire about you, because I can write about you everything in all the newspapers. — From writers, you mean? — Yes, Mr. Captain, and what an unworthy guest, Mr. Captain, when in a noble house… — Well, well, well!
