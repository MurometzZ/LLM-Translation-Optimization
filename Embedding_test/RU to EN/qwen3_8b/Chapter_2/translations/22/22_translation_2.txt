— Louise Ivanovna, please sit down, — said he to the hastily dressed crimson lady, who stood still as if not daring to sit herself, although a chair was nearby. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said, and quietly, with a silk-like sound, sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim, like a balloon, spread around the chair and occupied almost half the room. It carried the scent of perfume. But the lady, obviously, felt shy about taking up almost half the room and the fragrance emanating from her, although she smiled nervously and boldly, but with evident anxiety. The widow lady finally finished and began to rise. Suddenly, with some noise, very confidently and somehow particularly turning his shoulders with each step, entered the officer, tossed his hat with a cockade on the table, and sat in the chair. The wealthy lady jumped up from her seat upon seeing him and began to sit down with some special enthusiasm; but the officer paid her not the slightest attention, and she no longer dared to sit down in his presence.
