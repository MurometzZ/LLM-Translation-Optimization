There was a terrible stuffiness because of that. Up and down, janitors with books under their arms went up and down, strutting and various people of both sexes—visitors. The door to the very office was also left open. He entered and stopped in the hallway. Here everyone stood and waited for some men. Here there was also extraordinary stuffiness, and in addition, it was so bad that it made you nauseous—fresh, still wet paint on the old, rancid oil of newly painted rooms. After waiting a bit, he decided to move forward into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low. Terrible impatience dragged him further and further. Nobody noticed him.
