 Well, who are you? — I work as a partner in our office for merchant Shelopaev and I'm here on business. — Please sit down on this chair, — Razumikhin himself sat on another one, on the other side of the table. "You did well, brother, to come to your senses," he continued, turning to Raskolnikov. "You've barely been eating and drinking for four days now. They even fed you tea with a spoonful. I brought Zosimov to see you twice. Do you remember Zosimov? He examined you carefully and immediately said that it was all nonsense — some kind of blow to the head, perhaps. Some nervous nonsense, he says, the rations were bad, they gave too little beer and horseradish, that's why you got sick, but it will pass and everything will work out."
