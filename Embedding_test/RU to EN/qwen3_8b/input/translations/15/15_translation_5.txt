Put on the sock! — it suddenly occurred to him, — it will get even more dusty, and the traces will disappear. But just as he put it on, he immediately pulled it off with disgust and horror. He pulled it off, but realizing there was nothing else, he took it again and put it on — and laughed again. "All of this is conditional, all is relative, all of this are just forms," — he thought briefly, with just a fragment of thought, while trembling all over, — for here he put it on! For he ended up by putting it on!" Laughter, however, immediately turned into despair. "No, it's not within my strength..." — he thought. His legs were trembling. "Out of fear," — he muttered to himself. His head was spinning and aching from the heat.
