He was becoming extremely scattered and forgetful, and he knew it. He had to hurry! He went to the Neva along the B-avenue; but on the way, another thought suddenly came to him: "Why to the Neva? Why into the water? Wasn't it better to go somewhere very far, perhaps again to the Islands, and there, somewhere in a lonely place, in the forest, under a bush—bury all of this and the tree, perhaps even notice it?" Although he felt he couldn't fully discuss everything clearly and rationally at this moment, the thought seemed infallible to him. But even reaching the Islands was not destined for him, and instead, as he was exiting the B-avenue onto the square, he suddenly saw an entrance to a courtyard with completely solid walls. To the right, immediately after entering the gates, a solid, unpainted wall of the neighboring four-story building stretched far into the courtyard. To the left, parallel to the solid wall and also immediately from the gates, a wooden fence ran twenty steps into the courtyard, and then made a turn to the left. This was a secluded, walled-off area where some materials lay. Further, in the depth of the courtyard, a corner of a low, soot-stained, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some workshop.
