 “If they ask, maybe I will say,” he thought, approaching the office. The office was a quarter of a verst away from him. It had just moved to a new address, a new building, on the fourth floor. He had been there briefly once, but a very long time ago at the previous location. Having entered through the gate, he saw a staircase to his right, up which a peasant was going down with a book in his hands: “the janitor, that means; this must be the office,” and he began to go upstairs by guesswork. He didn’t want to ask anyone anything. “I will enter, kneel and tell them everything...” —he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and covered in slops. All the kitchens of all the apartments on all four floors opened onto this staircase and remained that way almost all day. That’s why it was terribly stuffy.
