If they ask me, I might say something, he thought as he approached the office. The office was a quarter of a verst away from him. It had just moved to a new apartment, in a new house, on the fourth floor. He had been there briefly once before, but that was a long time ago. As he entered through the gates, he saw a staircase to his right, down which a man with a book in his hands was walking: "a janitor, so it's here; so this must be the office," and he began to climb up randomly. He didn't want to ask anyone anything about anything. "I'll go in, get on my knees, and tell everything..." he thought as he entered the fourth floor. The staircase was narrow, steep, and full of filth. All the kitchens of all the apartments on all four floors opened onto this staircase and stood there almost all day. That's why it was terribly stuffy.
