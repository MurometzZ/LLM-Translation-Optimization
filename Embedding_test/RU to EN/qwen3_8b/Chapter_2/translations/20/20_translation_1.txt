It was some particularly disheveled person with an unshakable idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from this, because it's all the same to him," Raskolnikov thought. "Go there, to the clerk," the clerk said, pushing him forward with a finger to indicate the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), a cramped and densely packed space filled with people, somewhat better dressed than in those rooms. Among the visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning clothes, poorly dressed, sat at the table opposite the clerk, writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and flushed red with spots, an obvious woman, dressed ostentatiously, with a brooch on her chest as large as a tea saucer, stood aside waiting for something. Raskolnikov handed his summons to the clerk. The clerk glanced at it briefly, said, "Wait," and continued working with the mourning lady. He took a deeper breath.
