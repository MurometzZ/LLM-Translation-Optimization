There was a terrible stuffiness because of that. Up and down, janitors with books under their arms went up and down, strutting and various people of both sexes—visitors. The door to the very office was also left open. He entered and stopped in the hallway. Here, everyone stood and waited for some men. Here, too, there was extraordinary stuffiness, and in addition, the fresh, still wet paint on the old, rancid oil of the newly painted rooms made the nose feel sick. After waiting a bit, he decided to move further forward into the next room. All the rooms were tiny and low. Terrible impatience was pulling him further and further. Nobody noticed him.
