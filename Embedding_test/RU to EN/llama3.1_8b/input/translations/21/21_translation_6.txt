He took a deeper breath and said to himself: "Not that, probably!" He gradually began to calm down, trying with all his might to compose himself and regain his senses. "Some foolishness, some tiny carelessness, and I can be completely caught! Ah... it's a pity there is no air here - the stuffiness... My head spins even more... and my mind too..."

He felt an awful disorder in everything around him. He was afraid of losing control himself. He tried to cling to something and think about anything at all, but that didn't work out either. The clerk greatly interested him: he wanted to guess something from his face, figure it out. This was a very young man, twenty-two years old, with a dark and mobile physiognomy that seemed older than his age, dressed in the latest fashion and foppishly, with a part on the back of his head, combed and pomaded, with numerous rings and cuffs on his white, polished fingers and golden chains on his waistcoat. With one former foreigner here he even spoke two words in French, very satisfactorily. - Louise Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said briefly to a brightly dressed red-haired woman who stood as if not daring to sit down herself, although there was a chair next to her.
