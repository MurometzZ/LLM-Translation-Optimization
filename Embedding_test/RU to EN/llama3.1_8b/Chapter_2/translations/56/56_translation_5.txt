His face expressed amazement. "What's this?" he shouted, examining the entered comrade from head to foot; then fell silent and whistled. "Is it really that bad?" he added, looking at Raskolnikov's tattered clothes. "You've outdone our brother," he said, glancing at him. "Sit down, you must be tired!" And when Raskolnikov collapsed onto the thin Turkish divan, which was even worse than his own, Razumikhin suddenly realized that his guest was ill. "Are you seriously ill?" he asked, knowing it himself. He began to feel his pulse; Raskolnikov pulled away his hand. "No need," he said, "I came... well, I wanted to... but I don't need any lessons at all..." "Do you know what?"
