No evidence!" — and he laughed. Yes, he remembered later that he laughed nervously, small, inaudible, long laugh, and kept laughing all the time, as he passed through the square. But when he stepped onto the Kth boulevard, where he had met that girl three days ago, his laughter suddenly vanished. Other thoughts came into his head. It suddenly occurred to him that it was extremely repulsive for him to pass by that bench, on which he had sat and pondered after the girl left, and extremely difficult to meet again that mustached boy to whom he had given a kopeck: "Damn it!" He walked, looking around listlessly and spitefully. All his thoughts now revolved around one particular main point — and he himself felt that this was indeed such a main point and that now, precisely now, he was left alone with this main point — and that this was even the first time after these two months. "Damn it all!"
