— All these sensitive details, sir, do not concern us at all — Ilya Petrovich snapped brutally — you must give a review and an undertaking, and what you were pleased to be in love with and all these tragic places, we don't care about any of that. — Well, you... are cruel... — muttered Nikodim Fomichev, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He felt somehow ashamed. — Write on, said the clerk to Raskolnikov. — What am I supposed to write? — he asked somewhat especially roughly. — And I'll dictate it to you. Raskolnikov thought that the clerk had become careless and contemptuous towards him after his confession, but strangely enough, he suddenly felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change occurred somehow in one moment, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little bit, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak with them like that just a minute ago, and even impose his feelings on them? And where did these feelings come from?
