— All these sensitive details, my lord, have nothing to do with us, — Ilya Petrovich cut off rudely, — you must give your testimony and make a commitment; as for what you happened to be in love with and all these tragic places, it has nothing to do with us. — Well, you… cruelly… — muttered Nikodim Fomich, sitting down at the table and also beginning to sign. He suddenly felt ashamed. — Write down, — said the scribe to Rasputin. — What to write? — asked he rather rudely. — I shall dictate to you. Rasputin felt that the scribe had become more careless and disrespectful toward him after his confession, but strangely enough, — he suddenly felt completely indifferent to anyone's opinion, and this change occurred in an instant, in one minute. If he had wanted to think a little, he would certainly have been surprised at how he could speak to them a minute ago, and even insist on expressing his feelings? And where did these feelings come from?
