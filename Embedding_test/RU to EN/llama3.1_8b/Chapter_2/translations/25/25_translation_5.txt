"Don't shout! I'm not shouting, I'm speaking quite calmly, and it's you who are shouting at me; and I am a student, so I won't allow anyone to shout at me. The assistant was so flustered that in the first minute he couldn't even speak, and only some spittle flew out of his mouth. He jumped up from his seat. 'Allow yourself to...ahem...laugh! You are present here. Don't...ahem...gurgle, sir!' 'And you're present too,' Raskolnikov shouted back, 'and besides, what's more, you're shouting and smoking a cigar, so we're all equally guilty.' Having said this, Raskolnikov felt an indescribable sense of pleasure. The clerk was smiling at them."
