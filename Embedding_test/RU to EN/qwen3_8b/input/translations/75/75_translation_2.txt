He asked, pointing at the boy. "Oh, you're awake!" she said. "They woke up," replied the artel'nik. Realizing he had woken up, the landlady, who had been peeking through the door, immediately closed them and hid. She had always been shy and endured conversations and explanations with reluctance; she was forty years old, plump and fat, black-browed and black-eyed, kind from her plumpness and laziness; and she was even very smooth in her own way. She was shy beyond necessity. "Who are you?" he continued to interrogate, addressing the artel'nik directly. But at that moment the door opened wide again, and, leaning slightly because he was tall, Razumikhin entered.
