Then he left the room, this time even leaving it completely ajar. He walked quickly and firmly, though he felt entirely broken, but his mind was with him. He feared pursuit, feared that within half an hour, perhaps within a quarter of an hour, someone would come out, probably with instructions to monitor him; therefore, no matter what, he had to dispose of the ends before the time came. He had to get rid of it while there was still some strength left and some reasoning left… Where should he go? This had already been decided long ago: "Throw everything into the ditch, and the ends into the water, and that's that." He had decided this already during the night, in a delirium, during those moments when, as he remembered, he several times tried to get up and go: "Hurry, hurry, and throw everything away." But it turned out to be very difficult to throw it away. He had been wandering along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour, perhaps even more, and several times looked at the wharf of the ditch where they had met him. But it was impossible to carry out the intention: either the rafts were right at the wharf and the washerwomen were washing clothes on them, or the boats were moored, and everywhere people were crowding, and from all sides, from all around the embankment, one could see, notice: it would look suspicious that a person deliberately stepped off, stopped, and threw something into the water. What if the cases didn't sink but floated away?
