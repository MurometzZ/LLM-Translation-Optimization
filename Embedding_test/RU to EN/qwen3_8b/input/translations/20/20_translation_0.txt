It was some particularly disheveled person with an unmoving idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from this, because he doesn't care," thought Raskolnikov. "Go there, to the clerk," said the scribe, and pushed him forward with his finger, indicating the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in order), a cramped and densely packed space filled with people—more refined in appearance than those in the previous rooms. Among the visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning clothes, poorly dressed, sat at the desk facing the clerk, writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and flushed red with spots, an obvious woman, dressed extremely elaborately, with a brooch on her chest as large as a tea saucer, stood aside waiting for something. Raskolnikov handed his summons to the clerk. The clerk glanced at it briefly, said, "Wait," and continued working with the mourning lady. He took a deeper breath.
