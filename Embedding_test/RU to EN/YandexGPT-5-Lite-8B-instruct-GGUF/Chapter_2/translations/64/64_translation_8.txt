 But Raskolnikov was already going out into the street. On Nikolaevsky Bridge he had to come to his senses again due to a very unpleasant incident for him. He was hit hard on the back with a whip by the coachman of one carriage because he almost got run over by the horses, despite the fact that the coachman shouted at him three or four times. The lash so angered him that he, jumping to the railings (for some reason unknown to him, he was walking in the very middle of the bridge where vehicles travel rather than people walk), ground his teeth angrily and snapped them.
