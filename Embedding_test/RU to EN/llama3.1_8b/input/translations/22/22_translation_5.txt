— Louise Ivanovna, would you sit down, he said to the hastily dressed red-haired lady who stood there as if she dared not sit down herself, although a chair was right next to her. — Ich danke,[17 - Thank you (German) — Ed.] — she said and quietly, with a soft rustling sound, sat down on the chair. Her light-blue dress with white lace trim spread out around the chair like an air balloon and occupied almost half the room. It was carried by perfumes. But the lady, obviously nervous about occupying half the room and that it smelled of perfume from her, although she smiled timidly and shamelessly together, but with obvious anxiety. The mourning lady finally finished and began to stand up. Suddenly, with some noise, very gallantly and somehow especially turning his shoulders with each step, an officer entered, threw his cap with a cockade on the table and sat in the armchair. The plump lady jumped right out of her seat when she saw him and with some special excitement began to squat; but the officer did not pay any attention to her at all, and she was no longer able to sit down next to him.
