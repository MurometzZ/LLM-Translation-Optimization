His head spun and throbbed from the heat. "This is a trick! They want to lure me in with a trick and then knock me down all of a sudden," he continued thinking to himself as he stepped onto the stairs. "It's bad that I'm almost delirious... I might say something foolish..."  

On the stairs, he remembered that he always left everything exactly as it was, in the wall cavity—"and here, perhaps deliberately left it out for a search,"—and he remembered and stopped. But such despair and such, if one can say so, cynicism of death suddenly seized him, and he waved his hand and went on. "Only if it's quick...!"  

Again, unbearable heat stood on the street; not a drop of rain for all these days. Again dust, bricks, and lime, again the stench from the benches and taverns, again the constant presence of drunk, shouting peddlers and half-collapsed carriage drivers. The sun shone brightly into his eyes, making it painful to look, and his head spun completely—usual sensation of a feverish person suddenly stepping out into a bright sunny day. Reaching the corner leading to yesterday's street, he looked with a painfully anxious gaze into it, at that house... and immediately looked away. "If they ask, I might say," he thought, approaching the office.
