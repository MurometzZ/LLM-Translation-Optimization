 Further, in a recess of the courtyard, the corner of a low, sooty, stone shed peeked out from behind the fence, obviously part of some kind of workshop. There must have been some kind of establishment here, coach or locksmith, or something like that; everywhere, almost from the very gate, there was a lot of coal dust visible. “That’s where I could throw everything and leave!” — it suddenly occurred to him. Not noticing anyone in the yard, he stepped through the gate and immediately saw, right near the gate, a trough attached to the fence (as is often done in such houses where there are many factory workers, artel members, cab drivers, etc.), and above the trough, right there on the fence, was written in chalk the ever-present joke in such cases: “Stopping a cart is forbidden here.” So much the better, since there would be no suspicion that he had come and stopped. “I could throw everything all at once into a heap somewhere and leave!”

After looking around once more, he already stuck his hand in his pocket when suddenly he noticed a large rough stone near the outer wall, between the gate and the trough, where the entire distance was about an arshin wide (about 70 centimeters), adjacent directly to the stone street wall. Beyond this wall there was a street, a sidewalk, and you could hear passers-by снующих around, of which there were always many; but no one could see him behind the gate, unless someone came from the street, which, however, was very likely to happen, so he had to hurry. He bent down to the stone, grabbed its top firmly with both hands, gathered all his strength and turned the stone over. Under the stone a small depression formed; immediately he began to throw everything from his pocket into it.
