Poverty is not a vice, my friend, but anyway! It's known that he couldn't bear an insult. You probably offended him somehow and didn't hold back yourself, continued Nikodim Fomich, addressing Raskolnikov with courtesy - but it's pointless: the most non-blasphemous person, I'll tell you, but powder, powder! He blew up, boiled over, burned out – and that's it! And everything passed! And as a result, only gold in his heart! They even nicknamed him "lieutenant-powder"...

— And what another regiment was! — exclaimed Ilia Petrovich, very pleased that they had been so pleasantly teased, but still stirring up Raskolnikov. Suddenly, Raskolnikov wanted to say something unusually pleasant to all of them. — Forgive me, Captain, began he quite freely, addressing Nikodim Fomich suddenly - take an interest in my situation... I'm even ready to ask for their apologies if I made any mistakes on my part.
