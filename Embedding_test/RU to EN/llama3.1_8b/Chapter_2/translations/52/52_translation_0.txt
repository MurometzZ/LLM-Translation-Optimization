Yes, he remembered later that he had laughed nervously, quietly, almost inaudibly, and for a long time, laughing all the time as he walked through the square. But when he stepped onto K Boulevard, where three days ago he met that girl, his laughter suddenly stopped. Other thoughts came into his head. It seemed to him too that it was terribly unpleasant now to pass by that bench on which he sat and thought after the girl left, and it would be terribly hard to meet again that old man to whom he had given two grivnas: "Damn you!" He walked, looking around distractedly and sullenly. All his thoughts were now revolving around one main point, and he himself felt that this was indeed a main point and that now, precisely now, he was left alone with it for the first time in these two months.
