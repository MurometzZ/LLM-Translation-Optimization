 She appeared to be about thirty years old for Raskolnikov and was undoubtedly an odd wife for Marmeladov.... She hadn't heard them and didn't notice their arrival. She seemed lost in thought, hearing and seeing nothing. The room was stuffy, but she hadn't opened the window; a stench came up from the stairs, but the door to the staircase wasn't closed. Clouds of tobacco smoke drifted in from the inner rooms, she kept coughing but didn't close the door. The youngest child, a girl of six, was asleep, curled up on the floor with her head resting on the sofa.
