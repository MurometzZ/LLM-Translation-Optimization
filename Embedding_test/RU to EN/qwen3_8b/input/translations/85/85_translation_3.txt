Meanwhile, Razumikhin awkwardly, like a bear, sat down on the couch, encircling Raskolnikov's head with his left hand, despite the fact that he himself could have lifted up, and with his right hand brought a spoon of soup to his mouth, blowing on it several times beforehand to prevent him from burning. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov greedily swallowed one spoon, then another, the third. But after bringing several spoons, Razumikhin suddenly paused and announced that they needed to consult with Zosimov about the further course of action. Nastasya entered, carrying two bottles of beer. — Do you want some tea? — I want. — Go make tea quickly, Nastasya, because, I suppose, it's possible to do without the faculty for tea.
