 Then he left the room, this time even leaving it wide open. He walked quickly and firmly, and although he felt broken, his consciousness was with him. He was afraid of being chased, afraid that in half an hour, in a quarter of an hour, perhaps instructions would already be given to follow him; therefore, at any cost, it was necessary to hide the evidence for the time being. It was necessary to manage before there were no more forces and reasoning left... Where to go? This had been decided long ago: “Throw everything into the ditch, and out of sight, out of mind.” He had resolved this even in the night, in delirium, in those moments when, he remembered, he tried several times to get up and go: “quickly, quickly, and throw it all away.” But throwing it away turned out to be very difficult. He had been wandering along the embankment of the Catherine Canal for half an hour or more, and looked at the accesses to the ditch several times, where he met them. But it was impossible to even think about carrying out the intention: either rafts were standing near the accesses and laundresses were washing clothes on them, or boats were moored, and people swarmed everywhere, and from all sides of the embankments, from all directions, one could see, notice: it is suspicious that a person deliberately went down, stopped and throws something into the water. What if the cases don't sink but float?
