"—Is that how they hide it? My mind is leaving me!" He collapsed onto the couch, and immediately an unbearable chill shook him. Mechanically, he pulled his old student winter coat, warm but already in tatters, from the chair beside him, covered himself with it, and once again sleep and delirium seized him. He fell into a trance. No more than five minutes later, he jumped up again, and immediately, in a frenzy, rushed back to his dress. "How could I have fallen asleep again, with nothing done! That's exactly it, that's exactly it: I still haven't taken off the necktie under my armpit! I forgot, I forgot about such a thing! Such a clue!" He pulled the necktie and quickly began tearing it into pieces, stuffing them under the pillow in the laundry. "Pieces of torn linen will never raise suspicion; it seems so, it seems so!" — he repeated, standing in the middle of the room, and with a strained, painful attention, he began once again to scan around, on the floor and everywhere, to make sure he hadn't forgotten anything else?"
