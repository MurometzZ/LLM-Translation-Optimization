Meanwhile, Razumikhin sat down next to him on the divan, clumsily, like a bear, wrapped his left hand around his head, despite the fact that he himself could have straightened up, and with his right hand brought a spoonful of soup to his mouth, blowing on it several times beforehand so as not to burn himself. But the soup was only just warm. Raskolnikov swallowed one spoonful greedily, then another, third. But after bringing several spoons, Razumikhin suddenly stopped and announced that as for further action, they should consult with Zosima. Nastasya entered, carrying two bottles of beer. — Do you want tea? — I do. — Hurry up and bring the tea, Nastasya, because it seems we can do without a doctor for the tea.
