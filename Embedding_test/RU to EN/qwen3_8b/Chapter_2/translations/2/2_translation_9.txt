The light was enough, and he began to quickly examine himself, from head to toe, his entire dress: were there any traces? But that wasn't possible: shivering from chills, he began to remove everything from himself and examine the surroundings again. He turned everything inside out, down to the last thread and scrap, and, not trusting himself, repeated the inspection three times. But there was nothing, it seemed, no traces; only on the place where the pants were cut off at the bottom and hung with tassels, on the tassels there were thick traces of congealed blood. He grabbed a large folding knife and cut off the tassels. More, it seemed, there was nothing. Suddenly he remembered that the wallet and items he had taken from the old woman's trunk were still lying in his pockets! He hadn't even thought to take them out and hide them yet! He didn't even remember them now, as he examined the dress! What is this?
