The scribe began dictating to him the standard form of a regular withdrawal, that is, I cannot pay, I promise then—when-ever, I will not leave the city, I will neither sell nor give away my property, and so on.—You cannot write, your quill is slipping from your hand," remarked the scribe, looking curiously at Raskolnikov. "Are you ill?"—Yes... my head is spinning... go on, speak!—Yes, everything; sign. The scribe took the paper and began working with the others. Raskolnikov handed over the quill, but instead of rising and leaving, he placed both elbows on the table and clutched his head with his hands. It felt as though a nail was being driven into his forehead. A strange thought suddenly came to him: to rise now, approach Nikodim Fomich, and tell him everything from yesterday, every last detail, then go together with them to the apartment and show them the items in the corner, in the hole. The impulse was so strong that he had already risen from his seat, ready to carry it out.
