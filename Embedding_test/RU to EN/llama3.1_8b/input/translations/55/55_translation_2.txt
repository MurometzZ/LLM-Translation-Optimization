He would have just spat on someone and bitten them if anyone had spoken to him...

He stopped suddenly when he came out onto the embankment of the Little Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, near a bridge. "This is where he lives in this house," he thought. "But how did I come here? The same old story as before... But it's very interesting: was I coming to see him or just happened to walk by? Doesn't matter; I said I'd go the next day and now I'll go! As if I couldn't go in now..."

He went up to Razumikhin on the fifth floor. He was at home, in his own little room, and was writing at that moment, and he himself let him in. They hadn't seen each other for four months. Razumikhin sat in a tattered dressing gown, in slippers with one foot bare, disheveled, unshaven, and unwashed. A look of surprise appeared on his face.
