A small depression formed under the stone; immediately, he began throwing everything from his pockets into it. The wallet ended up at the top, yet there was still room left. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one twist, and it fell back into its original place, slightly seeming higher. But he scraped the soil and pressed down the edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he went out and headed to the square. Again, a strong, barely bearable joy, like the time in the office, seized him for a moment. "The ends are hidden! And who would ever think to look under this stone? It has been lying here since the house was built and will remain so for just as long."
