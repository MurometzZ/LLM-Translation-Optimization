This was some particularly disheveled person with a fixed idea in his gaze. "You won't learn anything from this, because he doesn't care," thought Raskolnikov. "Go there, to the clerk," said the clerk, jerking his finger forward to indicate the very last room. He entered this room (the fourth in sequence), a cramped and densely packed space filled with people, somewhat better dressed than in those rooms. Among the visitors were two ladies. One, in mourning attire, poorly dressed, sat at the table opposite the clerk, writing something under his dictation. The other lady, very plump and ruddy, with spots, a noticeable woman, dressed extremely flamboyantly, with a brooch on her chest as large as a tea saucer, stood aside waiting for something. Raskolnikov handed his summons to the clerk. The clerk glanced at it briefly, said, "Wait," and continued working with the mourning lady. He breathed more freely.
