A small depression formed under the stone; he immediately began throwing everything from his pocket into it. The wallet landed on top and yet there was still room in the depression. Then he grabbed the stone again, turned it over with one rotation to its original side, and it just fit back into its original place, except that it seemed a bit higher. He pushed some earth under it and pressed down on the edges with his foot. Nothing was noticeable. Then he left and headed towards the square. Again, strong, barely bearable joy, as before in the office, took hold of him for an instant. "The ends are buried! And who could think to look for them under this stone? It may be lying here since the house was built and will lie here just as long."
