The clerk began to dictate to him the usual form of such a letter, that is, I cannot pay, I promise to do so sometime, I will not leave the city, I will not sell or give away my property and so on. — You can't even write, your pen slips out of your hands, — observed the clerk, looking at Raskolnikov with curiosity. — Are you ill? — Yes... My head is spinning... Go on talking! — Yes, everything; sign it. The clerk took back the paper and busied himself with others. Raskolnikov gave him his pen, but instead of standing up and leaving, he placed both elbows on the table and clutched his head in his hands. They were hammering a nail into his skull. A strange idea suddenly came to him: get up now, go over to Nikodim Fyodorovich and tell him everything that happened yesterday, every detail, then go with them to the apartment and point out the things, in the corner, in the hole. The urge was so strong that he had already stood up from his seat to carry it out.
