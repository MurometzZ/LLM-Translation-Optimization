Thus he lay there for a very long time. It happened that he seemed to wake up, and during these moments he noticed that it was already night, but the thought of getting up never came to him. Finally, he noticed that it was already daylight. He lay on the couch on his back, still stunned from recent sleep. The terrible, desperate cries from the street reached him sharply, which he had listened to every night under his window at three o’clock. It was these cries that woke him up now. “Ah! Here come the drunkards from the taverns,” he thought, “it’s three o’clock— and suddenly he jumped up as if someone had pulled him off the couch. ‘What! It’s already three o’clock!’” He sat on the couch—and then everything came back to him!
