 She appeared to be about thirty years old for Raskolnikov and was undoubtedly an unusual wife for Marmeladov.... She hadn't heard them and didn't notice their arrival. She seemed to be lost in thought, hearing and seeing nothing. The room was stuffy, but she had not opened the window; a stench emanated from the staircase, but the door leading to it was not closed. Clouds of tobacco smoke drifted in from the inner rooms, she kept coughing, but did not close the door. The youngest child, a girl of six, was sleeping, curled up on the floor with her head resting on the sofa.
