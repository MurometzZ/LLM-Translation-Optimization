Quite the opposite!...

Suddenly he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question immediately threw him off and greatly surprised him:

"If indeed all this was done deliberately, not foolishly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how come you haven't even looked into your wallet yet and don't know what you've received, for which you've endured all these sufferings, and for which you deliberately went on such a low, nasty, and despicable course? You even wanted to throw it all into the water now, the wallet along with all the things you haven't even seen yet... How is that possible?"

Yes, that's exactly it; everything is just like that. He had known this all along, and it was not a new question for him at all; and when it was decided at night to throw everything into the water, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as if that was the only way it could be, as if there was no other possibility... Yes, he knew everything and remembered everything; perhaps it was decided just yesterday, at the very moment when he was sitting by the chest and taking out the cases from it... And that's exactly what happened!... 'It's because I'm very sick,' he decided gloomily at last, 'I've tortured and torn myself apart, and I don't even know what I'm doing... And yesterday, and three days ago, and all this time I've been tormenting myself... I'll recover and... stop tormenting myself... What if I never recover at all? Lord! How tired I am of all this!...' He kept walking without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow relieve himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to take action. One new, insurmountable feeling was taking over him more and more, almost with every minute: it was an infinite, almost physical revulsion towards everything encountered and surrounding, persistent, spiteful, hateful. Everything he met was repulsive to him—his face, his gait, his movements. He just wanted to spit on someone, bite someone, perhaps, if anyone spoke to him...

He suddenly stopped when he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on the Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge.
