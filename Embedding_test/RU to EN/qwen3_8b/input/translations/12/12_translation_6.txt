He did not answer and held the paper in his hands without unsealing it. "Don't get up," Nastasya continued, piteously, seeing that he was taking his legs off the couch. "He's ill, so don't walk: it won't burn." What's in your hands? He looked: in his right hand, he had cut-off pieces of fringe, a sock, and scraps from a torn pocket. So he slept with them. Later, reflecting on it, he remembered that, half-awake in the heat, he tightly clutched all of this in his hand and thus fell asleep again. "What a mess he's gathered and sleeps with them, as if with a treasure..." And Nastasya laughed with her painful-nervous laugh. He quickly put everything under the overcoat and stared at it intently with his eyes. Although he could hardly fully understand at that moment, he felt that they would not treat the person so when they came to take him.
