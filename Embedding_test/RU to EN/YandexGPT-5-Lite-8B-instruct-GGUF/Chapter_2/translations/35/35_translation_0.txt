 — Poverty is not a vice, my friend, well, what of it! It's known that the powder could not bear an insult. You must have been offended by him in some way and couldn't restrain yourself, — Nikodim Fomich continued, addressing Raskolnikov любезно, — but you were wrong to do so: I tell you, he is a most noble person, but powder, powder! He flared up, boiled over, burned out — and that's it! And everything passed! And the result is only the gold of his heart! They even called him "Lieutenant Powder" in the regiment...
— And what a regiment there was! — Ilya Petrovich exclaimed, quite pleased to be tickled so pleasantly, but still playful. Raskolnikov suddenly wanted to say something unusually pleasant to them all. — But please, captain, — he began very развязно, turning suddenly to Nikodim Fomich, — consider my situation... I'm even ready to ask them for forgiveness if I neglected anything on my part.
