Just spit on someone, bite them, I guess, if someone talked to him...  
He stopped suddenly as he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on Vasilyevsky Island, by the bridge. "Here he lives, in this house," he thought. "What's this, I came here myself to Razumikhin! The same story again, as before... But it's really interesting: did I come myself or just walked here by accident? Anyway; I said three days ago... that I would go to him the next day, well, I'll go! As if I can't even visit now..."  
He went up to Razumikhin's fifth floor. The latter was at home, in his room, writing, and opened the door himself. They hadn't seen each other for four months. Razum, in a tattered robe to rags, socks on bare feet, disheveled, unshaven, and dirty, had a surprised expression on his face.
