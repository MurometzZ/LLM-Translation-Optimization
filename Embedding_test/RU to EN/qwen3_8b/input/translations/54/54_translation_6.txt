Not at all!...

Suddenly he stopped; a new, completely unexpected and extremely simple question immediately confused him and greatly surprised him:

"If indeed all this was done intentionally, not foolishly, if you really had a definite and firm goal, then how come you haven't even looked into the wallet yet and don't know what you've received, for which you've endured all these torments and deliberately went through such a mean, nasty, lowly deed? Indeed, you wanted to throw it into the water now, the wallet, along with all the things you haven't even seen yet… What on earth?"

Yes, that's right; everything is like that. He, however, had known this before, and it was by no means a new question for him; and when it was decided at night to throw them into the water, it was decided without any hesitation or objection, as though it was only natural, as though otherwise it was impossible… Yes, he knew everything and remembered everything; indeed, perhaps it was already decided yesterday, at the very moment when he was sitting over the chest and was taking out the cases from it… But that's exactly it!... "It's because I'm very ill," he finally decided gloomily, "I've tortured and torn myself apart, and I don't even know what I'm doing…" He kept walking without stopping. He desperately wanted to somehow relieve himself, but he didn't know what to do or what to take action. One new, insurmountable feeling was taking over him more and more, almost with every minute: it was some kind of infinite, almost physical revulsion toward everything he encountered and surrounded him, persistent, spiteful, hateful. He found everyone he met repulsive—repulsive were their faces, their gait, their movements. He simply wanted to spit on someone, to bite someone, perhaps, if someone spoke to him…

He suddenly stopped when he reached the embankment of the Small Neva, on the Vasilyevsky Island, near the bridge.
